Understanding Migraines and Gluten Sensitivity

Migraines are a type of headache characterized by severe pain, often accompanied by nausea, vomiting, and sensitivity to light and sound. While the exact cause of migraines is not fully understood, several triggers have been identified, including stress, hormonal changes, and certain foods. Gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, has been implicated as a potential trigger for migraines in individuals with gluten sensitivity or celiac disease.

Gluten sensitivity is a condition in which the body reacts negatively to gluten, leading to a range of symptoms, including digestive issues, fatigue, and headaches. Celiac disease, on the other hand, is an autoimmune disorder in which the ingestion of gluten leads to damage in the small intestine. Both conditions can result in migraines, making it crucial for affected individuals to avoid gluten-containing foods.

While Annie's Gluten-Free Mac n Cheese can be a good option for migraine sufferers, there are a few considerations to keep in mind.
