How to Buy a Skateboard: A Comprehensive Guide

Skateboarding is more than just a sport; it's a lifestyle. Whether you're a beginner looking to learn the basics or an experienced skater wanting to upgrade your gear, buying the right skateboard is crucial. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to make an informed purchase.

1. Determine Your Skateboarding Style

Before you buy a skateboard, it's important to understand what type of skateboarding you'll be doing. Different styles require different types of boards:

  • Street Skateboarding: Performed on streets, skate parks, and urban environments. Requires a durable, lightweight board.
  • Vert Skateboarding: Done on ramps and half-pipes. Requires a wider, more stable board.
  • Cruising: For casual riding around town. Requires a longboard or cruiser board.

2. Understand the Parts of a Skateboard

A skateboard is made up of several key components. Knowing what each part does will help you make a better choice:

Part Description
Deck The flat board you stand on. Typically made of wood, but can also be made of composite materials.
Trucks The metal T-shaped pieces that attach the wheels to the deck. They allow you to turn.
Wheels Made of urethane, wheels come in different sizes and hardness levels depending on your style of skating.
Bearings Small metal rings that fit inside the wheels and allow them to spin smoothly.
Grip Tape A sandpaper-like material applied to the top of the deck to provide traction.

3. Choose the Right Deck Size

The size of your skateboard deck is crucial for comfort and performance. Deck width typically ranges from 7.5 to 8.5 inches:

  • 7.5 - 8.0 inches: Ideal for street skateboarding and technical tricks.
  • 8.0 - 8.5 inches: Best for vert skateboarding and cruising.

Your shoe size and personal preference will also play a role in choosing the right deck size.

4. Consider Your Budget

Skateboards can range from $50 to $200 or more, depending on the quality and brand. While it might be tempting to go for the cheapest option, investing in a higher-quality board will provide better performance and durability.

5. Where to Buy

You can purchase skateboards from local skate shops, online retailers, or even second-hand. Local shops often have knowledgeable staff who can help you make the right choice, while online retailers may offer a wider selection and better prices.

FAQs

Q: How often should I replace my skateboard?

A: It depends on how often you skate and the conditions you skate in. On average, a deck may last 3-6 months, while wheels and bearings can last longer with proper maintenance.

Q: Can I build my own skateboard?

A: Absolutely! Many skaters prefer to build their own boards to customize every component. You can buy individual parts and assemble them yourself.

Conclusion

Buying a skateboard is an exciting step in your skateboarding journey. By understanding your skating style, the components of a skateboard, and your budget, you can make an informed decision that will enhance your experience. Whether you're hitting the streets, the skate park, or just cruising around town, the right skateboard will make all the difference. Happy skating!
