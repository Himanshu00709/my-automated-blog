Understanding Gluten and Cross-Contamination

Gluten is a protein that can cause adverse reactions in individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. Even trace amounts of gluten can trigger symptoms, making it crucial to ensure that products are not only free from gluten-containing ingredients but also free from cross-contamination during manufacturing.

Cross-contamination can occur when gluten-free products are processed in facilities that also handle gluten-containing products. This can happen through shared equipment, storage areas, or even during packaging. Therefore, it is essential to look for products that are certified gluten-free, as they have undergone rigorous testing to ensure they meet strict gluten-free standards.

Is Cavender's Certified Gluten-Free?

While Cavender's Greek Seasoning does not contain gluten-containing ingredients, it is not certified gluten-free. This means that the product has not been tested by a third-party organization to confirm that it meets the gluten-free standards set by regulatory bodies. However, the manufacturer states that the product is made in a facility that does not process gluten-containing ingredients, reducing the risk of cross-contamination.

For individuals with severe gluten sensitivities or celiac disease, it is advisable to contact the manufacturer directly to inquire about their manufacturing processes and any potential risks of cross-contamination. Additionally, always read the product label for any updates or changes in ingredients or manufacturing practices.
