Is Lume Gluten-Free? A Comprehensive Guide

In recent years, the demand for gluten-free products has skyrocketed, driven by the increasing awareness of celiac disease, gluten sensitivity, and the general health-consciousness of consumers. Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease, consuming gluten can trigger an immune response that damages the small intestine, leading to a host of health issues. Even for those with non-celiac gluten sensitivity, gluten can cause symptoms like bloating, diarrhea, and fatigue. As a result, many people are vigilant about avoiding gluten not just in their diet but also in their personal care products.

Personal care products, including deodorants, can sometimes contain gluten-derived ingredients. While the risk of gluten absorption through the skin is low, those with severe gluten sensitivity or celiac disease may prefer to avoid any product that contains gluten to prevent accidental ingestion or cross-contamination.

Lume is also free from many common irritants found in conventional deodorants, such as aluminum, baking soda, and artificial fragrances. This makes it a popular choice for individuals with sensitive skin or those looking for a more natural alternative to traditional deodorants.
