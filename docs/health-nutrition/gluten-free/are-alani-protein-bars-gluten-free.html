Understanding Gluten and Its Impact on Health

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For most people, gluten is harmless, but for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of health issues, including digestive problems, fatigue, and even long-term damage to the small intestine. As a result, many individuals are turning to gluten-free diets to manage their health and well-being.

When it comes to protein bars, the presence of gluten can be a concern, especially for those who are highly sensitive. Cross-contamination during manufacturing is another issue that can affect the gluten-free status of a product. Therefore, it’s essential to examine not only the ingredients but also the production processes of the protein bars in question.

FAQs About Alani Protein Bars and Gluten

1. Are Alani protein bars safe for people with celiac disease?

While Alani protein bars do not contain gluten-containing ingredients, they are not certified gluten-free. Individuals with celiac disease should exercise caution and consult with their healthcare provider before consuming these bars.

No, Alani protein bars do not contain wheat, barley, or rye. The primary ingredients are gluten-free, but cross-contamination is always a possibility in facilities that process gluten-containing products.

3. Can I trust Alani protein bars if I have a gluten sensitivity?
