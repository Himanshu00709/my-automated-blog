Understanding Gluten and Its Implications

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to more severe health complications. As a result, many people are turning to gluten-free diets to manage their health. But what does it mean for a product to be gluten-free?

According to the FDA, a product can be labeled as gluten-free if it contains less than 20 parts per million (ppm) of gluten. This threshold is considered safe for most people with celiac disease. However, it’s essential to note that not all products labeled as gluten-free are created equal. Some may still contain trace amounts of gluten, which can be problematic for highly sensitive individuals.
