Understanding Gluten and Gluten-Free Diets

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease, consuming gluten triggers an immune response that damages the small intestine. Even for those with non-celiac gluten sensitivity, gluten can cause discomfort and digestive issues. As a result, many people opt for gluten-free diets to avoid these adverse effects.

Gluten-free diets require careful scrutiny of food labels and ingredients. Cross-contamination during manufacturing is also a concern, as even trace amounts of gluten can be harmful to those with celiac disease. Therefore, it's essential to determine whether Smart Food products meet these stringent gluten-free standards.

How to Identify Gluten-Free Smart Food Products

Identifying gluten-free Smart Food products is relatively straightforward if you know what to look for. Here are some tips:

  • Check the Label: Look for the "gluten-free" label on the packaging. This indicates that the product meets the FDA's gluten-free standards, containing less than 20 parts per million (ppm) of gluten.
  • Read the Ingredients: Review the ingredient list for any gluten-containing ingredients such as wheat, barley, rye, or malt.
  • Look for Certification: Some products may carry third-party gluten-free certification, providing an extra layer of assurance.
