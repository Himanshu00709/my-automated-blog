What Does Gluten-Free Mean?

Before we delve into the specifics of Two Good Smoothies, it’s essential to understand what "gluten-free" means. Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and their derivatives. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to severe health issues, including digestive problems, nutrient malabsorption, and even long-term damage to the small intestine.

A product is considered gluten-free if it contains less than 20 parts per million (ppm) of gluten, as per the FDA’s guidelines. This threshold is generally safe for most people with gluten-related disorders.

Based on this ingredient list, Two Good Smoothies appear to be free from gluten-containing ingredients. However, it’s always a good practice to check the label for any potential cross-contamination warnings.

Manufacturing Process and Cross-Contamination

Even if a product’s ingredients are gluten-free, cross-contamination during manufacturing can pose a risk. Cross-contamination occurs when gluten-free products come into contact with gluten-containing products or surfaces during production, packaging, or transportation.

Two Good Smoothies are produced by a reputable company that adheres to strict quality control measures. The brand has stated that their smoothies are manufactured in facilities that also process other products, some of which may contain gluten. However, they follow rigorous cleaning protocols to minimize the risk of cross-contamination.

For individuals with severe gluten sensitivity or celiac disease, it’s crucial to look for products that are certified gluten-free by a recognized organization. Unfortunately, Two Good Smoothies do not currently carry a gluten-free certification.
