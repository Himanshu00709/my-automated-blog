What is Gluta?

Gluta, or glutathione, is a naturally occurring antioxidant found in the human body. It plays a crucial role in detoxification, immune function, and the maintenance of cellular health. Gluta is composed of three amino acids: cysteine, glutamine, and glycine. Its primary function is to neutralize free radicals, which are unstable molecules that can damage cells and contribute to aging and diseases.

In recent years, Gluta has gained attention for its skin-whitening properties. It inhibits the production of melanin, the pigment responsible for skin color, leading to a brighter and more even complexion. This has made Gluta a popular ingredient in skincare products and supplements, particularly in Japan, where beauty standards prioritize fair and flawless skin.
