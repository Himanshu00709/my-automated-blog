Why Gluten-Free Pizza?

Gluten-free pizza has become increasingly popular as more people are diagnosed with celiac disease or choose to avoid gluten for health reasons. Gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, can cause severe digestive issues and other health problems for those with gluten intolerance. Fortunately, many pizzerias in Seattle have risen to the occasion, offering gluten-free crusts made from alternative flours like rice, almond, or cauliflower. These crusts are not only safe for gluten-sensitive individuals but also delicious and often packed with nutrients.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Pizza in Seattle

1. Is gluten-free pizza safe for people with celiac disease?

Yes, gluten-free pizza is safe for people with celiac disease, provided that the pizzeria takes proper precautions to avoid cross-contamination. Restaurants like Razzi's Pizzeria, which have a 100% gluten-free kitchen, are the safest options for those with celiac disease.

2. What are gluten-free pizza crusts made of?

Gluten-free pizza crusts are typically made from alternative flours such as rice flour, almond flour, or cauliflower. These flours provide a similar texture to traditional wheat crusts without the gluten.

3. Can I find gluten-free pizza at chain pizzerias in Seattle?

Yes, many chain pizzerias in Seattle, such as Zeeks Pizza and Pagliacci Pizza, offer gluten-free crust options. However, it's important to inquire about their cross-contamination practices to ensure the pizza is safe for those with celiac disease.

4. Are gluten-free pizzas more expensive?

Gluten-free pizzas can be slightly more expensive due to the cost of alternative ingredients and the additional precautions taken to avoid cross-contamination. However, the price difference is usually minimal and worth it for those who need a gluten-free diet.
