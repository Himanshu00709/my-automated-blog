Understanding Gluten in Sushi

Before diving into the gluten-free offerings at Blue Ribbon Sushi, it’s important to understand where gluten can hide in traditional sushi. Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, and it can be present in soy sauce, tempura batter, and certain types of vinegar used in sushi rice. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, even trace amounts of gluten can cause adverse reactions. Therefore, it’s crucial for restaurants to take extra precautions when preparing gluten-free dishes.

Precautions Taken by Blue Ribbon Sushi

Blue Ribbon Sushi takes the safety of their gluten-free diners seriously. The restaurant has implemented several measures to prevent cross-contamination and ensure that gluten-free dishes remain safe for consumption. These precautions include:

  • Using separate utensils and cutting boards for gluten-free preparations.
  • Training staff on the importance of gluten-free practices and cross-contamination prevention.
  • Offering gluten-free soy sauce as a standard option for gluten-free dishes.
  • Clearly labeling gluten-free items on the menu to help diners make informed choices.
