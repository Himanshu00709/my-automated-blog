What is Gluten-Free at Arby's? A Comprehensive Guide

For individuals with celiac disease, gluten sensitivity, or those simply choosing to avoid gluten, dining out can be a challenge. Arby's, known for its roast beef sandwiches and curly fries, may not seem like the most gluten-free-friendly fast-food option at first glance. However, with a little knowledge and careful selection, you can still enjoy a meal at Arby's without compromising your dietary needs. In this guide, we'll explore the gluten-free options available at Arby's, provide tips for safe dining, and answer some frequently asked questions.

Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For those with celiac disease, consuming gluten can lead to severe digestive issues, nutrient malabsorption, and long-term health complications. Even for those with non-celiac gluten sensitivity, gluten can cause discomfort and other symptoms. Therefore, it's crucial to identify safe food options when dining out.

Gluten-Free Options at Arby's

Arby's menu is primarily centered around sandwiches and breaded items, which are typically high in gluten. However, there are still some gluten-free options available if you know where to look. Below is a breakdown of gluten-free items you can enjoy at Arby's:

1. Meats

Arby's is famous for its roast beef, and the good news is that their roast beef is naturally gluten-free. Other gluten-free meat options include:

  • Roast Beef
  • Turkey Breast
  • Corned Beef
  • Ham

These meats are free from gluten-containing ingredients, but cross-contamination can occur during preparation. Always inform the staff of your dietary restrictions to minimize the risk.

2. Salads

Arby's offers a few salad options that can be made gluten-free by omitting certain ingredients like croutons or dressings that contain gluten. The following salads can be customized to fit a gluten-free diet:

  • Roast Chicken Salad
  • Farmhouse Salad

Be sure to ask for gluten-free dressing options, such as the Light Italian Dressing or the Buttermilk Ranch Dressing.

3. Sides

While many of Arby's sides are breaded or fried, there are a few gluten-free options:

  • Side Salad (without croutons)
  • Apple Slices
  • Potato Cakes (check for cross-contamination)

Note that the curly fries and mozzarella sticks are not gluten-free due to the breading and frying process.

4. Beverages

Most of Arby's beverages, including soft drinks, iced tea, and coffee, are gluten-free. However, always check the ingredients of specialty drinks or shakes, as some may contain gluten or be subject to cross-contamination.

Tips for Dining Gluten-Free at Arby's

When dining at Arby's, it's essential to take extra precautions to ensure your meal is safe. Here are some tips to help you navigate the menu:

  • Communicate with Staff: Inform the staff about your gluten intolerance or celiac disease. They can guide you on safe options and help minimize cross-contamination.
  • Check Ingredients: Always ask for the ingredient list or allergen menu to verify that your chosen items are gluten-free.
  • Avoid Fried Foods: Fried items are often cooked in shared fryers, which can lead to cross-contamination. Stick to grilled or baked options.
  • Customize Your Order: Don't hesitate to ask for modifications, such as removing croutons from salads or opting for gluten-free dressings.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Options at Arby's

1. Are Arby's curly fries gluten-free?

No, Arby's curly fries are not gluten-free. They are coated in a batter that contains wheat and are fried in shared fryers, which increases the risk of cross-contamination.

2. Can I get a gluten-free bun at Arby's?

As of now, Arby's does not offer gluten-free buns. However, you can enjoy their gluten-free meats in a salad or as a lettuce wrap.

3. Is Arby's roast beef gluten-free?

Yes, Arby's roast beef is naturally gluten-free. However, cross-contamination can occur during preparation, so it's essential to inform the staff of your dietary needs.

4. Are Arby's shakes gluten-free?

Most of Arby's shakes are gluten-free, but it's always best to check the ingredients and ask the staff to ensure there's no risk of cross-contamination.

Conclusion

While Arby's may not be the most gluten-free-friendly fast-food chain, there are still several options available for those avoiding gluten. By focusing on naturally gluten-free items like roast beef, turkey, and salads, and taking precautions to avoid cross-contamination, you can enjoy a satisfying meal at Arby's. Always communicate your dietary needs to the staff and double-check ingredients to ensure a safe dining experience. With a little planning, you can navigate the menu and find delicious gluten-free options that suit your lifestyle.
