Best Gluten-Free Afternoon Tea in London

Afternoon tea is a quintessentially British tradition, offering a delightful combination of savory sandwiches, sweet pastries, and a steaming pot of tea. For those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease, finding a gluten-free afternoon tea can be a challenge. Fortunately, London is home to a variety of establishments that cater to gluten-free diets without compromising on taste or elegance. In this article, we’ll explore some of the best gluten-free afternoon tea experiences in London, ensuring you can indulge in this timeless tradition with peace of mind.

1. The Ritz London

The Ritz London is synonymous with luxury and tradition, and their gluten-free afternoon tea is no exception. Set in the opulent Palm Court, the experience is nothing short of magical. The Ritz offers a dedicated gluten-free menu that includes a selection of finger sandwiches, freshly baked scones, and an array of pastries. Each item is carefully prepared to ensure it meets the highest standards of gluten-free dining.

2. The Savoy

Another iconic London hotel, The Savoy, offers a gluten-free afternoon tea that is both elegant and delicious. The Thames Foyer provides a stunning setting for this experience, with a glass dome allowing natural light to flood the room. The gluten-free menu features a variety of sandwiches, scones, and desserts, all crafted with the same attention to detail as their traditional offerings. The Savoy’s commitment to quality ensures that gluten-free guests can enjoy a truly memorable afternoon tea.

3. Fortnum & Mason

Fortnum & Mason, the famous department store, is renowned for its high-quality food and drink offerings. Their Diamond Jubilee Tea Salon offers a gluten-free afternoon tea that is both luxurious and satisfying. The menu includes a selection of gluten-free sandwiches, scones, and pastries, all made with the finest ingredients. The tea salon’s elegant decor and attentive service make it a must-visit for anyone seeking a gluten-free afternoon tea in London.

4. The Langham, London

The Langham, London, is another top destination for gluten-free afternoon tea. Their Palm Court lounge is the perfect setting for this indulgent experience, with its elegant decor and soothing atmosphere. The gluten-free menu includes a variety of sandwiches, scones, and desserts, all prepared with care to ensure they are safe for those with gluten sensitivities. The Langham’s commitment to excellence ensures that every guest enjoys a truly special afternoon tea.

5. Sketch

For a more contemporary take on afternoon tea, Sketch offers a unique and artistic experience. Located in Mayfair, Sketch is known for its quirky decor and innovative cuisine. Their gluten-free afternoon tea includes a selection of savory and sweet treats, all beautifully presented and bursting with flavor. The Gallery at Sketch, with its pink velvet chairs and playful artwork, provides a one-of-a-kind setting for this modern twist on a classic tradition.

FAQs

1. Is gluten-free afternoon tea safe for people with celiac disease?

Yes, most of the venues listed above take great care to ensure their gluten-free offerings are safe for those with celiac disease. However, it’s always a good idea to inform the staff of your dietary requirements when making a reservation.

2. Can I make a reservation for gluten-free afternoon tea?

Absolutely. It’s recommended to make a reservation in advance, especially at popular venues like The Ritz and The Savoy. Be sure to mention your gluten-free requirements when booking.

3. Are there vegan options available at these venues?

Some venues, such as Sketch, offer vegan options alongside their gluten-free menu. It’s best to check with the venue directly to confirm their offerings.

4. How much does gluten-free afternoon tea typically cost?

The price of gluten-free afternoon tea in London typically ranges from £60 to £95 per person, depending on the venue and the level of luxury.

5. Can I bring my own gluten-free items?

Most venues do not allow outside food, as they take pride in offering a complete and safe dining experience. However, it’s always best to check with the venue beforehand.

Conclusion

London offers a wealth of options for those seeking a gluten-free afternoon tea experience. From the timeless elegance of The Ritz and The Savoy to the contemporary flair of Sketch, there’s something to suit every taste and preference. Each venue takes great care to ensure their gluten-free offerings are both safe and delicious, allowing you to enjoy this cherished tradition without worry. Whether you’re a local or a visitor, indulging in a gluten-free afternoon tea in London is an experience not to be missed.
