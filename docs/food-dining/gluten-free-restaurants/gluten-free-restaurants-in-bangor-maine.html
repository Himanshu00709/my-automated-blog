What to Look for in a Gluten-Free Restaurant

When dining out gluten-free, it’s important to ensure that the restaurant takes the necessary precautions to avoid cross-contamination. Here are some key factors to consider:

  • Dedicated Gluten-Free Menu: A separate menu indicates that the restaurant is knowledgeable about gluten-free needs.
  • Cross-Contamination Prevention: Ask if the restaurant uses separate cooking utensils, fryers, and preparation areas for gluten-free dishes.
  • Staff Training: Well-trained staff can provide accurate information about ingredients and preparation methods.
  • Customer Reviews: Check online reviews to see what other gluten-free diners have experienced at the restaurant.

Gluten-Free Dining Tips in Bangor

Dining out gluten-free in Bangor can be a delightful experience if you follow these tips:

  1. Call Ahead: Contact the restaurant in advance to discuss your dietary needs and ensure they can accommodate you.
  2. Ask Questions: Don’t hesitate to ask the server or chef about ingredients and preparation methods.
  3. Bring Your Own Snacks: If you’re unsure about the options, bring a gluten-free snack just in case.
  4. Use Gluten-Free Apps: Apps like “Find Me Gluten Free” can help you locate gluten-free restaurants and read reviews from other diners.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Dining in Bangor

1. Are there any 100% gluten-free restaurants in Bangor?

While there are no 100% gluten-free restaurants in Bangor, many establishments offer extensive gluten-free menus and take precautions to prevent cross-contamination.

2. Can I find gluten-free beer in Bangor?

Yes, Mason’s Brewing Company offers gluten-free beer options, making it a great spot for those who want to enjoy a cold brew without the gluten.

3. Are gluten-free options clearly labeled on menus?

Most restaurants in Bangor that cater to gluten-free diets clearly label their gluten-free options. However, it’s always a good idea to confirm with the staff.

4. What should I do if I accidentally consume gluten?

If you accidentally consume gluten, it’s important to stay hydrated and rest. Over-the-counter medications may help alleviate symptoms, but consult your doctor if symptoms persist.
