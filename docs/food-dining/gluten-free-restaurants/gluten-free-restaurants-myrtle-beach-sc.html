Tips for Dining Gluten-Free in Myrtle Beach

When dining out gluten-free, it’s important to take precautions to ensure your meal is safe. Here are some tips to help you navigate the gluten-free dining scene in Myrtle Beach:

  • Research Ahead of Time: Before heading out, check online reviews and restaurant websites to see if they offer gluten-free options. Many restaurants now have gluten-free menus available online.
  • Communicate with Staff: When you arrive at the restaurant, inform your server about your gluten intolerance or celiac disease. Ask questions about how the food is prepared and if there’s a risk of cross-contamination.
  • Look for Certified Gluten-Free Restaurants: Some restaurants are certified gluten-free, meaning they adhere to strict guidelines to prevent cross-contamination. These are often the safest options for those with celiac disease.
  • Be Cautious with Fried Foods: Even if a dish is naturally gluten-free, it may be fried in the same oil as gluten-containing foods. Always ask if the restaurant uses a dedicated fryer for gluten-free items.
  • Bring Your Own Gluten-Free Snacks: If you’re unsure about the options available, it’s always a good idea to bring your own gluten-free snacks just in case.
