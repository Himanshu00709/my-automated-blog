Why Gluten-Free Dining Matters

For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to severe health issues. Gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, can cause inflammation in the small intestine, leading to symptoms like bloating, diarrhea, and fatigue. Even for those without a diagnosed condition, many people choose a gluten-free diet for its potential health benefits, such as improved digestion and increased energy levels. Myrtle Beach’s restaurants have recognized this growing demand and have adapted their menus to accommodate gluten-free diners.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Dining in Myrtle Beach

1. Are gluten-free options clearly labeled on menus?

Yes, many restaurants in Myrtle Beach clearly label their gluten-free options on the menu. However, it’s always a good idea to inform your server about your dietary restrictions to ensure your meal is prepared safely.

2. How do I know if a restaurant is safe for gluten-free dining?

Look for restaurants that have a dedicated gluten-free menu or those that are certified gluten-free. Additionally, reading reviews and asking the staff about their gluten-free practices can help you determine if a restaurant is safe for gluten-free dining.

3. Can I find gluten-free options at fast-food restaurants in Myrtle Beach?

Yes, some fast-food restaurants in Myrtle Beach offer gluten-free options. However, cross-contamination can be a concern, so it’s important to ask about their preparation methods.

4. Are there gluten-free bakeries in Myrtle Beach?

Yes, there are several gluten-free bakeries in Myrtle Beach, including Johnny D's Waffles and Bakery, which offers a variety of gluten-free pastries and baked goods.
