Understanding Gluten-Free Dining

Gluten-free dining is more than just a trend; it’s a necessity for millions of people worldwide. Gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, can cause severe health issues for those with celiac disease or gluten intolerance. For these individuals, consuming even trace amounts of gluten can lead to symptoms like abdominal pain, bloating, and long-term damage to the small intestine. Therefore, finding restaurants that understand and cater to these needs is crucial.

Precautions and Cross-Contamination

One of the biggest concerns for gluten-free diners is cross-contamination. Miller's Ale House takes this seriously by implementing strict kitchen protocols. Separate cooking areas, utensils, and fryers are used to prepare gluten-free dishes, minimizing the risk of cross-contact. Additionally, staff members are trained to handle gluten-free orders with care, ensuring that your meal is safe to eat.

Tips for a Safe and Enjoyable Experience

While Miller's Ale House goes above and beyond to accommodate gluten-free diners, there are a few steps you can take to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience:

  • Communicate Clearly: Inform your server about your dietary restrictions and ask questions about the preparation process.
  • Check the Menu: Review the gluten-free menu online before your visit to familiarize yourself with the options.
  • Ask About Specials: Some locations may offer gluten-free specials that aren’t listed on the regular menu.
  • Double-Check Your Order: When your meal arrives, confirm with your server that it’s gluten-free.
