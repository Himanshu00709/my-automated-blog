Gluten-Free Dining Near TD Garden: A Comprehensive Guide

For those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, finding safe and delicious gluten-free dining options can be a challenge. Why Gluten-Free Matters

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. For individuals with celiac disease, consuming gluten can lead to severe health issues, including damage to the small intestine. Even for those with non-celiac gluten sensitivity, avoiding gluten can alleviate symptoms like bloating, fatigue, and headaches. As awareness of gluten-related disorders has grown, so has the availability of gluten-free options in restaurants. The area around TD Garden is no exception, with several establishments offering dedicated gluten-free menus or clearly marked gluten-free dishes.

What to Look for in a Gluten-Free Restaurant

When dining out gluten-free, it's important to ensure that the restaurant takes the necessary precautions to avoid cross-contamination. Here are some key factors to consider:

  • Dedicated Gluten-Free Menu: A separate menu indicates that the restaurant is knowledgeable about gluten-free needs.
  • Staff Training: Well-trained staff can answer questions about ingredients and preparation methods.
  • Cross-Contamination Prevention: Look for restaurants that use separate cooking surfaces, utensils, and fryers for gluten-free dishes.
  • Certifications: Some restaurants are certified gluten-free by organizations like the Gluten Intolerance Group (GIG).
