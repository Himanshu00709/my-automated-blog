Understanding Gluten-Free Dining

Before diving into the menu, it’s important to understand what gluten-free dining entails. Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, which can cause adverse reactions in individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. For these individuals, consuming gluten can lead to symptoms ranging from mild discomfort to severe health issues. Therefore, it’s crucial for restaurants to offer gluten-free options that are prepared in a way that avoids cross-contamination.

Chicago Fire takes this responsibility seriously, ensuring that their gluten-free menu items are prepared with care. They use separate cooking surfaces and utensils to minimize the risk of cross-contamination, making it a safer choice for those with gluten-related dietary restrictions.

FAQs About Chicago Fire Gluten-Free Menu

1. Is Chicago Fire’s gluten-free menu safe for people with celiac disease?

Yes, Chicago Fire takes precautions to minimize cross-contamination by using separate cooking surfaces and utensils for gluten-free items. However, it’s always a good idea to inform your server about your dietary restrictions to ensure extra care is taken.

2. Can I customize my gluten-free pizza with any toppings?

Absolutely! You can customize your gluten-free pizza with any of the available toppings. Just make sure to double-check with your server to ensure that all toppings are gluten-free.

3. Are there any gluten-free pasta options at Chicago Fire?

Currently, Chicago Fire does not offer gluten-free pasta options. However, their gluten-free pizza and salad options provide plenty of variety for a satisfying meal.

4. Does Chicago Fire offer gluten-free beer?

Yes, Chicago Fire offers a selection of gluten-free beers. Be sure to ask your server for the current gluten-free beer options available.
