Tips for Dining Gluten-Free at Foxwoods

When dining gluten-free at Foxwoods, it’s important to communicate your dietary needs clearly to the staff. Here are some tips to ensure a safe and enjoyable dining experience:

  • Inform the Staff: Always inform your server about your gluten-free requirements. Most restaurants at Foxwoods are well-equipped to handle gluten-free requests, but clear communication is key.
  • Ask About Preparation: Inquire about how the food is prepared to avoid cross-contamination. Many restaurants have dedicated gluten-free preparation areas, but it’s always good to double-check.
  • Check the Menu: Many restaurants at Foxwoods have gluten-free menus or clearly marked gluten-free options. Don’t hesitate to ask for a gluten-free menu if it’s not immediately available.
  • Plan Ahead: For fine dining experiences, consider calling ahead to inform the restaurant of your dietary needs. This allows the chef to prepare accordingly and ensures a seamless dining experience.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Dining at Foxwoods

1. Are there gluten-free options at every restaurant in Foxwoods?

While most restaurants at Foxwoods offer gluten-free options, it’s always best to check with the specific restaurant beforehand. Some restaurants may have more extensive gluten-free menus than others.

2. Can I find gluten-free snacks at Foxwoods?

Yes, Foxwoods offers gluten-free snacks at various cafes and bars throughout the resort. Be sure to check the labels or ask the staff to confirm that the snacks are gluten-free.

3. How does Foxwoods prevent cross-contamination in gluten-free dishes?

Many restaurants at Foxwoods have dedicated gluten-free preparation areas and use separate cooking utensils to prevent cross-contamination. Always inform your server about your dietary needs to ensure proper precautions are taken.

4. Can I request gluten-free catering for events at Foxwoods?

Yes, Foxwoods’ event catering services can accommodate gluten-free requests for weddings, conferences, and other special events. Be sure to inform the catering team of your dietary needs when planning your event.
