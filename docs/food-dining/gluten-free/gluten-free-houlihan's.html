Understanding Gluten and Its Impact on Health

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease, consuming gluten can trigger an immune response that damages the small intestine, leading to a host of health issues. Even for those without celiac disease, gluten sensitivity can cause symptoms like bloating, diarrhea, and fatigue. As a result, many people choose to avoid gluten altogether, making it essential for restaurants to provide gluten-free options.

Tips for Dining Gluten-Free at Houlihan's

While Houlihan's offers a variety of gluten-free options, it's important to take certain precautions to ensure a safe dining experience. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Communicate with Your Server: Always inform your server about your gluten-free dietary needs. They can guide you through the menu and alert the kitchen staff to take extra precautions.
  • Ask About Cross-Contamination: Cross-contamination can occur when gluten-free foods come into contact with gluten-containing foods. Ask your server about the restaurant's procedures to prevent cross-contamination.
  • Check for Updates: Menus can change, so it's a good idea to check for the latest gluten-free options when you visit. You can also visit Houlihan's website or contact the restaurant directly for the most up-to-date information.
