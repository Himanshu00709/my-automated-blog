Why Go Gluten-Free?

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. For individuals with celiac disease, consuming gluten can lead to severe health issues, including damage to the small intestine, nutrient deficiencies, and a host of other symptoms. Even for those without celiac disease, gluten sensitivity can cause discomfort, bloating, and fatigue. As a result, many people are choosing to adopt a gluten-free diet to improve their overall health and well-being.

Tips for Dining Out Gluten-Free

When dining out, it's essential to communicate your dietary needs clearly to the staff. Here are some tips to ensure a safe and enjoyable gluten-free dining experience:

  • Ask Questions: Don't hesitate to ask about ingredients and preparation methods. Many restaurants are happy to accommodate gluten-free requests.
  • Check for Cross-Contamination: Ensure that the kitchen uses separate utensils and cooking surfaces to avoid cross-contamination.
  • Look for Certified Gluten-Free Restaurants: Some restaurants are certified gluten-free, meaning they adhere to strict guidelines to prevent cross-contamination.
