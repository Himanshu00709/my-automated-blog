Why Go Gluten-Free?

Gluten-free diets are essential for individuals with celiac disease, an autoimmune disorder triggered by gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. For others, gluten sensitivity or intolerance can cause symptoms like bloating, fatigue, and digestive issues. Additionally, some people choose a gluten-free diet for its potential health benefits, such as improved digestion and increased energy levels.

Middletown, CT, offers a variety of gluten-free options, from restaurants to grocery stores, making it easier for residents and visitors to maintain a gluten-free lifestyle.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Living in Middletown, CT

1. What is celiac disease?

Celiac disease is an autoimmune disorder where the ingestion of gluten leads to damage in the small intestine. It affects about 1% of the population and requires a strict gluten-free diet.

2. Are there gluten-free bakeries in Middletown?

Yes, Middletown has several bakeries that offer gluten-free options, including BrewBakers and the Middletown Farmers Market.

3. How can I ensure a restaurant meal is gluten-free?

Always inform the server about your dietary restrictions and ask about cross-contamination practices. Many restaurants in Middletown are well-versed in gluten-free dining and take precautions to ensure your meal is safe.

4. Where can I find gluten-free recipes?

Local cooking classes, community groups, and online resources are great places to find gluten-free recipes. Many Middletown residents also share recipes through social media groups.
