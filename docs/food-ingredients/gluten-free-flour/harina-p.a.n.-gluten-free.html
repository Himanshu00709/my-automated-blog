What is Harina P.A.N.?

Harina P.A.N. is a type of pre-cooked cornmeal that originated in Venezuela. It is made from white corn and is widely used to make arepas, a traditional Venezuelan and Colombian dish. The name "P.A.N." stands for "Productos Alimenticios Nacionales," which translates to "National Food Products." The brand has become synonymous with quality and authenticity, making it a household name in many countries.

How to Use Harina P.A.N. in Your Cooking

Harina P.A.N. is incredibly versatile and can be used in a variety of dishes. Here are some popular ways to use it:

1. Arepas

Arepas are perhaps the most well-known dish made with Harina P.A.N. These round, flatbreads are a staple in Venezuelan and Colombian cuisine. To make arepas, simply mix Harina P.A.N. with water and a pinch of salt, form the dough into patties, and cook them on a griddle or skillet until golden brown. Arepas can be filled with a variety of ingredients, such as cheese, beans, or meat, making them a versatile and satisfying meal.

2. Empanadas

Empanadas are another popular dish that can be made with Harina P.A.N. These stuffed pastries are typically filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables and then fried or baked. Using Harina P.A.N. for the dough ensures that your empanadas are gluten-free and delicious.

3. Tamales

Tamales are a traditional Mesoamerican dish made from masa (a dough made from corn) and filled with various ingredients, such as meat, cheese, or vegetables. Harina P.A.N. can be used to make the masa, resulting in gluten-free tamales that are both flavorful and nutritious.
