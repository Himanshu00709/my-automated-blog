What is Gluten-Free Flour?

Gluten-free flour is a type of flour that does not contain gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of health issues, including digestive problems, nutrient deficiencies, and even long-term damage to the small intestine. Gluten-free flour is made from alternative grains, nuts, or legumes, such as rice, almond, coconut, or chickpeas, providing a safe and nutritious option for those who need to avoid gluten.

How to Use Bob's Red Mill Gluten-Free Flour

Using gluten-free flour can be a bit different from using traditional wheat flour, but with a few tips and tricks, you can achieve excellent results. Here are some guidelines for using Bob's Red Mill Gluten-Free Flour:

  • Measure Accurately: Gluten-free flours can have different densities, so it's essential to measure them accurately. Use a kitchen scale for the best results.
  • Combine Flours: For baking, it's often beneficial to combine different types of gluten-free flours to achieve the desired texture and flavor. For example, a mix of almond flour and coconut flour can work well for cakes and cookies.
  • Add Binders: Since gluten-free flours lack the binding properties of gluten, you may need to add binders like xanthan gum or psyllium husk to help hold your baked goods together.
  • Adjust Liquid Levels: Gluten-free flours can absorb more or less liquid than wheat flour, so you may need to adjust the amount of liquid in your recipes accordingly.
