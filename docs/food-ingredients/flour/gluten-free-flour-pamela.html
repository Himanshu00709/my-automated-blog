What is Gluten-Free Flour?

Gluten-free flour is a type of flour that does not contain gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. Gluten is responsible for the elasticity and chewiness in traditional baked goods, but for individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to serious health issues. Gluten-free flours are made from a variety of grains, nuts, and starches, such as rice flour, almond flour, tapioca starch, and potato starch. These flours are often blended to mimic the texture and flavor of traditional wheat flour, making them suitable for a wide range of baking applications.

How to Use Pamela's Gluten-Free Flour

Using Pamela's gluten-free flour is straightforward, but there are a few tips to keep in mind to achieve the best results:

  • Measure Accurately: Gluten-free flours can be denser than traditional wheat flour, so it's important to measure accurately to avoid dry or dense baked goods.
  • Add Binders: Since gluten-free flours lack the elasticity of gluten, adding binders like xanthan gum or psyllium husk can help improve the texture of your baked goods.
  • Experiment: Don't be afraid to experiment with different recipes and techniques to find what works best for you.
