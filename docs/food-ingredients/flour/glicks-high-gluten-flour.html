What is Glicks High Gluten Flour?

Glicks High Gluten Flour is a type of wheat flour that contains a higher percentage of protein compared to all-purpose or bread flour. The protein content typically ranges between 13% to 14.5%, which is significantly higher than the 10% to 12% found in standard bread flours. This high protein content is crucial because it directly influences the amount of gluten that forms when the flour is mixed with water. Gluten is the network of proteins that gives dough its structure, elasticity, and chewiness.

Glicks High Gluten Flour is milled from hard wheat varieties, which are known for their strong gluten-forming properties. This makes it an ideal choice for recipes that require a robust dough structure, such as bagels, pizza crusts, and artisan bread. The flour is also unbleached and unbromated, ensuring a natural product that retains its nutritional value.

How to Use Glicks High Gluten Flour

Using Glicks High Gluten Flour is straightforward, but there are a few tips and tricks to get the most out of this premium ingredient. Here’s a step-by-step guide to incorporating it into your baking:

  1. Measure Accurately: Use a kitchen scale to measure your flour by weight rather than volume. This ensures consistency in your recipes.
  2. Hydrate Properly: High gluten flour absorbs more water than regular flour. Adjust your liquid ingredients accordingly to achieve the desired dough consistency.
  3. Knead Thoroughly: The dough made with high gluten flour requires thorough kneading to develop the gluten network fully. This can take anywhere from 10 to 15 minutes by hand or 5 to 7 minutes in a stand mixer.
  4. Allow for Proper Proofing: Give your dough enough time to rise. The strong gluten structure will trap gas bubbles, resulting in a well-risen final product.
  5. Experiment with Blending: If you’re new to high gluten flour, consider blending it with all-purpose or bread flour to ease into its unique properties.

FAQs About Glicks High Gluten Flour

1. Can I use Glicks High Gluten Flour for cakes and pastries?

While Glicks High Gluten Flour is excellent for bread and pizza, it’s not ideal for cakes and pastries, which require a softer, more tender crumb. For these baked goods, it’s best to use a lower-protein flour like cake or pastry flour.

2. Is Glicks High Gluten Flour suitable for gluten-free diets?

No, Glicks High Gluten Flour is not suitable for gluten-free diets. It contains a high amount of gluten, which is a protein found in wheat. Those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity should avoid this flour.

3. How should I store Glicks High Gluten Flour?

Store Glicks High Gluten Flour in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. For longer storage, you can keep it in the refrigerator or freezer to prevent rancidity and maintain freshness.

4. Can I substitute Glicks High Gluten Flour for all-purpose flour?

You can substitute Glicks High Gluten Flour for all-purpose flour, but the results will differ. The higher protein content will result in a chewier, denser texture, which may not be desirable for all recipes.
