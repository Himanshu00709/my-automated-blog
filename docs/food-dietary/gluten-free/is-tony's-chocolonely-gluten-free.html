Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to more severe health complications. Therefore, it's crucial for those affected to carefully scrutinize the ingredients of any food product, including chocolate.

When it comes to chocolate, gluten is not typically an ingredient. However, cross-contamination can occur during the manufacturing process, especially if the chocolate is produced in facilities that also handle gluten-containing products. This is why it's essential to look for certifications or statements from the manufacturer regarding gluten-free status.

Is Tony's Chocolonely Gluten-Free?

Tony's Chocolonely is known for its transparency and commitment to quality, and this extends to its gluten-free status. The company has stated that all of its chocolate bars are gluten-free. This means that the chocolate itself does not contain any gluten-containing ingredients, and the company takes precautions to avoid cross-contamination during production.

However, it's important to note that while Tony's Chocolonely does not intentionally add gluten to its products, the company does not certify its chocolate as gluten-free. This means that there is a small risk of cross-contamination, although the likelihood is minimal. For most people with gluten sensitivity, Tony's Chocolonely is considered safe to consume. However, those with celiac disease should exercise caution and consult with their healthcare provider if they have any concerns.
