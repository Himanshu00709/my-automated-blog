Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. For most people, gluten is harmless, but for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, it can cause a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to severe autoimmune reactions. This makes it crucial for individuals with these conditions to carefully scrutinize the ingredients and manufacturing processes of the foods they consume.

Manufacturing Processes and Cross-Contamination

Even if a product does not contain gluten-containing ingredients, cross-contamination during manufacturing can still pose a risk. This occurs when gluten-free products come into contact with gluten-containing products or surfaces during production. Unfortunately, Old El Paso does not specifically label their Fiesta Twists as gluten-free, which suggests that they may not take the necessary precautions to prevent cross-contamination.

For individuals with celiac disease, even trace amounts of gluten can trigger symptoms. Therefore, it’s essential to look for products that are certified gluten-free, meaning they have been tested and verified to contain less than 20 parts per million (ppm) of gluten, the threshold considered safe for most people with celiac disease.

Labeling and Certifications

When it comes to gluten-free products, labeling is key. In the United States, the FDA has specific guidelines for what can be labeled as gluten-free. Products must contain less than 20 ppm of gluten and must not contain any gluten-containing ingredients. However, Old El Paso Fiesta Twists do not carry a gluten-free label, which is a strong indication that they are not suitable for those on a gluten-free diet.

Additionally, some products may carry third-party certifications, such as the Gluten-Free Certification Organization (GFCO) seal, which provides an extra layer of assurance. Unfortunately, Old El Paso Fiesta Twists do not have this certification either.
