Understanding Gluten and Gluten-Free Diets

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, including digestive issues, fatigue, and even long-term health complications. A gluten-free diet eliminates all sources of gluten, requiring careful attention to food labels and ingredients.

Market Basket has made it easier for consumers to identify gluten-free products by clearly labeling them and offering a dedicated section for gluten-free items. This section includes everything from gluten-free bread and pasta to snacks and baking ingredients.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What does "gluten-free" mean?

A product labeled "gluten-free" means it contains less than 20 parts per million (ppm) of gluten, which is the threshold considered safe for most people with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity.

2. Are all Market Basket brand products gluten-free?

Not all Market Basket brand products are gluten-free. However, many of their products are labeled as gluten-free, so be sure to check the packaging for the gluten-free label.

3. Can I find gluten-free fresh produce at Market Basket?

Yes, fresh fruits and vegetables are naturally gluten-free and can be found in the produce section of Market Basket. However, always wash them thoroughly to avoid cross-contamination.

4. Does Market Basket offer gluten-free meal options?

Yes, Market Basket offers a variety of gluten-free meal options, including frozen meals, pre-packaged salads, and ready-to-eat items. Be sure to check the labels for gluten-free certification.

5. How can I avoid cross-contamination when shopping for gluten-free products?

To avoid cross-contamination, look for products that are specifically labeled as gluten-free and stored separately from gluten-containing items. Additionally, consider using separate shopping bags or containers for gluten-free products.
