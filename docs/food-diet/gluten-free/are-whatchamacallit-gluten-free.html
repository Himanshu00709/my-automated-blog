Are Whatchamacallit Gluten-Free? A Comprehensive Guide

For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, navigating the world of snacks can be a daunting task. One popular treat that often comes into question is the Whatchamacallit candy bar. Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For most people, gluten is harmless, but for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, even trace amounts can cause severe health issues. Symptoms can range from digestive problems to skin rashes, fatigue, and even neurological issues. Therefore, it’s crucial for individuals with these conditions to be vigilant about the foods they consume.

At first glance, none of these ingredients contain gluten. However, the devil is often in the details. Let’s take a closer look at each component:

Milk Chocolate

Milk chocolate typically contains sugar, cocoa butter, milk, and chocolate liquor. None of these ingredients are derived from gluten-containing grains. However, some chocolate products may include additives or flavorings that contain gluten. In the case of Whatchamacallit, the milk chocolate used does not contain any gluten-based additives.

Peanut Butter

Peanut butter is generally gluten-free, as it is made from ground peanuts and sometimes includes additional oils or sweeteners. The peanut butter in Whatchamacallit is no exception and does not contain any gluten-containing ingredients.

Crisped Rice

Crisped rice is a common ingredient in many candy bars and cereals. It is typically made from rice, which is naturally gluten-free. However, some crisped rice products may be processed in facilities that also handle gluten-containing grains, leading to potential cross-contamination. In the case of Whatchamacallit, the crisped rice is sourced from facilities that do not process gluten-containing grains, making it safe for those with gluten sensitivities.

Manufacturing and Cross-Contamination Risks

Even if all the ingredients in a product are gluten-free, cross-contamination during manufacturing can still pose a risk. This occurs when gluten-free products come into contact with gluten-containing products or surfaces during production. To assess the risk of cross-contamination with Whatchamacallit, we need to consider the manufacturing practices of the company that produces them.

Hershey’s, the manufacturer of Whatchamacallit, has a comprehensive allergen control program in place. According to Hershey’s, Whatchamacallit is produced in facilities that do not process gluten-containing ingredients, significantly reducing the risk of cross-contamination.
