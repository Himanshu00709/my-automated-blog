Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to more severe health complications. Therefore, it’s essential to ensure that the foods they consume are free from gluten contamination.

Gluten can be hidden in many processed foods, either as a direct ingredient or through cross-contamination during manufacturing. This makes it challenging for those on a gluten-free diet to navigate the grocery aisles confidently. Let’s take a closer look at Alexia Rosemary Fries to see if they meet the gluten-free criteria.

Manufacturing Processes and Cross-Contamination

Even if a product’s ingredients are gluten-free, cross-contamination during manufacturing can still pose a risk. Cross-contamination occurs when gluten-free products come into contact with gluten-containing products or surfaces during production. This can happen in facilities that process both gluten-free and gluten-containing foods.

Alexia Foods, the manufacturer of Alexia Rosemary Fries, has stated that they take precautions to prevent cross-contamination. However, they do not claim that their products are certified gluten-free. This means that while the ingredients themselves may be gluten-free, there is still a risk of cross-contamination during production.

Unfortunately, Alexia Rosemary Fries do not carry a gluten-free certification from either of these organizations.
