Understanding Gluten and Its Impact on Health

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease, consuming gluten triggers an immune response that damages the small intestine, leading to a host of health issues. Non-celiac gluten sensitivity (NCGS) is another condition where individuals experience symptoms similar to celiac disease but without the intestinal damage. Additionally, some people choose a gluten-free diet for perceived health benefits, such as improved digestion and increased energy levels.

Given the potential health risks and the growing number of people adopting gluten-free diets, restaurants like Bonefish Grill have recognized the importance of offering gluten-free options. This not only caters to those with medical conditions but also appeals to health-conscious diners.

Tips for Dining Gluten-Free at Bonefish Grill

While Bonefish Grill offers a variety of gluten-free options, it's important for diners to take certain precautions to ensure a safe dining experience. Here are some tips for dining gluten-free at Bonefish Grill:

  • Communicate Your Needs: Inform your server about your gluten intolerance or celiac disease. The staff at Bonefish Grill is well-trained to accommodate gluten-free requests and can guide you through the menu.
  • Ask About Preparation: Even if a dish is listed as gluten-free, it's always a good idea to ask about how it's prepared. Ensure that the kitchen takes steps to avoid cross-contamination, such as using separate utensils and cooking surfaces.
  • Check for Updates: Menus can change, so it's a good practice to check for the latest gluten-free options when you visit. You can also call ahead to inquire about the availability of specific dishes.
  • Enjoy with Confidence: Bonefish Grill's commitment to gluten-free dining means you can enjoy your meal with confidence, knowing that your dietary needs are being taken seriously.
