Understanding Gluten and Its Impact on Health

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and their derivatives. For individuals with celiac disease, consuming gluten triggers an immune response that damages the small intestine, leading to various health complications. Even for those with non-celiac gluten sensitivity, gluten can cause symptoms like bloating, fatigue, and digestive discomfort. As a result, many people are turning to gluten-free products to improve their health and well-being.

When it comes to seasonings, gluten can sometimes be a hidden ingredient. Cross-contamination during manufacturing is also a concern. Therefore, it’s essential to thoroughly examine the ingredients and production practices of any seasoning brand before incorporating it into a gluten-free diet.

Manufacturing Processes and Cross-Contamination Risks

Even if a product’s ingredients are gluten-free, cross-contamination during manufacturing can pose a risk. This occurs when gluten-free products come into contact with gluten-containing products or surfaces during production. To ensure safety, many brands implement strict protocols to prevent cross-contamination.

Dan-O's Seasoning is manufactured in facilities that prioritize quality and safety. According to the company, their seasonings are produced in environments where gluten-containing ingredients are not processed. This significantly reduces the risk of cross-contamination. However, it’s important to note that Dan-O's Seasoning is not certified gluten-free by a third-party organization, which means there is no official guarantee of its gluten-free status.
