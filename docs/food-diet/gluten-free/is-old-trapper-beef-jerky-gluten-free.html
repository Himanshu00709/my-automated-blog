Understanding Gluten and Its Impact on Health

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For most people, gluten is harmless, but for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, it can cause severe health issues. Symptoms of gluten intolerance can range from digestive problems to skin rashes, fatigue, and even neurological issues. Therefore, it’s crucial for individuals with these conditions to avoid gluten-containing foods.

When it comes to beef jerky, gluten can be present in the form of soy sauce, marinades, or other flavorings that contain wheat or barley. This makes it essential to scrutinize the ingredients and manufacturing processes of any beef jerky product.

Manufacturing Process and Cross-Contamination

Even if a product’s ingredients are gluten-free, cross-contamination can occur during the manufacturing process. This happens when gluten-free products are processed on the same equipment as gluten-containing products, leading to trace amounts of gluten being present in the final product.

Old Trapper does not explicitly state that their beef jerky is processed in a gluten-free facility. This means there is a possibility of cross-contamination, which could be a concern for individuals with severe gluten intolerance or celiac disease.

Is Old Trapper Beef Jerky Certified Gluten-Free?
