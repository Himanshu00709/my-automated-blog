Understanding Gluten and Gluten-Free Diets

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. It acts as a binding agent, giving bread its elasticity and chewy texture. For individuals with celiac disease, gluten sensitivity, or wheat allergy, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, including digestive issues, fatigue, and even long-term health complications. As a result, many people opt for gluten-free diets to avoid these adverse effects.
