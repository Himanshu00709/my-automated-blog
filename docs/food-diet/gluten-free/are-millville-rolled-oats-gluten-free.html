Understanding Gluten and Its Impact on Health

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, including digestive issues, fatigue, and even long-term health complications. Oats, by nature, do not contain gluten. However, they are often contaminated with gluten during processing, which is why many oats on the market are not considered gluten-free.

Are Millville Rolled Oats Gluten-Free?

Millville Rolled Oats are a popular choice for many households due to their affordability and versatility. However, when it comes to their gluten-free status, the answer is not straightforward. Millville Rolled Oats are not certified gluten-free, which means they may contain traces of gluten due to cross-contamination during processing. This is a critical consideration for individuals with celiac disease or severe gluten sensitivity.

It's important to note that while oats themselves do not contain gluten, they are often processed in facilities that also handle wheat, barley, or rye. This cross-contamination can introduce gluten into the oats, making them unsafe for those who need to avoid gluten entirely.
