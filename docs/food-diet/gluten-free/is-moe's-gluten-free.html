Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to more severe health complications. As a result, many people are turning to gluten-free diets to manage their health. However, navigating a gluten-free diet, especially when dining out, requires careful consideration of ingredients, preparation methods, and potential cross-contamination.

How Moe's Handles Cross-Contamination

Cross-contamination is a significant concern for individuals with celiac disease or severe gluten intolerance. Moe's Southwest Grill has implemented several practices to minimize the risk of cross-contamination:

  • Separate Preparation Areas: Moe's staff are trained to use separate utensils and preparation areas for gluten-free orders.
  • Gluten-Free Awareness: Employees are educated about gluten-free dietary needs and the importance of avoiding cross-contamination.
  • Customization: Customers can customize their orders to exclude gluten-containing ingredients, and staff are generally accommodating to these requests.

However, it's always a good idea to inform the staff about your gluten-free requirements and ask them to take extra precautions when preparing your meal.

Tips for Dining Gluten-Free at Moe's

To ensure a safe and enjoyable gluten-free dining experience at Moe's, consider the following tips:

  1. Communicate Clearly: Inform the staff about your gluten-free needs and ask them to change gloves and use clean utensils.
  2. Choose Wisely: Stick to menu items that are naturally gluten-free, such as burrito bowls, tacos with corn tortillas, and salads without croutons.
  3. Avoid Sauces and Dressings: Some sauces and dressings may contain gluten, so it's best to ask for gluten-free options or skip them altogether.
  4. Check for Updates: Menu items and ingredients can change, so always double-check with the staff or consult Moe's official website for the latest gluten-free information.
