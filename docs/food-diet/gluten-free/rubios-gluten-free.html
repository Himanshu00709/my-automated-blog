Understanding Gluten and Its Impact on Health

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. For individuals with celiac disease, consuming gluten can trigger an immune response that damages the small intestine, leading to a range of symptoms from digestive issues to nutrient deficiencies. Non-celiac gluten sensitivity, while less severe, can also cause discomfort and health problems. As a result, many people are opting for gluten-free diets to improve their health and well-being.

Rubio's recognizes the importance of catering to these dietary needs and has made significant efforts to provide safe and delicious gluten-free options. Their commitment to quality and customer satisfaction is evident in their menu, which features a variety of gluten-free dishes that don't compromise on taste or quality.

Precautions and Cross-Contamination

Cross-contamination is a significant concern for individuals with celiac disease or severe gluten sensitivity. Even trace amounts of gluten can cause adverse reactions. Rubio's understands this and has implemented several measures to reduce the risk of cross-contamination:

  • Separate Preparation Areas: Rubio's uses designated areas and utensils for preparing gluten-free meals.
  • Staff Training: Employees are trained to handle gluten-free orders with care, ensuring that gluten-free ingredients are not mixed with gluten-containing ones.
  • Clear Labeling: Gluten-free items are clearly marked on the menu, making it easier for customers to identify safe options.

Despite these precautions, it's always a good idea to communicate your dietary needs to the staff when placing your order. This will help ensure that your meal is prepared with the utmost care.
