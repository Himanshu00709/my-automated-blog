Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and their derivatives. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to more severe autoimmune reactions. Therefore, it is crucial for these individuals to carefully scrutinize the ingredients and manufacturing processes of the foods they consume.

Manufacturing Processes and Cross-Contamination

Even if a product's ingredients are gluten-free, cross-contamination during manufacturing can pose a risk. Cross-contamination occurs when gluten-free products come into contact with gluten-containing products or surfaces during production. This can happen in facilities that process both gluten-free and gluten-containing foods.

Brach's, the manufacturer of Mellowcreme Pumpkins, does not explicitly label their products as gluten-free. This lack of a gluten-free certification or label suggests that there may be a risk of cross-contamination. Additionally, Brach's produces a wide range of candies, some of which may contain gluten. Therefore, individuals with severe gluten sensitivities or celiac disease should exercise caution.
