Understanding Gluten and Gluten-Free Diets

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease, consuming gluten can lead to severe health issues, including damage to the small intestine. Even for those with non-celiac gluten sensitivity, gluten can cause discomfort and other symptoms. As a result, many people choose to follow a gluten-free diet to avoid these adverse effects.

When it comes to dairy products like cream cheese, the question of gluten content is particularly important. While dairy products are naturally gluten-free, cross-contamination during processing or the addition of gluten-containing ingredients can make them unsafe for those with gluten intolerance.

Cross-Contamination Concerns

While Tillamook Cream Cheese is gluten-free, it's important to consider the potential for cross-contamination. Cross-contamination can occur when gluten-free products come into contact with gluten-containing products during manufacturing, packaging, or transportation. Tillamook has stated that they take precautions to prevent cross-contamination, but it's always a good idea to be vigilant, especially if you have severe gluten sensitivity or celiac disease.

If you are highly sensitive to gluten, you may want to contact Tillamook directly to inquire about their manufacturing processes and any potential risks of cross-contamination. Additionally, always check the packaging for any allergen warnings or statements about shared equipment.
