Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. For individuals with celiac disease, consuming gluten can lead to severe health issues, including damage to the small intestine. Even for those without celiac disease, gluten sensitivity can cause discomfort and other health problems. As a result, many people are opting for gluten-free diets to improve their overall well-being.

Main Event has taken this into consideration by offering a variety of gluten-free options across its menu. Whether you're visiting for a fun day of bowling, laser tag, or arcade games, you can rest assured that there are gluten-free choices available to keep you energized and satisfied.

It's important to note that while Main Event strives to provide gluten-free options, cross-contamination can occur in the kitchen. If you have severe gluten intolerance or celiac disease, it's advisable to inform the staff so they can take extra precautions.

Tips for Dining Gluten-Free at Main Event

Dining out gluten-free can be challenging, but with a little preparation, you can enjoy a safe and delicious meal at Main Event. Here are some tips to help you navigate the menu:

  • Communicate with Staff: Always inform your server about your dietary restrictions. Main Event staff are trained to assist with gluten-free requests and can guide you to the safest options.
  • Check for Cross-Contamination: Ask about the kitchen's procedures to avoid cross-contamination. This is especially important if you have celiac disease.
  • Review the Menu: Take the time to review the menu carefully. Main Event's gluten-free options are clearly marked, but it's always good to double-check.
  • Bring Your Own Snacks: If you're unsure about the available options, consider bringing your own gluten-free snacks. This ensures you have something safe to eat while enjoying your time at Main Event.
