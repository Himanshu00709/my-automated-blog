Understanding Gluten-Free Diets

A gluten-free diet excludes the protein gluten, which is found in grains such as wheat, barley, and rye. This diet is essential for individuals with celiac disease, a condition where gluten consumption leads to damage in the small intestine. Additionally, people with non-celiac gluten sensitivity or wheat allergy may also benefit from a gluten-free diet.

Adopting a gluten-free lifestyle can be challenging, especially when it comes to finding suitable food options. However, Lakeland has made significant strides in catering to this dietary need, offering a variety of gluten-free products and dining options.

Benefits of a Gluten-Free Diet

While a gluten-free diet is essential for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, it can also offer several health benefits for others. These include improved digestive health, increased energy levels, and better nutrient absorption. However, it’s important to note that a gluten-free diet should be well-balanced to ensure adequate intake of essential nutrients.

4. What should I look for when buying gluten-free products?

When purchasing gluten-free products, look for labels that clearly state "gluten-free." Additionally, check the ingredient list to ensure there are no hidden sources of gluten.

5. Are gluten-free products more expensive?

Gluten-free products can be more expensive than their gluten-containing counterparts due to the cost of alternative ingredients and production processes. However, prices are becoming more competitive as demand increases.
