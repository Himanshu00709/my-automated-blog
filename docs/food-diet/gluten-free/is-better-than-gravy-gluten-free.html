Is Better Than Gravy Gluten-Free? A Comprehensive Guide

Gravy is a staple in many households, especially during holiday meals and family gatherings. However, for those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease, finding a gluten-free gravy option can be a challenge. Enter "Better Than Gravy," a popular gravy mix that claims to be a healthier and more convenient alternative to traditional gravy. But is Better Than Gravy gluten-free? In this article, we’ll explore everything you need to know about Better Than Gravy, its ingredients, and whether it’s a safe option for those avoiding gluten.

What is Better Than Gravy?

Better Than Gravy is a powdered gravy mix that promises to deliver the rich, savory flavor of traditional gravy without the hassle of making it from scratch. It’s marketed as a healthier alternative, often containing fewer calories, less fat, and no artificial flavors or preservatives. The mix is designed to be quick and easy to prepare, requiring only water or broth to create a delicious gravy in minutes.

Ingredients in Better Than Gravy

To determine whether Better Than Gravy is gluten-free, it’s essential to examine its ingredients. The primary ingredients in most Better Than Gravy mixes include:

  • Modified cornstarch
  • Wheat flour
  • Hydrolyzed soy protein
  • Natural flavors
  • Salt
  • Onion powder
  • Garlic powder
  • Spices

As you can see, wheat flour is listed as one of the main ingredients, which means that the standard Better Than Gravy mix is not gluten-free. Wheat flour contains gluten, a protein that can cause adverse reactions in individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity.

Gluten-Free Alternatives to Better Than Gravy

If you’re looking for a gluten-free gravy option, there are several alternatives to Better Than Gravy that you can consider. Many brands offer gluten-free gravy mixes that are made with gluten-free flours such as rice flour, potato starch, or tapioca starch. Here’s a comparison of some popular gluten-free gravy mixes:

How to Make Your Own Gluten-Free Gravy

If you prefer to make your own gluten-free gravy at home, it’s easier than you might think. Here’s a simple recipe for a delicious gluten-free gravy:

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons gluten-free flour (such as rice flour or cornstarch)
  • 2 tablespoons butter or olive oil
  • 2 cups gluten-free chicken or vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: herbs and spices for added flavor

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, melt the butter or heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Whisk in the gluten-free flour until it forms a smooth paste (roux).
  3. Gradually add the broth, whisking constantly to prevent lumps.
  4. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook until it thickens, stirring occasionally.
  5. Season with salt, pepper, and any additional herbs or spices you prefer.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!

This homemade gluten-free gravy is not only easy to make but also allows you to control the ingredients and flavors to suit your taste.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Gravy

1. Is Better Than Gravy gluten-free?

No, the standard Better Than Gravy mix contains wheat flour, which is not gluten-free. However, there are gluten-free alternatives available on the market.

2. What are some gluten-free gravy brands?

Some popular gluten-free gravy brands include McCormick Gluten-Free Gravy Mix, Simply Organic Gravy Mix, and Pacific Foods Gluten-Free Gravy.

3. Can I make gluten-free gravy at home?

Yes, you can easily make gluten-free gravy at home using gluten-free flour, butter or oil, and gluten-free broth. Follow the recipe provided above for a simple and delicious homemade gravy.

4. What are the main ingredients in gluten-free gravy?

Gluten-free gravy typically uses gluten-free flours such as rice flour, potato starch, or tapioca starch as a thickening agent, along with broth, butter or oil, and seasonings.

Conclusion

While Better Than Gravy is a convenient and flavorful option for many, it is not suitable for those following a gluten-free diet due to the inclusion of wheat flour. However, there are plenty of gluten-free gravy alternatives available, both in stores and as homemade recipes. By choosing the right ingredients and brands, you can enjoy delicious, gluten-free gravy that complements your meals without compromising your dietary needs. Whether you opt for a store-bought mix or decide to make your own, gluten-free gravy is a tasty and safe option for everyone at the table.
