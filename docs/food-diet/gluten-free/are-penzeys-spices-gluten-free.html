Understanding Gluten and Its Presence in Spices

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and their derivatives. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, even trace amounts of gluten can trigger adverse health effects. While spices themselves are naturally gluten-free, cross-contamination during processing or packaging can introduce gluten into the final product. This is why it’s essential to scrutinize the sourcing and manufacturing practices of spice brands.

Penzeys Spices has built a reputation for transparency and quality, but let’s take a closer look at their gluten-free claims.

Penzeys Spices is known for its dedication to providing high-quality, pure spices. The company sources its spices from around the world and takes pride in offering products that are free from artificial additives, preservatives, and fillers. But what about gluten?

According to Penzeys, the majority of their spices are gluten-free. The company states that they do not add gluten to any of their products, and they take steps to minimize the risk of cross-contamination. However, they also acknowledge that their facilities are not entirely gluten-free, as they handle a variety of products, including some that may contain gluten.
