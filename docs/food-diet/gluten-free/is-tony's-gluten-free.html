Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and their derivatives. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to more severe health complications. Therefore, it is crucial for these individuals to ensure that the products they consume are free from gluten contamination.

When evaluating whether a product is gluten-free, it's essential to consider not only the ingredients but also the manufacturing process. Cross-contamination can occur if gluten-free products are processed in facilities that also handle gluten-containing ingredients. This is why certifications and labeling are so important.

Certifications and Labeling

One of the most reliable ways to determine whether a product is gluten-free is to look for certifications from recognized organizations. Tony's products are certified gluten-free by the Gluten-Free Certification Organization (GFCO), which ensures that the products meet strict gluten-free standards. The GFCO certification requires that products contain less than 10 parts per million (ppm) of gluten, which is the threshold considered safe for most individuals with celiac disease.

In addition to the GFCO certification, Tony's products are clearly labeled as gluten-free, making it easy for consumers to identify safe options. The company is committed to transparency and provides detailed ingredient lists and allergen information on their packaging.
