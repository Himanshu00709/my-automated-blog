Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. For individuals with celiac disease, consuming gluten triggers an immune response that damages the small intestine. This can lead to a host of symptoms, including digestive issues, fatigue, and nutrient deficiencies. Even for those without celiac disease, gluten sensitivity can cause discomfort and health problems. As a result, many people are turning to gluten-free diets to improve their well-being.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is celiac disease?

Celiac disease is an autoimmune disorder where the ingestion of gluten leads to damage in the small intestine. It affects about 1% of the population and requires a strict gluten-free diet for management.

Is a gluten-free diet healthier?

For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, a gluten-free diet is essential. However, for those without these conditions, a gluten-free diet is not necessarily healthier and may lack certain nutrients if not properly balanced.

Can I find gluten-free options at regular restaurants in CDA?

Yes, many restaurants in Coeur d'Alene offer gluten-free options. It's always a good idea to inform the staff about your dietary restrictions to ensure that your meal is prepared safely.

Are there gluten-free events in CDA?

Yes, Coeur d'Alene hosts various gluten-free events, including cooking classes, support group meetings, and food festivals. These events are a great way to connect with others and learn more about living gluten-free.
