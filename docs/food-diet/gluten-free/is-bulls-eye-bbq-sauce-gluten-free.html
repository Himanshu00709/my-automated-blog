Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to more severe health complications. Therefore, it’s crucial to ensure that the foods and condiments they consume are free from gluten.

When it comes to BBQ sauces, gluten can sometimes be hidden in ingredients like malt vinegar, soy sauce, or certain thickeners. This makes it essential to scrutinize the ingredient list and understand the manufacturing processes of the products you consume.

Manufacturing Processes and Cross-Contamination Risks

Cross-contamination occurs when gluten-free products come into contact with gluten-containing products during manufacturing. This can happen through shared equipment, storage facilities, or even during packaging. For individuals with severe gluten sensitivities or celiac disease, even trace amounts of gluten can be harmful.

Bull's Eye BBQ Sauce is manufactured by Kraft Heinz, a company that has a reputation for transparency regarding allergen information. According to Kraft Heinz, Bull's Eye BBQ Sauce is produced in facilities that also handle gluten-containing ingredients. However, the company follows strict cleaning protocols to minimize the risk of cross-contamination.

Despite these precautions, the risk of cross-contamination cannot be entirely ruled out. Therefore, individuals with celiac disease or severe gluten sensitivities should exercise caution and consider reaching out to the manufacturer for more detailed information.

Certifications and Labeling
