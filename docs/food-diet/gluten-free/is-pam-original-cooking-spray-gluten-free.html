Is PAM Original Cooking Spray Gluten-Free? A Detailed Guide

When it comes to cooking, especially for those with dietary restrictions, knowing the ingredients in your kitchen staples is crucial. Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to severe health issues, including digestive problems, nutrient deficiencies, and long-term damage to the small intestine. Therefore, it’s essential for those with gluten-related disorders to ensure that the products they use are free from gluten contamination.

What is PAM Original Cooking Spray?

PAM Original Cooking Spray is a popular kitchen product used to prevent food from sticking to cookware. It’s made from a blend of oils, lecithin (an emulsifier), and propellant. The spray is known for its convenience and effectiveness in reducing the need for additional fats like butter or oil.

Manufacturing Process and Cross-Contamination

Even if the ingredients themselves are gluten-free, cross-contamination during manufacturing can introduce gluten into the product. PAM is produced by Conagra Brands, a company that takes food safety seriously. According to Conagra Brands, PAM Original Cooking Spray is manufactured in facilities that handle gluten-containing products, but they have strict protocols in place to prevent cross-contamination.

Conagra Brands also states that PAM Original Cooking Spray is labeled as gluten-free, which means it meets the FDA’s standards for gluten-free products. The FDA requires that products labeled as gluten-free contain less than 20 parts per million (ppm) of gluten, a level considered safe for most people with celiac disease.
