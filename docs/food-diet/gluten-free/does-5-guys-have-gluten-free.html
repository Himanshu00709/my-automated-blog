Does Five Guys Have Gluten-Free Options? A Detailed Guide

Five Guys is a popular fast-food chain known for its delicious burgers, fries, and customizable menu. However, for individuals with gluten intolerance or celiac disease, dining out can be a challenge. The question often arises: Does Five Guys have gluten-free options? In this article, we’ll explore the gluten-free offerings at Five Guys, how they handle cross-contamination, and what you need to know before ordering.

Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. For people with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to severe health issues, including digestive problems, nutrient deficiencies, and long-term damage to the small intestine. As a result, many individuals must adhere to a strict gluten-free diet.

When dining out, it’s crucial to ensure that the food is not only free from gluten-containing ingredients but also prepared in a way that minimizes the risk of cross-contamination. This is especially important in fast-food restaurants, where shared cooking surfaces and utensils are common.

Five Guys’ Gluten-Free Menu Options

Five Guys is known for its simple menu, which primarily consists of burgers, hot dogs, fries, and milkshakes. While the restaurant does not have a dedicated gluten-free menu, there are several items that can be customized to fit a gluten-free diet.

Burgers and Hot Dogs

Five Guys offers lettuce wraps as an alternative to traditional buns, making their burgers and hot dogs a viable option for those avoiding gluten. However, it’s important to note that the patties and hot dogs themselves are gluten-free, but cross-contamination can occur during preparation. Always inform the staff of your dietary restrictions to ensure they take extra precautions.

Fries

One of the highlights of Five Guys’ menu is their fries. The fries are cooked in 100% peanut oil and are naturally gluten-free. However, they are prepared in the same fryers as other items that may contain gluten, such as the Cajun seasoning. If you have a severe gluten allergy, it’s best to avoid the fries or ask the staff about their preparation process.

Milkshakes

Five Guys’ milkshakes are another gluten-free option. Made with real ice cream and a variety of mix-ins, these shakes are a safe choice for those avoiding gluten. Just be sure to avoid any mix-ins that may contain gluten, such as certain candies or cookies.

Cross-Contamination Concerns

While Five Guys does offer some gluten-free options, cross-contamination is a significant concern. The restaurant’s open kitchen layout means that gluten-containing ingredients are often handled near gluten-free items. Additionally, shared cooking surfaces and utensils increase the risk of cross-contact.

If you have celiac disease or a severe gluten allergy, it’s essential to communicate your dietary needs to the staff. While Five Guys cannot guarantee a completely gluten-free environment, they can take steps to minimize the risk of cross-contamination, such as using separate utensils and preparation areas.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Options at Five Guys

1. Are Five Guys fries gluten-free?

Yes, Five Guys fries are naturally gluten-free as they are cooked in 100% peanut oil. However, they are prepared in shared fryers, which may lead to cross-contamination.

2. Can I get a gluten-free bun at Five Guys?

Five Guys does not offer gluten-free buns. However, you can opt for a lettuce wrap as a gluten-free alternative.

3. Is Five Guys safe for people with celiac disease?

While Five Guys offers some gluten-free options, the risk of cross-contamination is high. Individuals with celiac disease should exercise caution and communicate their dietary needs to the staff.

4. Are Five Guys milkshakes gluten-free?

Yes, Five Guys milkshakes are gluten-free. However, be cautious with mix-ins, as some may contain gluten.

Conclusion

Five Guys does offer some gluten-free options, including lettuce-wrapped burgers and hot dogs, fries, and milkshakes. However, the risk of cross-contamination is a significant concern, especially for individuals with celiac disease or severe gluten allergies. If you’re considering dining at Five Guys, it’s essential to communicate your dietary needs to the staff and make informed choices about what you order. While Five Guys may not be the safest option for those with strict gluten-free requirements, it can be a viable choice for those with mild gluten sensitivity.
