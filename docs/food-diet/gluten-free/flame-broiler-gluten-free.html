What is Flame Broiler?

Flame Broiler is a fast-casual restaurant chain that specializes in healthy, Asian-inspired cuisine. Founded in 1995, the restaurant has gained a loyal following for its simple yet flavorful menu, which primarily consists of rice bowls, noodle bowls, and salads. The concept is straightforward: you choose your base (rice, noodles, or salad), add your protein (chicken, beef, or tofu), and top it off with a variety of sauces and vegetables. The result is a customizable, nutritious meal that caters to a wide range of dietary preferences.

For those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, the question of whether Flame Broiler is gluten-free is of utmost importance. The good news is that Flame Broiler offers several gluten-free options, making it a viable choice for those on a gluten-free diet. The noodles at Flame Broiler are made from wheat, which contains gluten. Additionally, the teriyaki sauce and miso soup also contain gluten. If you’re gluten-free, it’s best to steer clear of these items to avoid any adverse reactions.

Cross-Contamination Risks

One of the biggest concerns for individuals with celiac disease or severe gluten sensitivity is cross-contamination. Even if a menu item is inherently gluten-free, it can still come into contact with gluten during preparation. At Flame Broiler, the risk of cross-contamination is relatively low, but it’s not entirely eliminated. Here are some steps you can take to minimize the risk:

  • Inform the Staff: Let the staff know about your gluten-free dietary needs. They can take extra precautions, such as using clean utensils and preparing your meal separately.
  • Choose Simple Bowls: Opt for bowls with fewer ingredients to reduce the risk of cross-contamination.
  • Avoid Shared Fryers: If you’re ordering any fried items, make sure they’re not cooked in the same fryer as gluten-containing foods.
