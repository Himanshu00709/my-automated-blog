In recent years, the demand for gluten-free products has skyrocketed, driven by the increasing awareness of celiac disease and gluten sensitivity. Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and their derivatives. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to more severe health complications. Therefore, it is crucial for these individuals to carefully scrutinize the ingredients and manufacturing processes of the foods they consume.

Manufacturing Process and Cross-Contamination

Even if the ingredients in a product are gluten-free, cross-contamination during manufacturing can pose a risk. Cross-contamination occurs when gluten-free products come into contact with gluten-containing products or surfaces. This can happen during the production, packaging, or transportation of the food.

Jose Ole Taquitos are produced in facilities that may also process gluten-containing products. This increases the risk of cross-contamination. While the company may take precautions to minimize this risk, it is not always possible to eliminate it entirely. Therefore, individuals with severe gluten sensitivity or celiac disease should exercise caution when consuming products like Jose Ole Taquitos.

Labeling and Certification

One way to determine whether a product is gluten-free is to look for gluten-free labeling or certification. In the United States, the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) has established guidelines for gluten-free labeling. According to the FDA, a product labeled as "gluten-free" must contain less than 20 parts per million (ppm) of gluten.

As of the time of writing, Jose Ole Taquitos do not carry a gluten-free label. This absence of labeling suggests that the product may not meet the FDA's criteria for gluten-free certification. However, it is always a good idea to check the packaging for any updates or changes in labeling.
