Understanding Gluten and Gluten-Free Diets

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to more severe health complications. As a result, many people opt for gluten-free diets to avoid these adverse effects.

Gluten-free diets exclude all foods and beverages that contain gluten. This includes not only obvious sources like bread and pasta but also less obvious ones like certain alcoholic beverages. Therefore, it's crucial for those on a gluten-free diet to carefully scrutinize the ingredients of any product they consume, including alcoholic drinks like Nutrl.

Is Nutrl Gluten-Free?

The short answer is yes, Nutrl is gluten-free. Nutrl is made from vodka, which is typically distilled from grains like wheat or barley. However, the distillation process removes the gluten proteins, making the final product gluten-free. Additionally, Nutrl is certified gluten-free by relevant authorities, providing an extra layer of assurance for consumers.

It's important to note that while Nutrl is gluten-free, cross-contamination can occur during the manufacturing process. However, Nutrl takes stringent measures to prevent this, ensuring that their product remains safe for those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease.
