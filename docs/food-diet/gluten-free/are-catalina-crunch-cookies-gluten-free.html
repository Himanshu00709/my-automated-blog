Are Catalina Crunch Cookies Gluten-Free? A Comprehensive Guide

In recent years, the demand for gluten-free products has skyrocketed, driven by the increasing awareness of gluten-related health issues such as celiac disease, gluten sensitivity, and wheat allergies. For those who follow a gluten-free diet, finding delicious and safe snacks can be a challenge. Enter Catalina Crunch cookies—a popular snack that has gained a loyal following for its unique taste and health benefits. But are Catalina Crunch cookies gluten-free? In this article, we’ll dive deep into the ingredients, manufacturing process, and nutritional profile of Catalina Crunch cookies to answer this question and more.

What Are Catalina Crunch Cookies?

Catalina Crunch cookies are a line of keto-friendly, low-carb snacks designed to cater to health-conscious consumers. These cookies are known for their crunchy texture and rich flavors, making them a favorite among those following ketogenic, low-carb, or gluten-free diets. The brand prides itself on using high-quality, plant-based ingredients that are free from artificial additives, making their products a healthier alternative to traditional cookies.

Are Catalina Crunch Cookies Gluten-Free?

Yes, Catalina Crunch cookies are gluten-free. The brand explicitly states that all their products, including their cookies, are made without gluten-containing ingredients. This makes them a safe option for individuals with celiac disease, gluten sensitivity, or those who simply prefer to avoid gluten in their diet.

Catalina Crunch cookies are made with a blend of plant-based proteins, fiber, and natural sweeteners. The primary ingredients include pea protein, potato fiber, and chicory root fiber, all of which are naturally gluten-free. Additionally, the cookies are sweetened with monk fruit extract, which is also gluten-free and low in calories.

Manufacturing Process and Cross-Contamination

While the ingredients themselves are gluten-free, it’s also important to consider the manufacturing process. Cross-contamination can occur if gluten-free products are produced in facilities that also handle gluten-containing ingredients. Catalina Crunch takes this issue seriously and has implemented strict manufacturing protocols to prevent cross-contamination.

The brand states that their cookies are produced in a dedicated gluten-free facility, which significantly reduces the risk of cross-contamination. This is a crucial factor for individuals with celiac disease, as even trace amounts of gluten can trigger adverse reactions.
