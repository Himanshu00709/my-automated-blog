Understanding Gluten and Gluten-Free Diets

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease, consuming gluten triggers an immune response that damages the small intestine, leading to a range of symptoms and long-term health complications. Even for those with non-celiac gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can cause digestive discomfort, fatigue, and other symptoms. As a result, many people opt for gluten-free diets to avoid these issues.

Gluten-free products are those that contain less than 20 parts per million (ppm) of gluten, as defined by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA). This threshold is considered safe for most individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity.

Ingredients in Royo Bread

Royo Bread is made with a blend of alternative flours, including almond flour, coconut flour, and flaxseed meal. Manufacturing Process

One of the key factors in determining whether a product is truly gluten-free is the manufacturing process. Cross-contamination can occur if gluten-free products are produced in facilities that also process gluten-containing ingredients. Royo Bread claims to be produced in a dedicated gluten-free facility, which significantly reduces the risk of cross-contamination. However, it’s always a good idea to check the packaging for any disclaimers or certifications, such as the "Certified Gluten-Free" label from organizations like the Gluten-Free Certification Organization (GFCO).
