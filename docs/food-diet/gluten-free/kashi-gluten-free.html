What is Gluten and Why Go Gluten-Free?

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For most people, gluten is harmless, but for those with celiac disease, gluten sensitivity, or wheat allergies, consuming gluten can lead to serious health issues. Symptoms can range from digestive problems and fatigue to more severe conditions like nutrient deficiencies and autoimmune responses. As a result, many individuals are turning to gluten-free diets to manage their health and improve their overall well-being.

FAQs About Kashi Gluten-Free Products

1. Are all Kashi products gluten-free?

No, not all Kashi products are gluten-free. However, Kashi offers a dedicated line of gluten-free products that are clearly labeled. Always check the packaging to ensure the product is gluten-free.

2. Are Kashi gluten-free products suitable for people with celiac disease?

Yes, Kashi gluten-free products are certified gluten-free and are suitable for individuals with celiac disease. However, it's always a good idea to consult with a healthcare provider before making any dietary changes.

3. Where can I buy Kashi gluten-free products?

Kashi gluten-free products are widely available in grocery stores, health food stores, and online retailers. You can also purchase them directly from the Kashi website.

4. Do Kashi gluten-free products contain dairy?

Some Kashi gluten-free products may contain dairy or be processed in facilities that handle dairy. If you have a dairy allergy, be sure to read the ingredient list carefully.

5. Are Kashi gluten-free products vegan?

Many Kashi gluten-free products are vegan, but not all. Check the ingredient list to ensure the product meets your dietary preferences.
