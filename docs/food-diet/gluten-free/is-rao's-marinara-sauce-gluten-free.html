Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to severe health issues, including digestive problems, nutrient malabsorption, and even long-term damage to the small intestine. Therefore, it's crucial for these individuals to ensure that the foods they consume are free from gluten.

When evaluating whether a product like Rao's Marinara Sauce is gluten-free, it's important to consider not only the ingredients but also the potential for cross-contamination during manufacturing.

Manufacturing Process and Cross-Contamination

Rao's Homemade takes great care in its manufacturing process to prevent cross-contamination with gluten-containing products. The company states that their marinara sauce is produced in a facility that does not process wheat, barley, or rye, which significantly reduces the risk of gluten contamination.

Additionally, Rao's Homemade Marinara Sauce is certified gluten-free by the Gluten-Free Certification Organization (GFCO). This certification requires that the product contains less than 10 parts per million (ppm) of gluten, which is the threshold considered safe for individuals with celiac disease.
