The Rise of Gluten-Free Diets

The gluten-free diet has gained significant traction over the past decade, with more people recognizing the health benefits associated with reducing or eliminating gluten from their diets. Gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, can cause adverse reactions in individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. Symptoms can range from digestive issues to skin problems, fatigue, and even neurological symptoms. For these individuals, a gluten-free diet is not just a lifestyle choice but a necessity for maintaining good health.
