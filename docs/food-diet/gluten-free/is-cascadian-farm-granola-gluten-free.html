Understanding Gluten and Its Impact on Health

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For most people, gluten is harmless, but for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, it can cause a range of symptoms, including digestive issues, fatigue, and skin problems. Celiac disease is an autoimmune disorder where the ingestion of gluten leads to damage in the small intestine. Non-celiac gluten sensitivity, on the other hand, causes similar symptoms but without the intestinal damage. For these individuals, avoiding gluten is crucial for maintaining their health.

What Makes Granola Gluten-Free?

Traditional granola is made with oats, which are naturally gluten-free. However, oats are often contaminated with gluten during processing because they are frequently grown, harvested, and processed alongside gluten-containing grains like wheat and barley. To be considered gluten-free, granola must be made with oats that are certified gluten-free, meaning they have been processed in a facility that avoids cross-contamination with gluten-containing grains.

In addition to oats, granola may contain other ingredients like nuts, seeds, dried fruits, and sweeteners. These ingredients must also be free from gluten contamination. Therefore, when choosing a gluten-free granola, it’s important to look for products that are certified gluten-free by a reputable organization.
