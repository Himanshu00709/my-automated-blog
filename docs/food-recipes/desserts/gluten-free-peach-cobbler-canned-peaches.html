Gluten-Free Peach Cobbler with Canned Peaches

Peach cobbler is a classic dessert that brings warmth and comfort to any table. For those who follow a gluten-free diet, enjoying this delicious treat might seem challenging. However, with the right ingredients and a few simple steps, you can create a gluten-free peach cobbler that is just as delightful as the traditional version. This recipe uses canned peaches, making it convenient and accessible year-round. Let’s dive into the details of how to make this scrumptious dessert.

Why Choose Gluten-Free Peach Cobbler?

Gluten-free diets are essential for individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. Even if you don’t have these conditions, reducing gluten intake can sometimes lead to better digestion and overall health. This gluten-free peach cobbler recipe ensures that everyone can enjoy a classic dessert without compromising on taste or texture. Using canned peaches not only saves time but also provides a consistent flavor, making it a reliable choice for any season.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make this gluten-free peach cobbler, you’ll need the following ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity
Canned peaches (in syrup or juice) 2 cans (15 oz each)
Gluten-free flour blend 1 cup
Granulated sugar 1 cup
Baking powder 1 tsp
Salt 1/4 tsp
Unsalted butter 1/2 cup (1 stick)
Milk (or non-dairy alternative) 1/2 cup
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Ground cinnamon 1/2 tsp

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). This ensures that your cobbler bakes evenly and achieves that golden-brown crust we all love.

Step 2: Prepare the Peaches

Drain the canned peaches, reserving about 1/4 cup of the syrup or juice. Place the peaches in a baking dish and sprinkle them with a little sugar and cinnamon to enhance their natural sweetness.

Step 3: Make the Cobbler Topping

In a mixing bowl, combine the gluten-free flour blend, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Cut in the cold butter using a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Stir in the milk and vanilla extract until just combined. The batter should be thick but pourable.

Step 4: Assemble and Bake

Pour the batter over the peaches in the baking dish, spreading it evenly. Bake in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Allow the cobbler to cool slightly before serving. It pairs wonderfully with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream for an extra indulgent treat.

Tips for the Perfect Gluten-Free Peach Cobbler

  • Choose the Right Flour: Not all gluten-free flours are created equal. Look for a blend that is specifically designed for baking to ensure the best texture.
  • Adjust Sweetness: If your canned peaches are already sweetened, you may want to reduce the amount of sugar in the topping to balance the flavors.
  • Experiment with Spices: While cinnamon is a classic choice, you can also try adding a pinch of nutmeg or cardamom for a unique twist.

FAQs

Can I use fresh peaches instead of canned?

Yes, you can use fresh peaches if they are in season. Simply peel and slice the peaches, then cook them with a bit of sugar and water until they soften before using them in the recipe.

Can I make this recipe dairy-free?

Absolutely! Substitute the butter with a dairy-free alternative like coconut oil or margarine, and use a non-dairy milk such as almond or oat milk.

How should I store leftovers?

Store any leftover cobbler in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven or microwave before serving.

Can I freeze the cobbler?

Yes, you can freeze the baked cobbler for up to 2 months. Wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil before freezing. Thaw in the refrigerator overnight and reheat in the oven.
