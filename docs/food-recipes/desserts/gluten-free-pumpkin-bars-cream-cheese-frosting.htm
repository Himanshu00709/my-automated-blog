Gluten-Free Pumpkin Bars with Cream Cheese Frosting

As the leaves begin to change and the air turns crisp, there's no better way to embrace the flavors of fall than with a batch of gluten-free pumpkin bars topped with creamy, tangy cream cheese frosting. These bars are not only delicious but also cater to those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease. Whether you're hosting a fall gathering or simply craving a seasonal treat, these pumpkin bars are sure to delight.

Why Gluten-Free Pumpkin Bars?

Gluten-free baking has come a long way, and with the right ingredients, you can create desserts that are just as satisfying as their gluten-filled counterparts. Pumpkin bars are a classic fall dessert, and making them gluten-free allows everyone to enjoy this seasonal favorite. The combination of warm spices, moist pumpkin, and rich cream cheese frosting is simply irresistible.

Ingredients You'll Need

Before diving into the recipe, let's take a look at the ingredients you'll need to make these gluten-free pumpkin bars:

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free flour blend 1 ½ cups
Baking powder 1 teaspoon
Baking soda ½ teaspoon
Salt ¼ teaspoon
Ground cinnamon 1 teaspoon
Ground nutmeg ½ teaspoon
Ground cloves ¼ teaspoon
Pumpkin puree 1 cup
Granulated sugar 1 cup
Brown sugar ½ cup
Eggs 2 large
Vegetable oil ½ cup
Vanilla extract 1 teaspoon

For the Cream Cheese Frosting:

Ingredient Quantity
Cream cheese 8 ounces (softened)
Unsalted butter ½ cup (softened)
Powdered sugar 2 cups
Vanilla extract 1 teaspoon

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Preheat and Prepare

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13 inch baking pan and set it aside. This will ensure that your pumpkin bars don't stick to the pan and come out easily once baked.

2. Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour blend, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves. This ensures that all the dry ingredients are evenly distributed, which is crucial for a consistent texture in your bars.

3. Combine the Wet Ingredients

In a separate large bowl, mix together the pumpkin puree, granulated sugar, brown sugar, eggs, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract. Whisk until the mixture is smooth and well combined. The pumpkin puree adds moisture and a rich, autumnal flavor to the bars.

4. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can lead to dense bars. The batter should be thick but pourable.

5. Bake the Bars

Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan and spread it evenly. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. The bars should be golden brown and springy to the touch.

6. Prepare the Cream Cheese Frosting

While the bars are cooling, prepare the cream cheese frosting. In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and butter together until smooth. Gradually add the powdered sugar and vanilla extract, beating until the frosting is creamy and spreadable.

7. Frost and Serve

Once the bars have completely cooled, spread the cream cheese frosting evenly over the top. Cut into squares and serve. These bars can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 5 days, but they're best enjoyed fresh!

FAQs

Can I use regular flour instead of gluten-free flour?

Yes, you can use regular all-purpose flour if you don't need the bars to be gluten-free. Simply substitute the gluten-free flour blend with an equal amount of all-purpose flour.

Can I make these bars ahead of time?

Absolutely! These bars can be made a day in advance. Store them in an airtight container in the refrigerator and bring them to room temperature before serving.

Can I freeze these pumpkin bars?

Yes, you can freeze these bars. Wrap them tightly in plastic wrap and then in aluminum foil. They can be frozen for up to 3 months. Thaw in the refrigerator before serving.

What can I use instead of cream cheese frosting?

If you prefer a different topping, you can use a simple glaze made with powdered sugar and milk, or even a dollop of whipped cream. However, the cream cheese frosting pairs perfectly with the pumpkin flavor.
