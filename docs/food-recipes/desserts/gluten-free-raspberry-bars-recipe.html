Gluten-Free Raspberry Bars Recipe

Gluten-free baking has become increasingly popular, and for good reason. Whether you have celiac disease, a gluten intolerance, or simply want to explore healthier baking options, gluten-free recipes can be both delicious and nutritious. Today, we’re diving into a delightful recipe for gluten-free raspberry bars. These bars are not only easy to make but also packed with flavor, making them the perfect treat for any occasion.

Why Choose Gluten-Free Raspberry Bars?

Gluten-free raspberry bars are a fantastic option for those who need to avoid gluten but still want to enjoy a sweet treat. The combination of a buttery crust, tangy raspberry filling, and a crumbly topping makes these bars irresistible. Plus, they’re made with wholesome ingredients that are free from gluten, making them a healthier alternative to traditional desserts.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before we get started, let’s take a look at the ingredients you’ll need to make these delicious gluten-free raspberry bars:

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free all-purpose flour 2 cups
Almond flour 1 cup
Unsalted butter (cold) 1 cup
Granulated sugar 1/2 cup
Brown sugar 1/2 cup
Salt 1/4 teaspoon
Raspberry jam 1 cup
Fresh raspberries 1 cup
Vanilla extract 1 teaspoon

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line an 8x8-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving some overhang on the sides for easy removal later.

Step 2: Prepare the Crust

In a large mixing bowl, combine the gluten-free all-purpose flour, almond flour, granulated sugar, brown sugar, and salt. Cut the cold butter into small cubes and add it to the dry ingredients. Use a pastry cutter or your fingers to mix the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse crumbs.

Step 3: Press the Crust into the Pan

Reserve about 1 cup of the crumb mixture for the topping. Press the remaining mixture evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking pan. Use the back of a spoon or your fingers to press it down firmly.

Step 4: Add the Raspberry Filling

In a small bowl, mix the raspberry jam and fresh raspberries together. Spread this mixture evenly over the crust in the pan.

Step 5: Add the Topping

Sprinkle the reserved crumb mixture evenly over the raspberry filling. Press it down lightly to ensure it sticks to the filling.

Step 6: Bake the Bars

Bake in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the filling is bubbly. Remove from the oven and let the bars cool completely in the pan before cutting into squares.

Tips for Perfect Gluten-Free Raspberry Bars

  • Use Cold Butter: Cold butter is essential for achieving a crumbly texture in the crust and topping. If the butter is too soft, the mixture will become too dense.
  • Don’t Overmix: When combining the butter with the dry ingredients, be careful not to overmix. You want the mixture to remain crumbly, not form a dough.
  • Let the Bars Cool: It’s important to let the bars cool completely before cutting them. This will help them set and make them easier to slice.

FAQs

Can I use frozen raspberries instead of fresh?

Yes, you can use frozen raspberries if fresh ones are not available. Just make sure to thaw and drain them well before using to avoid excess moisture in the filling.

Can I substitute the almond flour?

If you have a nut allergy, you can substitute almond flour with an equal amount of gluten-free oat flour or additional gluten-free all-purpose flour.

How should I store these bars?

Store the gluten-free raspberry bars in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or in the refrigerator for up to a week. They can also be frozen for up to 3 months.

Can I use a different type of jam?

Absolutely! While raspberry jam is recommended for this recipe, you can experiment with other flavors like strawberry, blackberry, or even apricot jam.

Conclusion

With a buttery crust, tangy raspberry filling, and a crumbly topping, these bars are sure to become a favorite in your household. They’re easy to make, packed with flavor, and perfect for any occasion. Whether you’re serving them at a party or enjoying them as a sweet snack, these gluten-free raspberry bars are a must-try. Happy baking!
