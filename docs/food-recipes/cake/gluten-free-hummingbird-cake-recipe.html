Gluten-Free Hummingbird Cake Recipe

Hummingbird Cake is a classic Southern dessert that combines the flavors of banana, pineapple, and pecans into a moist and delicious cake. Traditionally, this cake is made with all-purpose flour, but for those who follow a gluten-free diet, this recipe offers a delightful alternative. In this blog post, we’ll walk you through a step-by-step guide to creating a gluten-free Hummingbird Cake that’s just as flavorful and moist as the original.

Why Choose a Gluten-Free Hummingbird Cake?

Gluten-free baking has come a long way, and with the right ingredients, you can create cakes that are just as delicious as their gluten-containing counterparts. Whether you have celiac disease, a gluten sensitivity, or simply want to explore gluten-free options, this recipe is perfect for you. The combination of ripe bananas, crushed pineapple, and toasted pecans ensures that this cake is bursting with flavor, while gluten-free flour blends provide the perfect texture.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before you start baking, make sure you have all the necessary ingredients. Here’s a list of what you’ll need:

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free all-purpose flour 3 cups
Baking soda 1 teaspoon
Salt 1/2 teaspoon
Ground cinnamon 1 teaspoon
Ripe bananas (mashed) 3 medium
Crushed pineapple (with juice) 1 cup
Vegetable oil 1 cup
Granulated sugar 1 1/2 cups
Large eggs 3
Vanilla extract 1 teaspoon
Chopped pecans 1 cup

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preheat and Prepare

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans, or line them with parchment paper for easy removal.

Step 2: Mix Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour, baking soda, salt, and ground cinnamon. Set this mixture aside.

Step 3: Combine Wet Ingredients

In another bowl, combine the mashed bananas, crushed pineapple (with juice), vegetable oil, granulated sugar, eggs, and vanilla extract. Mix until well combined.

Step 4: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix. Fold in the chopped pecans.

Step 5: Bake the Cake

Divide the batter evenly between the prepared cake pans. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Step 6: Cool and Frost

Allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely. Once cooled, frost with your favorite cream cheese frosting or a simple glaze.

Tips for the Perfect Gluten-Free Hummingbird Cake

Here are some tips to ensure your gluten-free Hummingbird Cake turns out perfectly:

  • Use a high-quality gluten-free flour blend: Not all gluten-free flours are created equal. Look for a blend that includes xanthan gum, which helps mimic the texture of gluten.
  • Don’t overmix the batter: Overmixing can lead to a dense cake. Mix until just combined.
  • Toast the pecans: Toasting the pecans before adding them to the batter enhances their flavor.
  • Let the cake cool completely before frosting: This prevents the frosting from melting and sliding off the cake.

FAQs

Can I make this cake dairy-free as well?

Yes, you can make this cake dairy-free by using a dairy-free cream cheese frosting or a simple glaze made with powdered sugar and a dairy-free milk alternative.

Can I use a different type of nut?

Absolutely! If you’re not a fan of pecans, you can substitute them with walnuts or even omit the nuts altogether.

How should I store the cake?

Store the cake in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days, or in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. If refrigerated, let the cake come to room temperature before serving for the best texture.

Can I freeze the cake?

Yes, you can freeze the unfrosted cake layers. Wrap them tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil, and freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw at room temperature before frosting and serving.
