Gluten-Free Cornbread Recipe with Creamed Corn

Cornbread is a classic comfort food that pairs perfectly with soups, stews, or even as a standalone snack. For those who follow a gluten-free diet, finding a delicious and moist cornbread recipe can be a challenge. This gluten-free cornbread recipe with creamed corn is not only easy to make but also incredibly flavorful and moist. The addition of creamed corn gives it a rich texture and a hint of sweetness that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

Why Choose Gluten-Free Cornbread?

Gluten-free diets are essential for individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. Even if you don't have these conditions, reducing gluten intake can sometimes lead to better digestion and overall health. This recipe ensures that you don't have to compromise on taste or texture while sticking to a gluten-free lifestyle.

Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need to make this delicious gluten-free cornbread:

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free cornmeal 1 cup
Gluten-free all-purpose flour 1 cup
Baking powder 1 tablespoon
Salt 1/2 teaspoon
Eggs 2 large
Milk (or dairy-free alternative) 1 cup
Unsalted butter (melted) 1/4 cup
Creamed corn 1 cup
Honey or sugar 1/4 cup

Instructions

Follow these simple steps to make your gluten-free cornbread:

  1. Preheat the oven: Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a 9-inch square baking pan or a cast-iron skillet with butter or cooking spray.
  2. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the gluten-free cornmeal, gluten-free all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt until well combined.
  3. Combine wet ingredients: In a separate bowl, beat the eggs, then add the milk, melted butter, and honey (or sugar). Mix until smooth.
  4. Combine wet and dry ingredients: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix. Fold in the creamed corn gently.
  5. Bake: Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan or skillet. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. Cool and serve: Allow the cornbread to cool for about 10 minutes before slicing and serving. Enjoy it warm with a pat of butter or a drizzle of honey.

Tips for Perfect Gluten-Free Cornbread

  • Use fresh ingredients: Fresh baking powder and high-quality gluten-free flour can make a big difference in the texture of your cornbread.
  • Don’t overmix: Overmixing the batter can lead to a dense cornbread. Mix until just combined for the best results.
  • Adjust sweetness: If you prefer a sweeter cornbread, you can increase the amount of honey or sugar to your liking.
  • Experiment with add-ins: Feel free to add shredded cheese, jalapeños, or herbs to customize your cornbread.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Can I use regular cornmeal instead of gluten-free cornmeal?

Yes, you can use regular cornmeal if you don’t have a gluten intolerance. However, ensure that all other ingredients are gluten-free if you’re catering to someone with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity.

Can I make this recipe dairy-free?

Absolutely! You can substitute the milk with a dairy-free alternative like almond milk or oat milk, and use a dairy-free butter substitute.

How do I store leftover cornbread?

Store leftover cornbread in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days, or in the refrigerator for up to a week. You can also freeze it for up to 3 months.

Can I make this cornbread in a muffin tin?

Yes, you can make cornbread muffins by pouring the batter into a greased muffin tin. Reduce the baking time to 15-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
