Gluten-Free Cornbread Recipe with Creamed Corn

Cornbread is a classic comfort food that pairs perfectly with soups, stews, or even as a standalone snack. For those who follow a gluten-free diet, finding a delicious and moist cornbread recipe can be a challenge. This gluten-free cornbread recipe with creamed corn is not only easy to make but also incredibly flavorful and moist. The addition of creamed corn adds a delightful sweetness and texture that will have everyone coming back for seconds.

Why Choose Gluten-Free Cornbread?

Gluten-free diets are essential for individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. However, even those without these conditions often choose gluten-free options for their potential health benefits. Traditional cornbread recipes often include wheat flour, which contains gluten. By using gluten-free flour alternatives, you can enjoy the same delicious cornbread without the adverse effects of gluten.

Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need to make this gluten-free cornbread with creamed corn:

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free cornmeal 1 cup
Gluten-free all-purpose flour 1 cup
Baking powder 1 tablespoon
Salt 1/2 teaspoon
Granulated sugar 1/4 cup
Eggs 2 large
Milk (or dairy-free alternative) 1 cup
Unsalted butter (melted) 1/4 cup
Creamed corn 1 cup

Instructions

Follow these simple steps to make your gluten-free cornbread:

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease an 8-inch square baking pan.
  2. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the gluten-free cornmeal, gluten-free all-purpose flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar.
  3. Combine wet ingredients: In another bowl, beat the eggs, then add the milk and melted butter. Mix well.
  4. Combine wet and dry ingredients: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Do not overmix.
  5. Add creamed corn: Gently fold in the creamed corn until evenly distributed throughout the batter.
  6. Bake: Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan and smooth the top. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Cool and serve: Allow the cornbread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Tips for Perfect Gluten-Free Cornbread

  • Use fresh ingredients: Ensure that your baking powder and gluten-free flour are fresh for the best results.
  • Do not overmix: Overmixing the batter can lead to dense cornbread. Mix until just combined.
  • Adjust sweetness: If you prefer a sweeter cornbread, you can increase the sugar to 1/2 cup.
  • Experiment with add-ins: Feel free to add shredded cheese, jalapeños, or herbs for a savory twist.

FAQs

Can I use regular cornmeal instead of gluten-free cornmeal?

Yes, you can use regular cornmeal if you do not have a gluten intolerance. However, ensure that the cornmeal is labeled gluten-free if you are making this recipe for someone with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity.

Can I make this recipe dairy-free?

Absolutely! You can substitute the milk with a dairy-free alternative like almond milk or oat milk, and use a dairy-free butter substitute.

How should I store leftover cornbread?

Store leftover cornbread in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days, or in the refrigerator for up to a week. You can also freeze it for up to 3 months.

Can I use frozen creamed corn?

Yes, you can use frozen creamed corn. Just make sure to thaw it completely before adding it to the batter.

Conclusion

This gluten-free cornbread recipe with creamed corn is a must-try for anyone looking to enjoy a classic comfort food without the gluten. The addition of creamed corn ensures that the cornbread is moist, flavorful, and slightly sweet. Whether you’re serving it alongside a hearty chili or enjoying it on its own, this cornbread is sure to be a hit. Give it a try and enjoy the delicious results!
