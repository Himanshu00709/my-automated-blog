Gluten-Free Banana Bread by Jamie Oliver

Banana bread is a timeless classic, loved by many for its moist texture and sweet flavor. When it comes to making a gluten-free version, Jamie Oliver has perfected the art. His recipe is not only delicious but also caters to those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease. In this blog post, we’ll dive into the details of Jamie Oliver’s gluten-free banana bread, exploring the ingredients, step-by-step instructions, and tips to ensure your loaf turns out perfectly every time.

Why Choose Gluten-Free Banana Bread?

Gluten-free baking has become increasingly popular, not just among those with gluten intolerances but also among health-conscious individuals. Gluten-free banana bread offers a lighter, more digestible alternative to traditional recipes. Jamie Oliver’s version uses a blend of gluten-free flours to achieve the perfect texture, ensuring that you don’t miss out on the classic banana bread experience.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make Jamie Oliver’s gluten-free banana bread, you’ll need the following ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity
Ripe bananas 3 medium-sized
Gluten-free flour blend 200g
Almond flour 50g
Baking powder 1 tsp
Baking soda 1/2 tsp
Salt 1/4 tsp
Unsalted butter (or coconut oil) 100g
Brown sugar 100g
Eggs 2 large
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Walnuts or chocolate chips (optional) 50g

Step-by-Step Instructions

Follow these steps to create Jamie Oliver’s gluten-free banana bread:

  1. Preheat the oven: Start by preheating your oven to 180°C (350°F). Grease a loaf tin and line it with parchment paper.
  2. Mash the bananas: In a large bowl, mash the ripe bananas until smooth. The riper the bananas, the sweeter and more flavorful your bread will be.
  3. Mix the dry ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour blend, almond flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  4. Cream the butter and sugar: In another bowl, cream the butter (or coconut oil) and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  5. Combine wet and dry ingredients: Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, stirring until just combined. Fold in the mashed bananas and any optional add-ins like walnuts or chocolate chips.
  6. Bake: Pour the batter into the prepared loaf tin and smooth the top. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Cool and serve: Allow the banana bread to cool in the tin for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely. Slice and serve!

Tips for Perfect Gluten-Free Banana Bread

Here are some expert tips to ensure your gluten-free banana bread turns out perfectly:

  • Use ripe bananas: The riper the bananas, the sweeter and more flavorful your bread will be. Overripe bananas with brown spots are ideal.
  • Don’t overmix: Overmixing the batter can lead to a dense loaf. Mix until just combined to keep the bread light and fluffy.
  • Check for doneness: Oven temperatures can vary, so start checking your bread at the 50-minute mark. If the top is browning too quickly, cover it with foil.
  • Experiment with add-ins: Feel free to customize your banana bread with nuts, seeds, or chocolate chips for added texture and flavor.

FAQs

Can I use a different gluten-free flour blend?

Yes, you can use any gluten-free flour blend you prefer. However, the combination of gluten-free flour and almond flour in Jamie Oliver’s recipe provides the best texture and flavor.

Can I make this recipe vegan?

Absolutely! You can substitute the eggs with flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tbsp water per egg) and use coconut oil instead of butter.

How should I store the banana bread?

Store the banana bread in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, wrap it tightly and freeze for up to 3 months.

Can I add other fruits to the recipe?

While bananas are the star of this recipe, you can add other fruits like blueberries or diced apples for a twist. Just be mindful of the moisture content.
