Gluten-Free Brown Gravy Recipe: A Delicious and Healthy Alternative

Gravy is a staple in many households, especially during holiday meals or Sunday dinners. However, for those with gluten intolerance or celiac disease, traditional gravy can be off-limits due to the use of wheat flour as a thickening agent. Fortunately, creating a gluten-free brown gravy that is just as rich and flavorful is entirely possible. In this article, we’ll walk you through a simple yet delicious gluten-free brown gravy recipe, along with tips, FAQs, and more.

Why Choose Gluten-Free Gravy?

Gluten-free diets are essential for individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. Consuming gluten can lead to severe digestive issues, inflammation, and other health problems for these individuals. Even if you don’t have a gluten intolerance, opting for gluten-free alternatives can be a healthier choice, as it often involves using whole, unprocessed ingredients. This gluten-free brown gravy recipe uses natural thickeners like cornstarch or arrowroot powder, ensuring a smooth and flavorful result without compromising on taste.

Ingredients for Gluten-Free Brown Gravy

Here’s what you’ll need to make this delicious gluten-free brown gravy:

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free beef or chicken broth 2 cups
Cornstarch or arrowroot powder 2 tablespoons
Cold water 1/4 cup
Butter or olive oil 2 tablespoons
Onion powder 1/2 teaspoon
Garlic powder 1/2 teaspoon
Salt To taste
Black pepper To taste
Optional: Soy sauce or tamari (gluten-free) 1 teaspoon

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Thickener

In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch or arrowroot powder with cold water until it forms a smooth slurry. This will prevent lumps from forming in your gravy.

Step 2: Heat the Broth

In a medium-sized saucepan, heat the gluten-free beef or chicken broth over medium heat. Bring it to a gentle simmer.

Step 3: Add the Thickener

Slowly pour the cornstarch slurry into the simmering broth, stirring constantly. Continue to stir until the gravy thickens, which should take about 2-3 minutes.

Step 4: Season the Gravy

Add the butter or olive oil, onion powder, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper to the gravy. If you’re using soy sauce or tamari, add it at this stage as well. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.

Step 5: Simmer and Serve

Allow the gravy to simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed. Once the gravy reaches your desired consistency, remove it from the heat and serve immediately.

Tips for Perfect Gluten-Free Gravy

  • Use high-quality broth: The flavor of your gravy largely depends on the broth you use. Opt for a high-quality, gluten-free beef or chicken broth for the best results.
  • Adjust the thickness: If your gravy is too thick, you can thin it out by adding a bit more broth. If it’s too thin, mix another tablespoon of cornstarch with cold water and stir it into the gravy.
  • Experiment with flavors: Feel free to add herbs like thyme or rosemary, or a splash of red wine for added depth of flavor.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Brown Gravy

Can I use flour alternatives like almond flour or coconut flour?

While almond flour and coconut flour are popular gluten-free alternatives, they are not ideal for thickening gravy. Cornstarch or arrowroot powder works best for achieving a smooth and consistent texture.

Is this gravy recipe suitable for vegans?

Yes, you can make this gravy vegan by using olive oil instead of butter and ensuring that the broth and soy sauce are vegan-friendly.

How long can I store gluten-free gravy?

You can store leftover gravy in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat it on the stovetop or in the microwave, adding a bit of broth if it thickens too much.

Can I freeze gluten-free gravy?

Yes, gluten-free gravy freezes well. Pour it into a freezer-safe container and store it for up to 3 months. Thaw it in the refrigerator overnight before reheating.

Conclusion

Making gluten-free brown gravy is easier than you might think, and it’s a fantastic way to enjoy a classic comfort food without the gluten. With just a few simple ingredients and steps, you can create a rich, flavorful gravy that pairs perfectly with mashed potatoes, roasted meats, or your favorite gluten-free dishes. Whether you’re cooking for someone with dietary restrictions or simply exploring healthier alternatives, this recipe is sure to become a staple in your kitchen. Give it a try and savor the delicious results!
