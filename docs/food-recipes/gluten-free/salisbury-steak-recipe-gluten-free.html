Gluten-Free Salisbury Steak Recipe: A Comfort Food Classic

When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the hearty and satisfying Salisbury steak. Traditionally made with ground beef, breadcrumbs, and a rich gravy, this dish is a staple in many households. However, for those following a gluten-free diet, the classic recipe can be off-limits due to the presence of gluten in breadcrumbs and certain gravy thickeners. But fear not! With a few simple substitutions, you can enjoy a delicious, gluten-free Salisbury steak that’s just as comforting and flavorful as the original.

What is Salisbury Steak?

Salisbury steak is a dish made from ground beef that’s shaped into patties, seared, and then smothered in a rich onion gravy. It’s often served with mashed potatoes or vegetables, making it a complete and satisfying meal. The dish is named after Dr. James Salisbury, who promoted a meat-centric diet in the 19th century. While the original recipe included breadcrumbs, our gluten-free version uses almond flour and gluten-free breadcrumbs to achieve the same texture and flavor.

Ingredients for Gluten-Free Salisbury Steak

To make gluten-free Salisbury steak, you’ll need the following ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity
Ground beef 1 pound
Almond flour 1/4 cup
Gluten-free breadcrumbs 1/4 cup
Egg 1 large
Onion (finely chopped) 1/2 cup
Garlic (minced) 2 cloves
Worcestershire sauce (gluten-free) 1 tablespoon
Salt 1 teaspoon
Black pepper 1/2 teaspoon
Olive oil 2 tablespoons

Ingredients for Gluten-Free Gravy

For the gluten-free gravy, you’ll need:

Ingredient Quantity
Beef broth (gluten-free) 2 cups
Onion (sliced) 1 medium
Garlic (minced) 2 cloves
Cornstarch 2 tablespoons
Water 1/4 cup
Worcestershire sauce (gluten-free) 1 tablespoon
Salt 1/2 teaspoon
Black pepper 1/4 teaspoon

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Salisbury Steak Patties

In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef, almond flour, gluten-free breadcrumbs, egg, chopped onion, minced garlic, Worcestershire sauce, salt, and black pepper. Mix until all ingredients are well incorporated. Shape the mixture into 4-6 oval-shaped patties, depending on your preferred size.

2. Cook the Patties

Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the patties and cook for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until they are browned and cooked through. Remove the patties from the skillet and set them aside on a plate.

3. Prepare the Gravy

In the same skillet, add the sliced onion and minced garlic. Sauté for about 2-3 minutes until the onions are softened and fragrant. In a small bowl, whisk together the cornstarch and water until smooth. Pour the beef broth into the skillet, followed by the cornstarch mixture. Stir well to combine. Add the Worcestershire sauce, salt, and black pepper. Bring the mixture to a simmer, stirring constantly, until the gravy thickens.

4. Combine and Serve

Return the cooked patties to the skillet, spooning the gravy over them. Reduce the heat to low and let the patties simmer in the gravy for an additional 5 minutes to absorb the flavors. Serve the Salisbury steaks hot, with mashed potatoes or your favorite gluten-free side dish.

FAQs

Can I use a different type of flour instead of almond flour?

Yes, you can substitute almond flour with other gluten-free flours such as coconut flour or oat flour. However, keep in mind that the texture and flavor may vary slightly.

Is Worcestershire sauce always gluten-free?

Not all Worcestershire sauces are gluten-free. Be sure to check the label and choose a brand that specifically states it is gluten-free.

Can I make this recipe ahead of time?

Yes, you can prepare the patties and gravy ahead of time and store them separately in the refrigerator. When ready to serve, reheat the patties in the gravy on the stovetop until warmed through.

What can I serve with gluten-free Salisbury steak?

Gluten-free Salisbury steak pairs well with mashed potatoes, roasted vegetables, or a fresh green salad. You can also serve it over gluten-free pasta or rice.

Conclusion

Gluten-free Salisbury steak is a delicious and comforting dish that can be enjoyed by everyone, regardless of dietary restrictions. By using simple substitutions like almond flour and gluten-free breadcrumbs, you can recreate this classic recipe without compromising on flavor or texture. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or for a crowd, this gluten-free Salisbury steak is sure to be a hit. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy a hearty, gluten-free meal that’s perfect for any occasion.
