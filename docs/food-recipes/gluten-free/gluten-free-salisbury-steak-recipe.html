Gluten-Free Salisbury Steak Recipe

If you're looking for a comforting, hearty meal that's both delicious and gluten-free, you've come to the right place. Salisbury steak is a classic dish that brings back memories of home-cooked meals. Traditionally made with breadcrumbs and flour, this recipe has been reimagined to be completely gluten-free without sacrificing any of the flavor or texture. Whether you're following a gluten-free diet or simply want to try something new, this recipe is sure to become a family favorite.

Why Choose a Gluten-Free Salisbury Steak?

Gluten-free diets are essential for individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. However, even if you don't have a medical reason to avoid gluten, many people find that reducing gluten in their diet can lead to improved digestion and overall health. This gluten-free Salisbury steak recipe uses alternative ingredients to create a dish that's just as satisfying as the traditional version, but without the gluten.

Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need to make this delicious gluten-free Salisbury steak:

Ingredient Quantity
Ground beef (80/20 blend) 1 pound
Gluten-free breadcrumbs 1/2 cup
Onion (finely chopped) 1/4 cup
Garlic (minced) 2 cloves
Egg 1
Worcestershire sauce (gluten-free) 1 tablespoon
Salt 1/2 teaspoon
Black pepper 1/4 teaspoon
Olive oil 2 tablespoons
Beef broth (gluten-free) 2 cups
Cornstarch 2 tablespoons
Water 2 tablespoons
Mushrooms (sliced) 1 cup

Instructions

Follow these steps to create your gluten-free Salisbury steak:

  1. Prepare the Patties: In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, gluten-free breadcrumbs, chopped onion, minced garlic, egg, Worcestershire sauce, salt, and black pepper. Mix until well combined. Shape the mixture into 4 oval-shaped patties.
  2. Cook the Patties: Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the patties and cook for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until they are browned and cooked through. Remove the patties from the skillet and set aside.
  3. Make the Gravy: In the same skillet, add the sliced mushrooms and cook until they are softened, about 3-4 minutes. In a small bowl, whisk together the cornstarch and water until smooth. Pour the beef broth into the skillet with the mushrooms, then add the cornstarch mixture. Stir well and bring to a simmer. Cook until the gravy thickens, about 5 minutes.
  4. Combine and Serve: Return the patties to the skillet, spooning the gravy over them. Let them simmer in the gravy for an additional 2-3 minutes to absorb the flavors. Serve hot, ideally with mashed potatoes or steamed vegetables.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use a different type of meat?

Yes, you can substitute ground beef with ground turkey or chicken for a lighter version. However, keep in mind that the flavor and texture will be slightly different.

What can I use instead of gluten-free breadcrumbs?

If you don’t have gluten-free breadcrumbs, you can use almond flour or crushed gluten-free crackers as a substitute.

How do I store leftovers?

Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in the microwave or on the stovetop with a splash of beef broth to keep the patties moist.

Can I freeze this dish?

Yes, you can freeze the cooked patties and gravy separately. Place them in freezer-safe containers and store for up to 2 months. Thaw in the refrigerator overnight before reheating.

Conclusion

With its rich, savory gravy and tender beef patties, it’s a dish that will satisfy everyone at the table. Whether you’re cooking for a family dinner or meal prepping for the week, this recipe is both versatile and delicious. Give it a try and discover how easy it is to make a gluten-free version of this beloved dish!
