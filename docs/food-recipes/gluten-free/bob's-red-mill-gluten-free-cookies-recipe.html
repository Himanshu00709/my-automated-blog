Bob's Red Mill Gluten-Free Cookies Recipe

Gluten-free baking has become increasingly popular, and for good reason. Whether you're managing celiac disease, gluten sensitivity, or simply exploring healthier dietary options, gluten-free cookies can be just as delicious as their traditional counterparts. Bob's Red Mill, a trusted name in gluten-free products, offers a fantastic recipe for gluten-free cookies that are easy to make and incredibly satisfying. In this blog post, we'll dive into the details of this recipe, explore its ingredients, and provide tips for perfecting your gluten-free cookies.

Why Choose Bob's Red Mill Gluten-Free Flour?

Bob's Red Mill is renowned for its high-quality gluten-free products, and their gluten-free flour blend is no exception. This flour is a mix of garbanzo bean flour, potato starch, tapioca flour, white sorghum flour, and fava bean flour, which provides a balanced texture and flavor for baking. It’s perfect for cookies, cakes, and other baked goods, ensuring that you don’t have to compromise on taste or texture when going gluten-free.

Ingredients for Bob's Red Mill Gluten-Free Cookies

Here’s what you’ll need to make these delightful gluten-free cookies:

Ingredient Quantity
Bob's Red Mill Gluten-Free 1-to-1 Baking Flour 2 1/4 cups
Unsalted Butter (softened) 1 cup
Granulated Sugar 3/4 cup
Brown Sugar 3/4 cup
Eggs 2 large
Vanilla Extract 1 teaspoon
Baking Soda 1 teaspoon
Salt 1/2 teaspoon
Chocolate Chips (gluten-free) 2 cups

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preheat Your Oven

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). This ensures that your cookies bake evenly and achieve the perfect texture.

Step 2: Cream the Butter and Sugars

In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until the mixture is light and fluffy. This step is crucial for creating a soft and chewy cookie texture.

Step 3: Add Eggs and Vanilla

Beat in the eggs one at a time, ensuring each egg is fully incorporated before adding the next. Then, mix in the vanilla extract. This adds moisture and flavor to your cookies.

Step 4: Combine Dry Ingredients

In a separate bowl, whisk together the Bob's Red Mill Gluten-Free 1-to-1 Baking Flour, baking soda, and salt. Gradually add this dry mixture to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined. Overmixing can lead to dense cookies, so be gentle.

Step 5: Fold in Chocolate Chips

Stir in the gluten-free chocolate chips until they are evenly distributed throughout the dough. You can also add nuts or other mix-ins if desired.

Step 6: Scoop and Bake

Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto ungreased baking sheets, spacing them about 2 inches apart. Bake for 9-11 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown. The centers may look slightly undercooked, but they will firm up as they cool.

Step 7: Cool and Enjoy

Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. Enjoy your delicious gluten-free cookies with a glass of milk or your favorite beverage!

Tips for Perfect Gluten-Free Cookies

  • Use Room Temperature Ingredients: Ensure your butter and eggs are at room temperature for easier mixing and better texture.
  • Don’t Overmix: Overmixing the dough can lead to tough cookies. Mix until just combined.
  • Experiment with Mix-Ins: Feel free to add nuts, dried fruit, or even white chocolate chips for variety.
  • Store Properly: Keep your cookies in an airtight container to maintain their freshness.

FAQs

Can I use a different gluten-free flour?

While Bob's Red Mill Gluten-Free 1-to-1 Baking Flour is specifically formulated for baking, you can experiment with other gluten-free flours. However, the texture and taste may vary.

Can I make these cookies dairy-free?

Yes, you can substitute the butter with a dairy-free alternative like coconut oil or vegan butter. Ensure your chocolate chips are also dairy-free.

How long do these cookies stay fresh?

When stored in an airtight container, these cookies can stay fresh for up to a week. You can also freeze the dough or baked cookies for longer storage.

Can I reduce the sugar in this recipe?

Yes, you can reduce the sugar slightly, but keep in mind that sugar contributes to the texture and spread of the cookies. Reducing it too much may alter the final result.

Whether you’re new to gluten-free baking or a seasoned pro, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your household.
