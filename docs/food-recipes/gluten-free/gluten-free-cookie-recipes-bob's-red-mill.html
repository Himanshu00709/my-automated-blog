1. Classic Gluten-Free Chocolate Chip Cookies

Who doesn't love a classic chocolate chip cookie? This recipe uses Bob's Red Mill 1-to-1 Gluten-Free Baking Flour to create cookies that are soft, chewy, and full of chocolatey goodness.

Ingredients Quantity
Bob's Red Mill 1-to-1 Gluten-Free Baking Flour 2 1/4 cups
Unsalted butter (softened) 1 cup
Granulated sugar 3/4 cup
Brown sugar 3/4 cup
Eggs 2
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Baking soda 1 tsp
Salt 1/2 tsp
Semi-sweet chocolate chips 2 cups

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375째F (190째C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, cream together the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour, baking soda, and salt.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  6. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  7. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
  8. Bake for 9-11 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown.
  9. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

2. Gluten-Free Peanut Butter Cookies

Peanut butter cookies are a timeless treat, and this gluten-free version is no exception. Using Bob's Red Mill Almond Flour, these cookies are rich, nutty, and perfectly crumbly.

Ingredients Quantity
Bob's Red Mill Almond Flour 1 1/2 cups
Creamy peanut butter 1 cup
Granulated sugar 1/2 cup
Brown sugar 1/2 cup
Egg 1
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Baking soda 1/2 tsp
Salt 1/4 tsp

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350째F (175째C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, mix together the peanut butter, granulated sugar, brown sugar, egg, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the almond flour, baking soda, and salt.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a dough forms.
  5. Roll the dough into 1-inch balls and place them on the prepared baking sheet.
  6. Use a fork to press down on each ball, creating a crisscross pattern.
  7. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden.
  8. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Baking with Bob's Red Mill

1. Can I substitute Bob's Red Mill 1-to-1 Gluten-Free Baking Flour for regular flour in any recipe?

Yes, Bob's Red Mill 1-to-1 Gluten-Free Baking Flour is designed to be a direct substitute for all-purpose flour in most recipes. However, it's always a good idea to follow a recipe specifically designed for gluten-free baking to ensure the best results.

2. How do I store gluten-free cookies?

Gluten-free cookies can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to a week. For longer storage, you can freeze the cookies in a freezer-safe container for up to 3 months.

3. Are Bob's Red Mill products safe for people with celiac disease?

Yes, Bob's Red Mill offers a wide range of certified gluten-free products that are safe for individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivities. Always check the packaging for the gluten-free certification label.

4. Can I use almond flour for all gluten-free baking?

Almond flour is a great option for many gluten-free recipes, but it has a different texture and moisture content compared to other gluten-free flours. It works best in recipes that are designed for almond flour, such as certain cookies, cakes, and muffins.
