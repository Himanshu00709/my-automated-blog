Gluten-Free Christmas Pudding Recipe

Christmas is a time for indulgence, but for those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease, traditional holiday treats can be off-limits. Fear not! This gluten-free Christmas pudding recipe is here to save the day. Rich, moist, and packed with festive flavors, this pudding is a perfect addition to your holiday table. Whether you're gluten-free by choice or necessity, this recipe ensures that everyone can enjoy a slice of Christmas joy.

Why Choose a Gluten-Free Christmas Pudding?

Gluten-free diets are essential for individuals with celiac disease or gluten intolerance. Even a small amount of gluten can cause severe discomfort and long-term health issues for these individuals. By opting for a gluten-free Christmas pudding, you ensure that everyone at your table can partake in the festive cheer without worry. Plus, gluten-free recipes often incorporate a variety of alternative flours and ingredients that can add unique textures and flavors to your dessert.

Ingredients You'll Need

To make this gluten-free Christmas pudding, you'll need the following ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free breadcrumbs 100g
Gluten-free self-raising flour 100g
Mixed dried fruit 200g
Dark brown sugar 100g
Butter (dairy-free if needed) 100g
Eggs 2 large
Mixed spice 1 tsp
Ground cinnamon 1 tsp
Brandy or orange juice 2 tbsp
Zest of 1 orange 1

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Your Ingredients

Start by gathering all your ingredients. Ensure that your breadcrumbs and flour are certified gluten-free. If you're using dairy-free butter, make sure it's at room temperature for easier mixing.

Step 2: Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the gluten-free breadcrumbs, self-raising flour, mixed dried fruit, dark brown sugar, mixed spice, and ground cinnamon. Stir well to ensure an even distribution of ingredients.

Step 3: Add the Wet Ingredients

In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, melted butter, brandy (or orange juice), and orange zest. Once combined, pour this mixture into the dry ingredients. Stir thoroughly until everything is well incorporated.

Step 4: Steam the Pudding

Grease a 1.2-liter pudding basin and pour the mixture into it. Cover the basin with a double layer of baking parchment and foil, securing it with string. Place the basin in a large pot with enough boiling water to come halfway up the sides of the basin. Cover the pot with a lid and steam the pudding for 4-5 hours, checking occasionally to ensure the water doesn't evaporate completely.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Once steamed, remove the pudding from the pot and let it cool slightly before turning it out onto a serving plate. Serve warm with a dollop of brandy butter or custard for an extra festive touch.

FAQs

Can I make this pudding ahead of time?

Absolutely! In fact, Christmas puddings often taste better when made a few weeks in advance. Simply store it in a cool, dry place and reheat by steaming for an hour before serving.

Can I use fresh fruit instead of dried fruit?

While dried fruit is traditional for Christmas pudding, you can experiment with fresh fruit. Just be aware that fresh fruit may alter the texture and moisture content of the pudding.

Is this recipe suitable for vegans?

This recipe can be made vegan by substituting the eggs with a flaxseed or chia seed egg and using dairy-free butter. Ensure all other ingredients are vegan-friendly as well.

Can I freeze the pudding?

Yes, you can freeze the pudding for up to three months. Wrap it tightly in cling film and foil before freezing. Thaw and reheat by steaming when ready to serve.

Conclusion

With its rich, fruity flavors and moist texture, it's sure to become a festive favorite. Whether you're hosting a large family gathering or enjoying a quiet Christmas at home, this pudding is a must-try. So, gather your ingredients, follow the steps, and get ready to impress your guests with this delicious gluten-free treat. Happy holidays!
