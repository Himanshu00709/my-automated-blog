2. Gluten-Free Chocolate Chip Cookies

Who doesn’t love a classic chocolate chip cookie? This gluten-free version is chewy, soft, and packed with chocolatey goodness.

Ingredients Instructions
1 cup Krusteaz Gluten-Free Flour 1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
1/2 tsp baking soda 2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt.
1/4 tsp salt 3. In a separate bowl, cream together the butter, sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy.
1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened 4. Beat in the egg and vanilla extract until well combined.
1/4 cup granulated sugar 5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
1/4 cup brown sugar 6. Fold in the chocolate chips.
1 large egg 7. Drop tablespoon-sized portions of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
1 tsp vanilla extract 8. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown.
1 cup chocolate chips 9. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

3. Gluten-Free Banana Bread

This moist and flavorful banana bread is a great way to use up overripe bananas. It’s perfect for breakfast, a snack, or even dessert.

Ingredients Instructions
1 1/2 cups Krusteaz Gluten-Free Flour 1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan.
1 tsp baking soda 2. In a large bowl, mash the bananas until smooth.
1/2 tsp salt 3. Stir in the melted butter, sugar, egg, and vanilla extract until well combined.
3 ripe bananas, mashed 4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt.
1/3 cup melted butter 5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
3/4 cup sugar 6. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top.
1 large egg, beaten 7. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
1 tsp vanilla extract 8. Allow the banana bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

FAQs About Krusteaz Gluten-Free Flour

1. Can I use Krusteaz Gluten-Free Flour in any recipe?

Yes, Krusteaz Gluten-Free Flour can be used as a 1:1 substitute for regular flour in most recipes. However, for best results, it’s recommended to follow recipes specifically designed for gluten-free baking.

2. Does Krusteaz Gluten-Free Flour contain xanthan gum?

No, Krusteaz Gluten-Free Flour does not contain xanthan gum. If your recipe calls for xanthan gum, you may need to add it separately.

3. How should I store Krusteaz Gluten-Free Flour?

Store Krusteaz Gluten-Free Flour in a cool, dry place, such as a pantry or cupboard. For longer shelf life, you can store it in an airtight container in the refrigerator or freezer.

4. Is Krusteaz Gluten-Free Flour suitable for people with celiac disease?

Yes, Krusteaz Gluten-Free Flour is certified gluten-free and is safe for individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity.
