Gluten-Free Corn Casserole with Krusteaz: A Delicious and Easy Recipe

Corn casserole is a classic comfort food that brings warmth and nostalgia to any meal. For those who follow a gluten-free diet, finding the perfect recipe can be a challenge. Enter Krusteaz, a trusted brand known for its high-quality gluten-free baking mixes. In this blog post, we’ll explore how to create a mouthwatering gluten-free corn casserole using Krusteaz’s gluten-free cornbread mix. Whether you’re hosting a holiday dinner or simply craving a cozy side dish, this recipe is sure to impress.

Why Choose Krusteaz for Gluten-Free Baking?

Krusteaz has been a household name in baking for decades, and their gluten-free products are no exception. Their gluten-free cornbread mix is made with simple, wholesome ingredients, ensuring that you get the same great taste and texture as traditional cornbread without the gluten. Here are a few reasons why Krusteaz stands out:

  • Quality Ingredients: Krusteaz uses non-GMO cornmeal and other high-quality ingredients to create a delicious and nutritious product.
  • Easy to Use: The mix is incredibly easy to prepare, making it perfect for both novice and experienced bakers.
  • Versatile: Beyond traditional cornbread, Krusteaz’s gluten-free mix can be used in a variety of recipes, including corn casserole.

Ingredients for Gluten-Free Corn Casserole

To make this gluten-free corn casserole, you’ll need the following ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity
Krusteaz Gluten-Free Cornbread Mix 1 box (15 oz)
Whole kernel corn (drained) 1 can (15 oz)
Creamed corn 1 can (14.75 oz)
Sour cream 1 cup
Butter (melted) 1/2 cup
Eggs 2 large
Shredded cheddar cheese 1 cup
Salt and pepper To taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

Follow these simple steps to create your gluten-free corn casserole:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  2. Mix the Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the Krusteaz gluten-free cornbread mix, whole kernel corn, creamed corn, sour cream, melted butter, and eggs. Stir until well combined.
  3. Add Cheese: Fold in the shredded cheddar cheese, reserving a small amount to sprinkle on top.
  4. Season: Add salt and pepper to taste, and mix well.
  5. Bake: Pour the mixture into the prepared baking dish and sprinkle the remaining cheese on top. Bake for 45-50 minutes, or until the casserole is golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. Serve: Allow the casserole to cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy!

Tips for the Perfect Gluten-Free Corn Casserole

To ensure your corn casserole turns out perfectly every time, consider these tips:

  • Use Fresh Ingredients: Fresh corn and high-quality dairy products can make a significant difference in the flavor and texture of your casserole.
  • Don’t Overmix: Overmixing the batter can lead to a dense casserole. Mix just until the ingredients are combined.
  • Customize: Feel free to add your favorite ingredients, such as diced jalapeños, green chilies, or bacon, to give the casserole a unique twist.
  • Check for Doneness: Oven temperatures can vary, so keep an eye on your casserole as it bakes. If the top is browning too quickly, cover it with aluminum foil.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Corn Casserole

Can I make this recipe dairy-free?

Yes, you can make this recipe dairy-free by substituting the sour cream with a dairy-free alternative, such as coconut cream, and using a dairy-free cheese and butter substitute.

Can I freeze the corn casserole?

Absolutely! This casserole freezes well. Allow it to cool completely, then wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil before freezing. To reheat, thaw it in the refrigerator overnight and bake at 350°F until heated through.

What can I serve with gluten-free corn casserole?

This casserole pairs well with a variety of dishes, including roasted chicken, grilled steak, or a fresh green salad. It’s also a great addition to holiday meals, such as Thanksgiving or Christmas dinner.

Can I use fresh corn instead of canned?

Yes, you can use fresh corn if you prefer. Simply cut the kernels off the cob and use them in place of the canned corn. You may need to adjust the cooking time slightly, as fresh corn can release more moisture.
