Basic Gluten-Free Biscuit Recipe

Let's start with the basics. Here's a simple recipe to make classic gluten-free biscuits using Bob's Red Mill Gluten-Free Biscuit Mix:

Ingredients Quantity
Bob's Red Mill Gluten-Free Biscuit Mix 2 cups
Cold Butter 1/2 cup (1 stick)
Milk (or non-dairy alternative) 2/3 cup

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the biscuit mix and cold butter. Use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the mix until it resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. Add the milk and stir until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix.
  4. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead it a few times. Roll it out to about 1/2 inch thickness.
  5. Use a biscuit cutter to cut out biscuits and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  6. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the biscuits are golden brown.
  7. Serve warm and enjoy!

Cheesy Garlic Biscuits

Add a savory twist to your biscuits by incorporating cheese and garlic. Here's how:

Ingredients Quantity
Bob's Red Mill Gluten-Free Biscuit Mix 2 cups
Cold Butter 1/2 cup (1 stick)
Milk (or non-dairy alternative) 2/3 cup
Shredded Cheddar Cheese 1 cup
Garlic Powder 1 tsp
Fresh Parsley (chopped) 1 tbsp

Instructions:

  1. Follow the basic biscuit recipe, but add the shredded cheddar cheese, garlic powder, and fresh parsley to the dough before mixing.
  2. Proceed with cutting and baking as usual.
  3. For an extra touch, brush the tops of the biscuits with melted butter and sprinkle with additional garlic powder and parsley before baking.

Sweet Cinnamon Biscuits

If you have a sweet tooth, these cinnamon biscuits are sure to satisfy:

Ingredients Quantity
Bob's Red Mill Gluten-Free Biscuit Mix 2 cups
Cold Butter 1/2 cup (1 stick)
Milk (or non-dairy alternative) 2/3 cup
Sugar 1/4 cup
Cinnamon 1 tsp
Vanilla Extract 1 tsp

Instructions:

  1. Follow the basic biscuit recipe, but add the sugar, cinnamon, and vanilla extract to the dough before mixing.
  2. Proceed with cutting and baking as usual.
  3. For an extra treat, drizzle the baked biscuits with a simple glaze made from powdered sugar and milk.

FAQs About Bob's Red Mill Gluten-Free Biscuit Mix

1. Can I use a non-dairy milk alternative in the recipes?

Yes, you can easily substitute regular milk with a non-dairy alternative like almond milk, soy milk, or oat milk. The results will be just as delicious!

2. How should I store leftover biscuits?

Store leftover biscuits in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days. For longer storage, you can freeze the biscuits and reheat them in the oven when ready to serve.

3. Can I add other ingredients to the mix?

Absolutely! Feel free to experiment with additional ingredients like herbs, spices, dried fruits, or nuts to customize your biscuits to your liking.

4. Is Bob's Red Mill Gluten-Free Biscuit Mix suitable for vegans?

The mix itself is vegan, but some recipes may call for butter or milk. You can easily make vegan biscuits by using plant-based butter and non-dairy milk.
