Gluten-Free Peach Blueberry Cobbler: A Delightful Summer Dessert

As the summer season rolls in, so does the abundance of fresh fruits like peaches and blueberries. What better way to celebrate the season than with a warm, comforting dessert that’s both gluten-free and bursting with flavor? Enter the Gluten-Free Peach Blueberry Cobbler—a dessert that combines the sweetness of ripe peaches with the tanginess of blueberries, all topped with a golden, gluten-free biscuit-like crust. Whether you’re gluten-intolerant or simply looking for a healthier dessert option, this cobbler is sure to impress.

Why Choose a Gluten-Free Cobbler?

Gluten-free baking has become increasingly popular, not just among those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, but also among health-conscious individuals. Gluten-free desserts can be just as delicious and satisfying as their traditional counterparts, and this peach blueberry cobbler is no exception. By using gluten-free flour blends, you can enjoy a dessert that’s light, fluffy, and free from the discomfort that gluten can cause for some people.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the recipe, let’s take a look at the ingredients you’ll need to make this delightful dessert. The beauty of this recipe lies in its simplicity and the use of fresh, seasonal fruits.

Ingredient Quantity
Fresh peaches (sliced) 4 cups
Fresh blueberries 2 cups
Granulated sugar 1/2 cup
Lemon juice 1 tablespoon
Cornstarch 2 tablespoons
Gluten-free flour blend 1 1/2 cups
Baking powder 1 teaspoon
Salt 1/4 teaspoon
Unsalted butter (cold) 1/2 cup
Milk (or dairy-free alternative) 1/2 cup
Vanilla extract 1 teaspoon

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that you have all your ingredients ready, let’s walk through the steps to create this mouthwatering gluten-free peach blueberry cobbler.

Step 1: Prepare the Fruit Filling

Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced peaches, blueberries, granulated sugar, lemon juice, and cornstarch. Gently toss the fruits until they are evenly coated. The sugar will help draw out the natural juices of the fruits, while the cornstarch will thicken the filling as it bakes. Set the fruit mixture aside while you prepare the cobbler topping.

Step 2: Make the Cobbler Topping

In a separate bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour blend, baking powder, and salt. Cut the cold butter into small cubes and add it to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add the milk and vanilla extract, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a tough topping.

Step 3: Assemble and Bake

Transfer the fruit mixture to a greased 9x13 inch baking dish. Drop spoonfuls of the cobbler topping evenly over the fruit. Don’t worry if the topping doesn’t cover every inch—this rustic look is part of the charm. Bake in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the fruit filling is bubbling. Allow the cobbler to cool slightly before serving.

Serving Suggestions

This gluten-free peach blueberry cobbler is delicious on its own, but you can take it to the next level by serving it with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream. The contrast between the warm cobbler and the cold ice cream is simply irresistible. For a dairy-free option, consider using coconut milk ice cream or a non-dairy whipped topping.

FAQs

Can I use frozen fruit instead of fresh?

Yes, you can use frozen peaches and blueberries if fresh ones are not available. Just make sure to thaw and drain the fruit before using it in the recipe to avoid excess liquid in the filling.

What gluten-free flour blend works best?

Any high-quality gluten-free flour blend that contains xanthan gum will work well in this recipe. Popular brands include Bob’s Red Mill and King Arthur Flour. If your blend doesn’t contain xanthan gum, you may need to add 1/2 teaspoon to the dry ingredients.

Can I make this cobbler ahead of time?

Absolutely! You can prepare the cobbler up to a day in advance and store it in the refrigerator. When you’re ready to serve, simply reheat it in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 15-20 minutes, or until warmed through.

With its juicy fruit filling and tender, golden topping, this cobbler is sure to become a favorite in your recipe collection.
