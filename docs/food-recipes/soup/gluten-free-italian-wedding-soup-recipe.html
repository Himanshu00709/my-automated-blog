Gluten-Free Italian Wedding Soup Recipe

Italian Wedding Soup is a classic comfort food that combines savory meatballs, tender greens, and a flavorful broth. Traditionally, this soup includes small pasta, but for those following a gluten-free diet, this recipe offers a delicious alternative without sacrificing flavor. Whether you're hosting a dinner party or simply craving a hearty meal, this gluten-free Italian Wedding Soup is sure to impress.

Why Choose Gluten-Free Italian Wedding Soup?

Gluten-free diets are essential for individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. However, even if you don't have dietary restrictions, opting for gluten-free recipes can be a healthier choice. This soup is packed with nutrient-rich ingredients like spinach, carrots, and lean protein, making it a wholesome meal for everyone. Plus, the gluten-free meatballs are just as flavorful as the traditional version!

Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need to make this delicious soup:

Ingredient Quantity
Ground turkey or chicken 1 lb
Gluten-free breadcrumbs 1/2 cup
Parmesan cheese (grated) 1/4 cup
Egg 1
Garlic (minced) 2 cloves
Fresh parsley (chopped) 2 tbsp
Olive oil 2 tbsp
Chicken broth 8 cups
Carrots (diced) 2
Celery (diced) 2 stalks
Spinach (fresh) 4 cups
Gluten-free orzo or rice 1 cup
Salt and pepper To taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Meatballs

In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground turkey or chicken, gluten-free breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, egg, minced garlic, and chopped parsley. Mix until well combined. Roll the mixture into small, bite-sized meatballs (about 1 inch in diameter). Set aside.

2. Cook the Vegetables

Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced carrots and celery, and sauté for about 5 minutes until they begin to soften. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

3. Add the Broth

Pour the chicken broth into the pot and bring it to a gentle boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat to a simmer.

4. Cook the Meatballs

Carefully drop the meatballs into the simmering broth. Let them cook for about 10-12 minutes, or until they are fully cooked through.

5. Add the Greens and Pasta

Stir in the fresh spinach and gluten-free orzo or rice. Cook for an additional 8-10 minutes, or until the pasta is tender and the spinach is wilted.

6. Serve and Enjoy

Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with extra Parmesan cheese and fresh parsley if desired. Serve hot and enjoy!

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use beef instead of turkey or chicken?

Yes, you can substitute ground beef for the turkey or chicken. However, keep in mind that beef has a stronger flavor and higher fat content, which may alter the taste and nutritional profile of the soup.

What can I use instead of gluten-free orzo?

If you don’t have gluten-free orzo, you can use rice, quinoa, or even gluten-free pasta shapes like small shells or elbows.

Can I make this soup ahead of time?

Absolutely! This soup stores well in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat it on the stove or in the microwave before serving. If you plan to freeze it, omit the pasta and add it fresh when reheating to prevent it from becoming mushy.

Is this soup suitable for vegetarians?

This recipe includes meat, but you can make a vegetarian version by using plant-based meatballs and vegetable broth instead of chicken broth.

Conclusion

Gluten-Free Italian Wedding Soup is a versatile and satisfying dish that’s perfect for any occasion. With its flavorful meatballs, hearty vegetables, and comforting broth, it’s a meal that everyone can enjoy, regardless of dietary restrictions. Plus, it’s easy to customize with your favorite ingredients. Give this recipe a try, and you’ll see why it’s a beloved classic!
