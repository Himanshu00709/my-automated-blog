Gluten-Free Dairy-Free Biscuits and Gravy: A Delicious Alternative

For those with dietary restrictions, finding comfort food that fits within your dietary needs can be a challenge. Biscuits and gravy, a classic Southern dish, is often off-limits for those who are gluten-free or dairy-free. However, with a few clever substitutions, you can enjoy this hearty and satisfying meal without compromising your health or taste buds. In this article, we’ll explore how to make gluten-free dairy-free biscuits and gravy that are just as delicious as the traditional version.

Why Go Gluten-Free and Dairy-Free?

Gluten and dairy are common allergens and can cause digestive issues for many people. Gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, can trigger symptoms in those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. Dairy, on the other hand, contains lactose, which can be difficult to digest for those who are lactose intolerant. By opting for gluten-free and dairy-free alternatives, you can enjoy your favorite dishes without the discomfort or health risks associated with these ingredients.

Ingredients for Gluten-Free Dairy-Free Biscuits

Making gluten-free dairy-free biscuits requires a few key substitutions. Here’s a list of ingredients you’ll need:

Ingredient Substitute
Flour Gluten-free flour blend (e.g., almond flour, coconut flour, or a pre-made gluten-free mix)
Butter Dairy-free butter or coconut oil
Milk Non-dairy milk (e.g., almond milk, oat milk, or coconut milk)
Baking Powder Gluten-free baking powder
Salt Sea salt or kosher salt

How to Make Gluten-Free Dairy-Free Biscuits

Follow these steps to create fluffy, delicious gluten-free dairy-free biscuits:

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Mix the dry ingredients: In a large bowl, combine 2 cups of gluten-free flour, 1 tablespoon of baking powder, and 1/2 teaspoon of salt.
  3. Cut in the fat: Add 1/2 cup of dairy-free butter or coconut oil to the dry ingredients. Use a pastry cutter or your fingers to mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Add the liquid: Gradually pour in 3/4 cup of non-dairy milk, stirring until the dough comes together. Be careful not to overmix.
  5. Shape the biscuits: Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and gently pat it into a 1-inch thick circle. Use a biscuit cutter or a glass to cut out biscuits.
  6. Bake: Place the biscuits on the prepared baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until golden brown.

Ingredients for Dairy-Free Gravy

The gravy is the heart of this dish, and making it dairy-free is easier than you might think. Here’s what you’ll need:

Ingredient Substitute
Flour Gluten-free flour blend
Butter Dairy-free butter or olive oil
Milk Non-dairy milk (e.g., almond milk, oat milk, or coconut milk)
Broth Vegetable or chicken broth
Seasonings Salt, pepper, and optional herbs like thyme or sage

How to Make Dairy-Free Gravy

Follow these steps to create a rich, creamy dairy-free gravy:

  1. Melt the fat: In a saucepan, melt 1/4 cup of dairy-free butter or heat 1/4 cup of olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the flour: Whisk in 1/4 cup of gluten-free flour until smooth, creating a roux. Cook for 1-2 minutes to remove the raw flour taste.
  3. Add the liquid: Gradually whisk in 2 cups of non-dairy milk and 1 cup of broth. Continue whisking until the mixture thickens.
  4. Season: Add salt, pepper, and any optional herbs to taste. Simmer for 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the gravy reaches your desired consistency.

Serving Suggestions

Once your biscuits and gravy are ready, serve them warm for the best experience. You can pair this dish with a side of scrambled tofu or a fresh green salad for a complete meal. For added flavor, consider topping your biscuits with a sprinkle of nutritional yeast or a dash of hot sauce.

FAQs

Can I make these biscuits ahead of time?

Yes, you can prepare the biscuit dough ahead of time and store it in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours. When you're ready to bake, simply shape the biscuits and bake as directed.

Can I freeze the biscuits?

Absolutely! Once baked, let the biscuits cool completely, then store them in an airtight container or freezer bag. They can be frozen for up to 3 months. To reheat, simply warm them in the oven or microwave.

What can I use instead of gluten-free flour?

If you don’t have a gluten-free flour blend, you can use almond flour or coconut flour. Keep in mind that these flours have different textures and may require adjustments to the liquid ingredients.

Is this recipe vegan?

Yes, this recipe is vegan as long as you use plant-based butter and non-dairy milk. Be sure to check the labels of your ingredients to ensure they are vegan-friendly.

Conclusion

Gluten-free dairy-free biscuits and gravy is a comforting and satisfying dish that can be enjoyed by everyone, regardless of dietary restrictions. With the right ingredients and a little creativity, you can recreate this classic Southern meal in a way that’s both delicious and inclusive. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or for a crowd, this recipe is sure to become a favorite. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy a plate of biscuits and gravy that’s as good for your body as it is for your soul.
