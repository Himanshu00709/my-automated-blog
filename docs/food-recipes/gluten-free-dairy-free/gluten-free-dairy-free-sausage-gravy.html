Gluten-Free Dairy-Free Sausage Gravy: A Delicious and Inclusive Recipe

For those who follow a gluten-free or dairy-free diet, finding comfort food that fits dietary restrictions can be a challenge. Sausage gravy, a Southern classic, is traditionally made with flour and milk, making it off-limits for many. However, with a few clever substitutions, you can enjoy a rich, creamy, and flavorful gluten-free dairy-free sausage gravy that’s just as satisfying as the original. In this article, we’ll explore how to make this dish, its health benefits, and answer some common questions about gluten-free and dairy-free cooking.

Why Choose Gluten-Free Dairy-Free Sausage Gravy?

Gluten-free and dairy-free diets are essential for individuals with celiac disease, gluten sensitivity, lactose intolerance, or dairy allergies. Even if you don’t have these conditions, reducing gluten and dairy can sometimes lead to improved digestion, reduced inflammation, and better overall health. This recipe ensures that everyone can enjoy a hearty breakfast or brunch without compromising their dietary needs.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make gluten-free dairy-free sausage gravy, you’ll need the following ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity Notes
Gluten-free sausage 1 pound Ensure it’s certified gluten-free.
Gluten-free flour blend 1/4 cup Use a 1:1 substitute like almond flour or rice flour.
Unsweetened almond milk 2 cups Or any other dairy-free milk like oat or coconut.
Olive oil or dairy-free butter 2 tablespoons For sautéing and richness.
Salt and pepper To taste Adjust according to preference.
Optional: Garlic powder, onion powder, or paprika 1/2 teaspoon each For added flavor.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Cook the Sausage

In a large skillet, cook the gluten-free sausage over medium heat until it’s browned and crumbled. Break it into small pieces as it cooks. Once done, remove the sausage from the skillet and set it aside, leaving the drippings in the pan.

Step 2: Make the Roux

Add olive oil or dairy-free butter to the skillet with the sausage drippings. Sprinkle the gluten-free flour blend over the fat and whisk continuously to create a roux. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the mixture is smooth and slightly golden.

Step 3: Add the Dairy-Free Milk

Slowly pour in the unsweetened almond milk, whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Continue to cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens. This should take about 5-7 minutes.

Step 4: Season and Combine

Add salt, pepper, and any optional spices to the gravy. Return the cooked sausage to the skillet and stir to combine. Let the gravy simmer for another 2-3 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Step 5: Serve and Enjoy

Serve your gluten-free dairy-free sausage gravy over gluten-free biscuits, toast, or hash browns. Garnish with fresh herbs like parsley or chives if desired.

Health Benefits of Gluten-Free Dairy-Free Sausage Gravy

This recipe offers several health benefits:

  • Digestive Health: By eliminating gluten and dairy, this dish is easier on the digestive system, especially for those with sensitivities.
  • Lower Inflammation: Gluten and dairy can sometimes cause inflammation in certain individuals. This recipe helps reduce that risk.
  • Nutrient-Dense: Using almond milk and gluten-free flour adds vitamins and minerals like vitamin E and magnesium.

FAQs

Can I use a different type of milk?

Yes, you can use any dairy-free milk such as oat milk, coconut milk, or cashew milk. Just ensure it’s unsweetened and unflavored for the best results.

What can I use instead of gluten-free flour?

You can use almond flour, rice flour, or a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend. Each will slightly alter the texture, so choose based on your preference.

Is this recipe suitable for vegans?

This recipe can be made vegan by using plant-based sausage and ensuring all other ingredients are vegan-friendly.

How do I store leftovers?

Store any leftover gravy in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat on the stovetop or in the microwave, adding a splash of dairy-free milk if needed to thin it out.

Conclusion

With simple substitutions and easy-to-follow steps, this recipe is perfect for breakfast, brunch, or even dinner. Whether you’re new to gluten-free and dairy-free cooking or a seasoned pro, this gravy is sure to become a staple in your kitchen. Give it a try and savor the rich, creamy flavors that everyone can enjoy!
