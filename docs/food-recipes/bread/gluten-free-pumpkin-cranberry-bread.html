Gluten-Free Pumpkin Cranberry Bread: A Delicious and Healthy Treat

As the leaves change color and the air turns crisp, there's nothing quite like the comforting aroma of freshly baked pumpkin bread wafting through your kitchen. But what if you're following a gluten-free diet? Fear not! This gluten-free pumpkin cranberry bread is here to satisfy your autumn cravings without compromising your dietary needs. Packed with the warm flavors of pumpkin, the tartness of cranberries, and the wholesome goodness of gluten-free ingredients, this bread is a must-try for anyone looking to enjoy a healthier, yet equally delicious, treat.

Why Gluten-Free Pumpkin Cranberry Bread?

Gluten-free baking has come a long way in recent years, and this pumpkin cranberry bread is a testament to how delicious and satisfying it can be. Whether you're gluten-intolerant, have celiac disease, or simply prefer to avoid gluten, this recipe offers a perfect balance of flavor and texture. The combination of pumpkin and cranberries not only provides a delightful contrast in taste but also packs a nutritional punch. Pumpkin is rich in vitamins A and C, while cranberries are known for their antioxidant properties. Together, they create a bread that's as nutritious as it is delicious.

Ingredients You'll Need

Before we dive into the recipe, let's take a look at the ingredients you'll need to make this gluten-free pumpkin cranberry bread:

Ingredient Quantity Notes
Gluten-free flour blend 2 cups Ensure it contains xanthan gum or add 1 tsp separately
Baking powder 1 tsp Make sure it's gluten-free
Baking soda 1/2 tsp
Salt 1/2 tsp
Ground cinnamon 1 tsp
Ground nutmeg 1/2 tsp
Ground cloves 1/4 tsp
Pumpkin puree 1 cup Not pumpkin pie filling
Eggs 2 large
Coconut oil (melted) 1/2 cup Can substitute with another oil
Maple syrup 1/2 cup Or honey
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Fresh or dried cranberries 1 cup If using dried, soak in warm water for 10 minutes

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that you have all your ingredients ready, let's get baking! Follow these simple steps to create your gluten-free pumpkin cranberry bread:

Step 1: Preheat and Prepare

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan with a little coconut oil or line it with parchment paper for easy removal.

Step 2: Mix Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour blend, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves. Ensure that all the dry ingredients are well combined.

Step 3: Combine Wet Ingredients

In a separate bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, eggs, melted coconut oil, maple syrup, and vanilla extract until smooth and well combined.

Step 4: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a dense bread. Gently fold in the cranberries.

Step 5: Bake

Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Bake in the preheated oven for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Step 6: Cool and Serve

Allow the bread to cool in the pan for about 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely. Slice and serve as is, or with a dollop of your favorite spread.

FAQs

Can I use fresh cranberries instead of dried?

Yes, you can use fresh cranberries if you prefer. Just make sure to chop them into smaller pieces before adding them to the batter. Fresh cranberries will add a slightly more tart flavor to the bread.

Can I substitute the coconut oil with another oil?

Absolutely! You can use any neutral-flavored oil, such as avocado oil or even melted butter, if you're not strictly dairy-free.

How should I store the bread?

Store the bread in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw at room temperature before serving.

Can I make this recipe vegan?

Yes, you can make this recipe vegan by substituting the eggs with flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tbsp water per egg) and using a plant-based sweetener like maple syrup.
