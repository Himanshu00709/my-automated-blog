Gluten-Free Blueberry Zucchini Bread: A Healthy Delight

In recent years, gluten-free diets have gained immense popularity, not just among those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, but also among health-conscious individuals seeking to improve their overall well-being. One of the most delightful ways to enjoy gluten-free baking is by making a moist, flavorful, and nutrient-packed bread like Gluten-Free Blueberry Zucchini Bread. This recipe combines the natural sweetness of blueberries with the subtle earthiness of zucchini, creating a treat that is both delicious and nutritious.

Why Choose Gluten-Free Blueberry Zucchini Bread?

Gluten-free baking can be challenging, but this recipe ensures a perfect balance of texture and flavor. The zucchini adds moisture, while the blueberries provide a burst of sweetness and antioxidants. This bread is not only gluten-free but also packed with vitamins, fiber, and healthy fats, making it a guilt-free indulgence for breakfast, snacks, or dessert.

Ingredients You'll Need

To make this gluten-free blueberry zucchini bread, you’ll need the following ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity Notes
Gluten-free flour blend 2 cups Ensure it contains xanthan gum or add 1 tsp separately.
Baking powder 1 tsp Helps the bread rise.
Baking soda 1/2 tsp Adds lightness to the texture.
Salt 1/4 tsp Enhances flavor.
Cinnamon 1 tsp Adds warmth and depth.
Eggs 2 large Binds the ingredients together.
Honey or maple syrup 1/2 cup Natural sweetener.
Coconut oil (melted) 1/4 cup Adds moisture and healthy fats.
Vanilla extract 1 tsp Enhances flavor.
Zucchini (grated) 1 cup Adds moisture and nutrients.
Fresh blueberries 1 cup Provides natural sweetness and antioxidants.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preheat and Prepare

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan with coconut oil or line it with parchment paper for easy removal.

Step 2: Mix Dry Ingredients

In a large bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour blend, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon. Ensure there are no lumps.

Step 3: Combine Wet Ingredients

In a separate bowl, beat the eggs, then add the honey (or maple syrup), melted coconut oil, and vanilla extract. Mix until well combined.

Step 4: Incorporate Zucchini

Fold the grated zucchini into the wet mixture. The zucchini will add moisture and a subtle vegetable flavor that complements the sweetness of the blueberries.

Step 5: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Gradually add the wet mixture to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can make the bread dense.

Step 6: Add Blueberries

Gently fold in the fresh blueberries. If you’re using frozen blueberries, toss them in a bit of gluten-free flour to prevent them from sinking to the bottom of the bread.

Step 7: Bake

Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Step 8: Cool and Serve

Allow the bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely. Slice and serve with a drizzle of honey or a dollop of Greek yogurt for added indulgence.

FAQs

Can I use frozen blueberries instead of fresh?

Yes, you can use frozen blueberries. Just make sure to toss them in a bit of gluten-free flour before adding them to the batter to prevent them from sinking.

Can I substitute the honey with another sweetener?

Absolutely! Maple syrup, agave nectar, or even a sugar substitute like stevia can be used. Adjust the quantity to taste.

How should I store this bread?

Store the bread in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days, or in the refrigerator for up to a week. You can also freeze it for up to 3 months.
