Gluten-Free Cranberry Pumpkin Bread: A Delicious Fall Treat

As the leaves change color and the air turns crisp, there's nothing quite like the comforting aroma of freshly baked pumpkin bread wafting through your kitchen. For those who follow a gluten-free diet, finding the perfect fall treat can be a challenge. Enter gluten-free cranberry pumpkin bread—a moist, flavorful, and wholesome alternative that captures the essence of autumn in every bite. This recipe is not only gluten-free but also packed with nutrients, making it a guilt-free indulgence for everyone to enjoy.

Why Gluten-Free Cranberry Pumpkin Bread?

Gluten-free baking has come a long way in recent years, with a variety of flours and ingredients now available to create delicious and satisfying baked goods. Pumpkin bread is a classic fall favorite, and adding cranberries gives it a delightful tartness that balances the sweetness of the bread. This gluten-free version is perfect for those with celiac disease, gluten intolerance, or anyone looking to reduce their gluten intake without sacrificing flavor.

Ingredients You'll Need

To make this gluten-free cranberry pumpkin bread, you'll need the following ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity Notes
Gluten-free flour blend 2 cups Ensure it contains xanthan gum or add 1 tsp separately
Baking powder 1 tsp Ensure it's gluten-free
Baking soda 1/2 tsp
Salt 1/4 tsp
Ground cinnamon 1 tsp
Ground nutmeg 1/2 tsp
Pumpkin puree 1 cup Ensure it's pure pumpkin, not pie filling
Eggs 2 large
Granulated sugar 3/4 cup Can substitute with coconut sugar
Vegetable oil 1/2 cup Can substitute with melted coconut oil
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Fresh cranberries 1 cup Can use dried cranberries if fresh are unavailable

Step-by-Step Instructions

Follow these simple steps to create your gluten-free cranberry pumpkin bread:

  1. Preheat your oven: Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan and set it aside.
  2. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour blend, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg until well combined.
  3. Combine wet ingredients: In a separate bowl, mix the pumpkin puree, eggs, sugar, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract until smooth.
  4. Combine wet and dry ingredients: Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  5. Fold in cranberries: Gently fold in the fresh cranberries, ensuring they are evenly distributed throughout the batter.
  6. Bake: Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Cool and serve: Allow the bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely. Slice and serve.

Nutritional Benefits

This gluten-free cranberry pumpkin bread is not only delicious but also packed with nutritional benefits. Pumpkin is rich in vitamins A and C, which are essential for maintaining healthy skin and boosting the immune system. Cranberries are a great source of antioxidants, which help protect the body from free radicals. Additionally, using gluten-free flour ensures that this bread is suitable for those with gluten sensitivities, making it a healthier option for everyone.

FAQs

Can I use a different type of flour?

Yes, you can use other gluten-free flours such as almond flour or oat flour. However, the texture and flavor may vary slightly. Be sure to adjust the liquid content if necessary.

Can I make this bread vegan?

Absolutely! You can substitute the eggs with flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tbsp water per egg) and use a plant-based oil like coconut oil.

How should I store the bread?

Store the bread in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or in the refrigerator for up to a week. You can also freeze it for up to 3 months.

Can I add nuts or other mix-ins?

Yes, feel free to add chopped nuts, chocolate chips, or other dried fruits to the batter for added texture and flavor.

Conclusion

Whether you're gluten-free by necessity or choice, this bread is a delicious and nutritious option that everyone can enjoy. With its simple ingredients and easy preparation, it's a great recipe to add to your autumn baking repertoire. So, grab your apron, preheat your oven, and get ready to fill your home with the delightful aroma of this seasonal favorite!
