Low Sugar Gluten-Free Pumpkin Bread: A Healthy Delight

As the leaves change color and the air turns crisp, there's nothing quite like the comforting aroma of pumpkin bread wafting through your kitchen. But what if you're looking for a healthier alternative that fits your dietary needs? Enter low sugar gluten-free pumpkin bread—a delicious, nutrient-packed treat that satisfies your cravings without compromising your health. Whether you're managing diabetes, following a gluten-free diet, or simply looking to reduce your sugar intake, this recipe is a game-changer.

Why Choose Low Sugar Gluten-Free Pumpkin Bread?

Traditional pumpkin bread recipes often call for refined sugars and all-purpose flour, which can spike blood sugar levels and cause inflammation in some individuals. By opting for a low sugar and gluten-free version, you can enjoy the same comforting flavors while supporting your overall health. This bread is rich in fiber, vitamins, and antioxidants, making it a guilt-free indulgence.

Step-by-Step Recipe

Now that you know the key ingredients, let's dive into the recipe. Follow these steps to create your own low sugar gluten-free pumpkin bread:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups almond flour
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup monk fruit sweetener
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil (melted)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp pumpkin spice
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a loaf pan with coconut oil or line it with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the almond flour, baking soda, pumpkin spice, cinnamon, and salt. Mix well.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, eggs, monk fruit sweetener, melted coconut oil, and vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until a smooth batter forms.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and spread it evenly.
  6. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow the bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

FAQs About Low Sugar Gluten-Free Pumpkin Bread

1. Can I use a different sweetener?

Yes, you can substitute monk fruit sweetener with other low-calorie sweeteners like stevia or erythritol. However, adjust the quantity based on the sweetness level of the alternative sweetener.

2. How should I store the pumpkin bread?

Store the bread in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, refrigerate for up to a week or freeze for up to 3 months.

3. Can I add nuts or chocolate chips?

Absolutely! Feel free to add chopped walnuts, pecans, or sugar-free chocolate chips to the batter for added texture and flavor.
