Gluten-Free Zucchini Blueberry Bread: A Healthy Delight

In the world of gluten-free baking, finding a recipe that is both delicious and nutritious can be a challenge. Enter gluten-free zucchini blueberry bread—a moist, flavorful, and health-conscious treat that combines the natural sweetness of blueberries with the subtle earthiness of zucchini. This bread is perfect for breakfast, a midday snack, or even dessert. Whether you're gluten-intolerant or simply looking to incorporate more vegetables into your diet, this recipe is a must-try.

Why Gluten-Free Zucchini Blueberry Bread?

Gluten-free diets have gained popularity not only among those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity but also among health-conscious individuals. Zucchini, a versatile summer squash, adds moisture and nutrients to baked goods without altering the flavor significantly. Blueberries, on the other hand, are packed with antioxidants and provide a burst of sweetness. Together, they create a bread that is not only gluten-free but also rich in vitamins, fiber, and flavor.

Ingredients You'll Need

Before diving into the recipe, let's take a look at the ingredients required to make this delightful bread:

Ingredient Quantity Notes
Gluten-free flour blend 2 cups Ensure it contains xanthan gum or add 1 tsp separately
Baking powder 1 tsp Ensure it's gluten-free
Baking soda 1/2 tsp
Salt 1/4 tsp
Cinnamon 1 tsp Optional, but adds warmth
Eggs 2 large Room temperature
Honey or maple syrup 1/2 cup For natural sweetness
Unsweetened applesauce 1/2 cup Adds moisture
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Zucchini 1 cup (grated) Drain excess moisture
Blueberries 1 cup Fresh or frozen

Step-by-Step Instructions

Follow these simple steps to create your gluten-free zucchini blueberry bread:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan.
  2. Prepare the Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon.
  3. Mix the Wet Ingredients: In another bowl, beat the eggs, then add the honey (or maple syrup), applesauce, and vanilla extract. Mix until well combined.
  4. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients: Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Avoid overmixing.
  5. Add Zucchini and Blueberries: Fold in the grated zucchini and blueberries gently. If using frozen blueberries, toss them in a tablespoon of gluten-free flour to prevent sinking.
  6. Bake: Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Cool and Serve: Allow the bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

Nutritional Benefits

This gluten-free zucchini blueberry bread is not only delicious but also packed with nutritional benefits:

  • Zucchini: Low in calories and high in fiber, zucchini supports digestion and provides essential vitamins like vitamin C and vitamin A.
  • Blueberries: Known as a superfood, blueberries are rich in antioxidants, which help combat oxidative stress and inflammation.
  • Gluten-Free Flour: Ideal for those with gluten intolerance, gluten-free flours like almond or oat flour add protein and healthy fats.
  • Natural Sweeteners: Using honey or maple syrup instead of refined sugar provides a healthier alternative while adding a touch of natural sweetness.

FAQs

Can I use almond flour instead of a gluten-free flour blend?

Yes, almond flour can be used, but it will change the texture of the bread. Almond flour tends to make baked goods denser, so you may need to adjust the baking time.

Can I substitute the eggs for a vegan option?

Absolutely! You can use flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tbsp water per egg) or chia eggs as a vegan alternative.

How should I store the bread?

Store the bread in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days, or refrigerate for up to a week. You can also freeze it for up to 3 months.
