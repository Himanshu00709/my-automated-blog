Gluten-Free Oatmeal Raisin Cookies: A Delicious and Healthy Treat

For those who love the comforting taste of oatmeal raisin cookies but need to avoid gluten, gluten-free oatmeal raisin cookies are a perfect solution. These cookies are not only delicious but also cater to dietary restrictions, making them a great option for anyone with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. In this blog post, we’ll explore the benefits of gluten-free oatmeal raisin cookies, provide a detailed recipe, and answer some frequently asked questions.

Why Choose Gluten-Free Oatmeal Raisin Cookies?

Gluten-free oatmeal raisin cookies are a healthier alternative to traditional cookies, especially for those who need to avoid gluten. Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, and it can cause digestive issues and other health problems for people with celiac disease or gluten intolerance. By using gluten-free oats and other gluten-free ingredients, you can enjoy the same delicious taste without the negative effects of gluten.

Additionally, gluten-free oatmeal raisin cookies can be made with wholesome ingredients like almond flour, coconut oil, and natural sweeteners, making them a healthier option for everyone. They are also a great source of fiber, which can help with digestion and keep you feeling full longer.

Ingredients for Gluten-Free Oatmeal Raisin Cookies

Here’s a list of ingredients you’ll need to make gluten-free oatmeal raisin cookies:

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free rolled oats 2 cups
Almond flour 1 cup
Coconut oil (melted) 1/2 cup
Maple syrup 1/2 cup
Raisins 1/2 cup
Egg 1
Vanilla extract 1 teaspoon
Baking soda 1/2 teaspoon
Cinnamon 1 teaspoon
Salt 1/4 teaspoon

Step-by-Step Recipe

Follow these steps to make your own gluten-free oatmeal raisin cookies:

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Mix the dry ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the gluten-free rolled oats, almond flour, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.
  3. Mix the wet ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted coconut oil, maple syrup, egg, and vanilla extract.
  4. Combine the ingredients: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until well combined. Fold in the raisins.
  5. Form the cookies: Scoop out tablespoon-sized portions of the dough and place them on the prepared baking sheet. Flatten each cookie slightly with the back of a spoon.
  6. Bake: Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown.
  7. Cool: Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Oatmeal Raisin Cookies

1. Are oats gluten-free?

Oats are naturally gluten-free, but they can be contaminated with gluten during processing. To ensure your oats are gluten-free, look for certified gluten-free oats.

2. Can I use a different type of flour?

Yes, you can substitute almond flour with other gluten-free flours like oat flour, coconut flour, or a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend. Keep in mind that the texture and taste may vary slightly.

3. Can I make these cookies vegan?

Yes, you can make these cookies vegan by substituting the egg with a flax egg (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tablespoons water) and using a vegan sweetener like agave syrup instead of maple syrup.

4. How should I store these cookies?

Store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days. You can also freeze them for up to 3 months.

5. Can I add other ingredients like nuts or chocolate chips?

Absolutely! Feel free to customize your cookies by adding nuts, chocolate chips, or even dried cranberries for a different flavor profile.

Conclusion

Gluten-free oatmeal raisin cookies are a delightful treat that everyone can enjoy, regardless of dietary restrictions. They are easy to make, packed with wholesome ingredients, and offer the same comforting taste as traditional oatmeal raisin cookies. Whether you’re baking for yourself or for a loved one with gluten sensitivity, these cookies are sure to be a hit. So, preheat your oven, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy a batch of delicious, gluten-free oatmeal raisin cookies!
