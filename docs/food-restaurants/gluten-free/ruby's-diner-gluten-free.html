Ruby's Diner, a beloved retro-style diner chain, has been a favorite among families and food enthusiasts for decades. Why Gluten-Free Matters

Gluten-free diets have become increasingly popular over the years, not just among those with celiac disease or gluten intolerance, but also among individuals seeking healthier eating habits. Gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, can cause adverse reactions in some people, ranging from mild discomfort to severe digestive issues. For those who must avoid gluten, dining out can often be a challenge. However, Ruby's Diner has recognized this need and has worked to provide a range of gluten-free options that are both delicious and safe.

How Ruby's Diner Ensures Gluten-Free Safety

For those with celiac disease or severe gluten intolerance, cross-contamination is a significant concern. Ruby's Diner takes this issue seriously and has implemented several measures to ensure the safety of their gluten-free offerings:

  • Dedicated Fryers: French fries and other fried items are cooked in dedicated fryers to avoid cross-contamination with gluten-containing foods.
  • Separate Preparation Areas: Gluten-free dishes are prepared in separate areas of the kitchen to minimize the risk of cross-contact.
  • Staff Training: Ruby's Diner staff are trained to handle gluten-free orders with care, ensuring that all precautions are taken to prevent cross-contamination.
  • Clear Labeling: The menu clearly labels gluten-free options, making it easier for customers to identify safe choices.
