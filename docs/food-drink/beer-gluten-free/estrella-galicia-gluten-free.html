The History of Estrella Galicia

Estrella Galicia is a Spanish beer brand with a rich history dating back to 1906. Founded by José María Rivera Corral in the region of Galicia, Spain, the brewery has grown to become one of the most recognized beer brands in the country. Known for its commitment to quality and tradition, Estrella Galicia has consistently delivered beers that are both flavorful and refreshing.

In response to the growing demand for gluten-free options, Estrella Galicia introduced its gluten-free beer, catering to those who suffer from celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. This move not only expanded their market reach but also demonstrated their dedication to inclusivity and innovation.

The Brewing Process of Estrella Galicia Gluten-Free

Brewing a gluten-free beer is no small feat, especially for a brand that prides itself on traditional brewing methods. Estrella Galicia's gluten-free beer is crafted using a combination of gluten-free grains, such as millet, buckwheat, and rice. These grains are carefully selected to ensure that the final product is free from gluten while maintaining the rich, full-bodied flavor that Estrella Galicia is known for.

The brewing process involves several key steps:

Step Description
1. Malting Gluten-free grains are malted to convert their starches into fermentable sugars.
2. Mashing The malted grains are mashed with water to extract the sugars, creating a sweet liquid known as wort.
3. Boiling The wort is boiled and hops are added for bitterness and aroma.
4. Fermentation The wort is cooled and yeast is added to begin the fermentation process, converting sugars into alcohol and carbon dioxide.
5. Filtration The beer is filtered to remove any remaining solids, ensuring a clear and crisp final product.
6. Packaging The beer is packaged in bottles or cans, ready for distribution and consumption.

Throughout the process, strict quality control measures are in place to ensure that the beer remains gluten-free, with regular testing conducted to verify gluten levels.
