The History of Omission Beer

Omission Beer was introduced in 2012 by the Craft Brew Alliance, a company known for its commitment to quality and innovation. The brand was created with the goal of providing a gluten-free beer option that doesn't compromise on taste. Omission Beer quickly gained popularity among beer enthusiasts and those with gluten sensitivities alike, thanks to its unique brewing process and dedication to quality.

What sets Omission Beer apart from other gluten-free beers is its use of traditional brewing ingredients, such as barley, which are typically high in gluten. However, through a proprietary process, the gluten is removed, making the beer safe for those with gluten sensitivities. This process has been rigorously tested and certified by the Gluten Intolerance Group (GIG), ensuring that each batch of Omission Beer meets strict gluten-free standards.

The Brewing Process

The brewing process of Omission Beer is a fascinating blend of traditional brewing techniques and modern innovation. Here's a step-by-step breakdown of how Omission Beer is made:

Step Description
1. Malting Barley is malted to convert starches into fermentable sugars.
2. Mashing The malted barley is mixed with water and heated to create a mash, which extracts the sugars.
3. Boiling The mash is boiled, and hops are added for flavor and aroma.
4. Fermentation Yeast is added to the boiled wort, converting sugars into alcohol and carbon dioxide.
5. Gluten Removal After fermentation, the beer undergoes a proprietary process to remove gluten, ensuring it meets gluten-free standards.
6. Packaging The beer is filtered, carbonated, and packaged for distribution.

This meticulous process ensures that Omission Beer retains the rich flavors and aromas of traditional beer while being safe for those with gluten sensitivities.
