Is Stella Cider Gluten-Free? A Comprehensive Guide

For those who are gluten-sensitive or have celiac disease, finding safe and enjoyable beverages can be a challenge. Cider, a popular alternative to beer, often comes up as a gluten-free option. But what about Stella Cider? Is it truly gluten-free? In this article, we’ll dive deep into the ingredients, production process, and certifications of Stella Cider to determine whether it’s a safe choice for those avoiding gluten.

What is Stella Cider?

Stella Cider is a product of the well-known Stella Artois brand, which is famous for its premium Belgian beer. However, Stella Cider is a different beverage altogether. It is a crisp, refreshing cider made from apples, offering a sweet and tangy flavor profile that appeals to a wide range of drinkers. Unlike beer, which is typically made from barley, wheat, or rye, cider is made from fermented apple juice, making it a natural candidate for being gluten-free.

Production Process

The production process of Stella Cider is another critical factor in determining its gluten-free status. Cider is typically made by fermenting apple juice with yeast, a process that does not involve gluten-containing grains. However, cross-contamination can occur if the cider is produced in a facility that also handles gluten-containing products.

Stella Cider is produced by Anheuser-Busch InBev, a large beverage company that produces a variety of alcoholic drinks, including beer. While the company takes precautions to prevent cross-contamination, there is always a risk when products are made in shared facilities. For those with celiac disease or severe gluten intolerance, this could be a concern.

Certifications and Labeling

One way to ensure that a product is gluten-free is to look for certifications from recognized organizations. Unfortunately, Stella Cider does not currently carry a gluten-free certification from organizations such as the Gluten-Free Certification Organization (GFCO) or the Celiac Support Association (CSA). However, the absence of certification does not necessarily mean that the product contains gluten.

In the United States, the Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau (TTB) regulates the labeling of alcoholic beverages. According to TTB guidelines, a product can be labeled as gluten-free if it contains less than 20 parts per million (ppm) of gluten. While Stella Cider does not explicitly state that it is gluten-free on its label, the ingredients and production process suggest that it likely meets this threshold.

2. Does Stella Cider contain barley or wheat?

No, Stella Cider is made from apple juice and does not contain barley, wheat, or other gluten-containing grains. However, the risk of cross-contamination exists due to shared production facilities.
