What Are Aguas Frescas?

Aguas frescas, which translates to "fresh waters," are traditional Mexican beverages made by blending fruits, grains, or seeds with water and sugar. These drinks are known for their refreshing qualities and are often enjoyed during hot weather. Common flavors include horchata (made from rice and cinnamon), tamarind, hibiscus, and various fruit combinations like watermelon, pineapple, and cucumber. Aguas frescas are typically non-alcoholic, but Modelo has taken this concept to the next level by introducing a spiked version that retains the essence of the traditional drink while adding a subtle kick of alcohol.

The Rise of Gluten-Free Beverages
