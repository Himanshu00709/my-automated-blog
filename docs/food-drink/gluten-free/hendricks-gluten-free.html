Hendrick's Gluten-Free: A Comprehensive Guide

In recent years, the demand for gluten-free products has surged, driven by an increasing awareness of celiac disease, gluten sensitivity, and a general preference for healthier lifestyle choices. What Makes Hendrick's Gin Gluten-Free?

Hendrick's Gin is crafted using a unique distillation process that ensures the final product is free from gluten. The primary ingredients in Hendrick's Gin include juniper berries, coriander, citrus peel, and a blend of botanicals. These ingredients are naturally gluten-free, and the distillation process further removes any trace of gluten proteins, making the gin safe for those with gluten intolerance or celiac disease.

It's important to note that while the base spirit used in Hendrick's Gin is typically derived from grains, the distillation process effectively eliminates gluten proteins. According to the FDA, distilled spirits are considered gluten-free if they contain less than 20 parts per million (ppm) of gluten, a threshold that Hendrick's Gin comfortably meets.

The Distillation Process: Ensuring Gluten-Free Quality

The distillation process is a critical factor in determining whether a spirit is gluten-free. During distillation, the liquid is heated to create vapor, which is then condensed back into liquid form. This process separates the alcohol from the solid components, including gluten proteins, which are left behind in the still. As a result, the final distilled product is free from gluten, even if it was originally derived from gluten-containing grains.

Hendrick's Gin undergoes a meticulous distillation process that ensures the highest quality and purity. The gin is distilled in small batches using a combination of traditional pot stills and a unique Carter-Head still, which allows for precise control over the infusion of botanicals. This careful process not only enhances the flavor profile of the gin but also guarantees its gluten-free status.
