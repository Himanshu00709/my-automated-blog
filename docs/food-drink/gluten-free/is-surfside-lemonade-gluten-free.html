Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and their derivatives. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to more severe autoimmune reactions. Therefore, it’s crucial for these individuals to ensure that the products they consume are free from gluten contamination.

Manufacturing Process and Cross-Contamination

Even if the ingredients in a product are gluten-free, cross-contamination during the manufacturing process can introduce gluten. This is a significant concern for individuals with celiac disease, as even trace amounts of gluten can trigger symptoms. Surfside Lemonade is produced in facilities that also handle other products, some of which may contain gluten. However, the company has implemented strict protocols to minimize the risk of cross-contamination.

Surfside Lemonade is manufactured in a facility that follows Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP), which include thorough cleaning procedures between production runs. Additionally, the company conducts regular testing to ensure that their products meet gluten-free standards. While the risk of cross-contamination is low, it’s always a good idea for individuals with severe gluten sensitivity to contact the manufacturer directly for the most up-to-date information.
