Understanding Gluten and Its Presence in Spirits

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and other grains. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to adverse health effects. While distilled spirits are generally considered gluten-free due to the distillation process, some people remain cautious about the potential for gluten contamination.

Distillation involves heating a liquid to create vapor and then cooling it to produce a purified liquid. This process typically removes gluten proteins, making the final product safe for those with gluten sensitivities. However, cross-contamination can occur during production or bottling, especially if the spirit is aged in barrels that previously contained gluten-containing products.
