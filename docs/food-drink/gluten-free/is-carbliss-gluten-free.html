Understanding Gluten and Gluten-Free Diets

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease, gluten sensitivity, or wheat allergy, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, including digestive issues, skin rashes, and even neurological problems. As a result, many people opt for gluten-free diets to avoid these adverse effects.

Gluten-free diets involve avoiding all foods and beverages that contain gluten. This includes not only obvious sources like bread and pasta but also less obvious ones like sauces, dressings, and alcoholic beverages. Therefore, it is crucial for individuals following a gluten-free diet to carefully scrutinize the ingredients and manufacturing processes of the products they consume.

Manufacturing Process and Cross-Contamination

Even if a product is made with gluten-free ingredients, cross-contamination during the manufacturing process can introduce gluten into the final product. Cross-contamination can occur if the same equipment is used to process both gluten-containing and gluten-free products, or if the facility is not properly cleaned between production runs.

Carbliss is manufactured in a facility that takes strict measures to prevent cross-contamination.
