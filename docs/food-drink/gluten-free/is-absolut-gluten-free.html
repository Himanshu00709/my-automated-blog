Absolut Vodka is made from winter wheat, which does contain gluten. However, the distillation process used to produce vodka effectively removes gluten proteins. During distillation, the liquid is heated to create vapor, which is then condensed back into liquid form. This process separates the alcohol from the gluten proteins, resulting in a product that is free from gluten.

According to the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA), distilled spirits like vodka are generally considered gluten-free, even if they are made from gluten-containing grains.
