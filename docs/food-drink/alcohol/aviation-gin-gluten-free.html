What Makes Aviation Gin Gluten-Free?

Aviation Gin is crafted using a meticulous distillation process that ensures it is gluten-free. The primary ingredient in gin is typically grain, which contains gluten. However, during the distillation process, the gluten proteins are removed, leaving behind a spirit that is safe for those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease. Aviation Gin is made from a blend of botanicals, including juniper, lavender, cardamom, and sarsaparilla, which contribute to its distinctive flavor profile.

It's important to note that while Aviation Gin is gluten-free, individuals with severe gluten allergies should always check with the manufacturer or consult their healthcare provider before consuming any spirit. The distillation process generally removes gluten, but cross-contamination can occur during production or bottling.

The Distillation Process: A Closer Look

The distillation process is key to understanding why Aviation Gin is gluten-free. Here's a step-by-step breakdown of how it works:

Step Description
1. Fermentation The base ingredients, typically grains like wheat or barley, are fermented to produce alcohol. This step creates a liquid known as "wash," which contains alcohol and gluten proteins.
2. Distillation The wash is heated in a still, causing the alcohol to evaporate. The vapor is then condensed back into liquid form. Gluten proteins, which are larger molecules, do not evaporate and are left behind in the still.
3. Botanicals Infusion During or after distillation, botanicals are added to the spirit to impart flavor. Aviation Gin uses a unique blend of botanicals that give it its signature taste.
4. Filtration The final product is filtered to remove any remaining impurities, ensuring a smooth and clean finish.

This process ensures that the final product is free from gluten, making Aviation Gin a safe choice for those with gluten sensitivities.

FAQs About Aviation Gin and Gluten

Is Aviation Gin completely gluten-free?

Yes, Aviation Gin is considered gluten-free due to the distillation process, which removes gluten proteins. However, individuals with severe gluten allergies should exercise caution and consult with a healthcare provider.

Can I drink Aviation Gin if I have celiac disease?

Most people with celiac disease can safely consume distilled spirits like Aviation Gin, as the gluten proteins are removed during distillation. However, it's always best to consult with a healthcare professional if you have concerns.

What makes Aviation Gin different from other gins?

Aviation Gin is known for its balanced flavor profile, which is less juniper-forward than traditional gins. It also uses a unique blend of botanicals, including lavender and sarsaparilla, which give it a distinctive taste.

How should I store Aviation Gin?

Aviation Gin should be stored in a cool, dark place, away from direct sunlight. Once opened, it can be kept for several months without losing its flavor, though it's best enjoyed fresh.
