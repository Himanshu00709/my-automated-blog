Understanding Gluten and Its Presence in Alcoholic Beverages

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to severe health issues, including digestive problems, fatigue, and long-term damage to the small intestine. Alcoholic beverages, particularly those made from grains, can sometimes contain gluten. However, the distillation process used in producing spirits like whiskey can remove gluten proteins, making the final product gluten-free.

It’s important to note that while the distillation process removes gluten, cross-contamination can occur during production or packaging. Additionally, some flavored alcoholic beverages may contain gluten-based additives or flavorings. Therefore, it’s crucial to examine the specific product in question.
