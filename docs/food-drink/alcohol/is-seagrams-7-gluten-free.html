Understanding Gluten and Its Presence in Alcohol

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and their derivatives. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to severe health issues, including digestive problems, skin rashes, and long-term damage to the small intestine.

When it comes to alcoholic beverages, gluten can be present in products made from gluten-containing grains. However, the distillation process typically removes gluten proteins, making many distilled spirits safe for those with gluten sensitivities. But what about Seagram's 7?

Is Seagram's 7 Gluten-Free?

Seagram's 7 Crown is a blended whiskey, which means it is made from a mixture of different grains, including corn, rye, and barley. Barley is a gluten-containing grain, which raises concerns for those avoiding gluten. However, the distillation process plays a crucial role in determining whether the final product is gluten-free.

During distillation, the liquid is heated to create vapor, which is then cooled and condensed back into liquid form. This process separates the alcohol from the solid components, including gluten proteins. As a result, most distilled spirits, including whiskey, are considered gluten-free by the time they reach the bottle.

However, it's important to note that some individuals with extreme gluten sensitivity or celiac disease may still react to trace amounts of gluten that could be present due to cross-contamination during production or bottling. Additionally, some whiskeys may add flavorings or other ingredients after distillation that could contain gluten.

Gluten Content in Seagram's 7
