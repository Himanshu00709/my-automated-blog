Understanding Gluten and Whiskey

Gluten is a group of proteins found in grains like wheat, barley, and rye. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to more severe health complications. Traditional whiskey is typically made from grains that contain gluten, which raises concerns for those who need to avoid gluten in their diet.

However, the distillation process used in whiskey production can remove gluten proteins, making some whiskeys safe for those with gluten sensitivities. But the key question is whether Skrewball Peanut Butter Whiskey follows this process and uses gluten-free ingredients.

Ingredients and Production Process

Skrewball Peanut Butter Whiskey is made from a blend of whiskey and natural peanut butter flavors. The whiskey base is distilled from corn, which is naturally gluten-free. Corn is a common base for many American whiskeys, and it’s a safe choice for those avoiding gluten.

During the distillation process, the gluten proteins are removed, leaving behind a spirit that is technically gluten-free. However, it’s important to note that some individuals with extreme gluten sensitivities or celiac disease may still react to trace amounts of gluten that could be present due to cross-contamination during production or bottling.

Is Skrewball Peanut Butter Whiskey Certified Gluten-Free?

While Skrewball Peanut Butter Whiskey is made from gluten-free ingredients and undergoes a distillation process that removes gluten, it is not certified gluten-free by any official organization. This means that while the product is likely safe for most people with gluten sensitivities, those with celiac disease should exercise caution and consult with their healthcare provider before consuming it.
