Understanding Gluten in Spirits

Gluten is a protein found in certain grains, including wheat, barley, and rye. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to more severe health complications. As a result, it is crucial for these individuals to carefully select their food and beverages to avoid gluten.

When it comes to spirits, the distillation process plays a key role in determining whether a product is gluten-free. Distillation involves heating a liquid to create vapor and then cooling the vapor to produce a purified liquid. During this process, the gluten proteins are left behind in the original mixture, resulting in a gluten-free distillate.

However, it is important to note that not all spirits are created equal. Some spirits may contain added ingredients or flavorings that introduce gluten into the final product. Therefore, it is essential to read labels and verify the gluten-free status of any spirit before consumption.
