Understanding Gluten in Whiskey

Gluten is a protein found in grains such as wheat, barley, and rye. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to more severe health complications. Traditional whiskeys are typically made from these gluten-containing grains, which raises concerns about their safety for gluten-sensitive individuals.

However, the distillation process used to produce whiskey can remove gluten proteins, making the final product potentially safe for those with gluten sensitivities. The key factor is whether the whiskey is made from gluten-containing grains and whether any gluten remains after distillation.
