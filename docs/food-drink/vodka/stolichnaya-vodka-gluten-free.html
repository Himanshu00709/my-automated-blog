Understanding Gluten and Vodka Production

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and other grains. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to severe health issues, including digestive problems, skin rashes, and even long-term damage to the intestines. Vodka, traditionally made from grains, raises concerns for those who need to avoid gluten.

However, the distillation process plays a significant role in determining whether a vodka is gluten-free. During distillation, the liquid is heated to create vapor, which is then condensed back into liquid form. This process typically removes gluten proteins, as they are too large to pass through the distillation process. However, cross-contamination can occur if the vodka is produced in facilities that also handle gluten-containing products.
