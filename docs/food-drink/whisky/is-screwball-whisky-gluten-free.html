Understanding Gluten and Its Presence in Whisky

Gluten is a group of proteins found in grains like wheat, barley, and rye. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to serious health issues. Traditional whisky is typically made from grains that contain gluten, which raises concerns for those avoiding gluten in their diet.

However, the distillation process plays a crucial role in determining whether a whisky is gluten-free. During distillation, the liquid is heated, and the alcohol vapors are collected and condensed. Gluten proteins are not volatile, meaning they do not evaporate and are left behind in the distillation process. This theoretically makes distilled spirits gluten-free, even if they are made from gluten-containing grains.

Is Screwball Whisky Gluten-Free?

Given that Screwball Whisky contains barley, a gluten-containing grain, it’s natural to question whether the final product is safe for those avoiding gluten. However, as mentioned earlier, the distillation process typically removes gluten proteins from the final product. This means that even though Screwball Whisky is made from barley, the distillation process should eliminate gluten.

That said, it’s important to note that some individuals with celiac disease or severe gluten sensitivity may still react to distilled spirits made from gluten-containing grains. This is due to the potential for trace amounts of gluten to remain in the final product, or cross-contamination during production.
