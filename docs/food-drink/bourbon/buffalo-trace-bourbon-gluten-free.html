What Makes Buffalo Trace Bourbon Unique?

Buffalo Trace Bourbon is produced at the Buffalo Trace Distillery, one of the oldest continuously operating distilleries in the United States. The distillery is located in Frankfort, Kentucky, and has a rich history dating back to the late 18th century. The bourbon is crafted using a unique mash bill that includes corn, rye, and malted barley. Is Buffalo Trace Bourbon Gluten-Free?

For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, the gluten content in alcoholic beverages is a significant concern. Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, which are common ingredients in many alcoholic beverages. However, the distillation process used in the production of bourbon, including Buffalo Trace Bourbon, removes gluten proteins from the final product.

According to the Celiac Disease Foundation, distilled spirits, including bourbon, are generally considered gluten-free, even if they are made from gluten-containing grains. This is because the distillation process separates the alcohol from the proteins, including gluten. As a result, the final product contains negligible amounts of gluten, making it safe for most people with gluten sensitivities.

Buffalo Trace Bourbon is made from a mash bill that includes corn, rye, and malted barley. While barley contains gluten, the distillation process ensures that the gluten is removed, making Buffalo Trace Bourbon a gluten-free option. However, it’s important to note that individuals with severe gluten sensitivities or celiac disease should consult with their healthcare provider before consuming any distilled spirits.
