FAQs About Gluten-Free Beer of the Month Clubs

1. What makes a beer gluten-free?

Gluten-free beers are made without barley, wheat, or rye, which are the primary sources of gluten in traditional beers. Instead, they use alternative grains like sorghum, millet, rice, or corn. Some breweries also use enzymes to break down gluten in traditional grains, making the beer safe for those with gluten sensitivities.

2. Are gluten-free beers less flavorful than traditional beers?

Not at all! Many gluten-free beers are crafted with the same care and attention to detail as traditional beers. With the rise of craft brewing, gluten-free beers now come in a wide range of styles and flavors, from hoppy IPAs to rich stouts, ensuring there’s something for every palate.

3. Can I join a gluten-free beer of the month club if I’m not gluten-intolerant?

Absolutely! Gluten-free beers are enjoyed by a wide range of people, not just those with dietary restrictions. If you’re curious about trying new beers or want to support small breweries, a gluten-free beer of the month club is a great option.

4. How do I know if a beer is truly gluten-free?

Look for beers that are certified gluten-free by organizations like the Gluten-Free Certification Organization (GFCO). These certifications ensure that the beer meets strict gluten-free standards and is safe for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivities.
