Omission Gluten-Free Beer: A Comprehensive Guide

In recent years, the demand for gluten-free products has skyrocketed, and the beer industry is no exception. For those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, finding a beer that doesn't compromise on taste or quality can be a challenge. Enter Omission Gluten-Free Beer, a brand that has made waves in the gluten-free beer market. In this article, we'll dive deep into what makes Omission stand out, its brewing process, and why it might be the perfect choice for those avoiding gluten.

What is Omission Gluten-Free Beer?

Omission Gluten-Free Beer is a product of the Craft Brew Alliance, specifically designed for individuals who need or choose to avoid gluten. Unlike many other gluten-free beers that use alternative grains like sorghum or rice, Omission is brewed with traditional barley and then undergoes a proprietary process to remove gluten. This allows the beer to retain the classic flavors and characteristics of traditional beer while being safe for those with gluten sensitivities.

Omission offers a variety of styles, including a Pale Ale, Lager, IPA, and Ultimate Light Golden Ale. Each style is crafted to cater to different taste preferences, ensuring that there's something for everyone.

The Brewing Process: How Omission Removes Gluten

One of the most intriguing aspects of Omission Gluten-Free Beer is its brewing process. The beer starts with traditional ingredients, including barley, which contains gluten. However, after the brewing process, the beer is treated with an enzyme called Brewers Clarex. This enzyme breaks down the gluten proteins into smaller fragments, reducing the gluten content to below 20 parts per million (ppm), which is the threshold set by the FDA for a product to be labeled gluten-free.

It's important to note that while Omission tests its beers to ensure they meet the gluten-free standard, the brand is transparent about the fact that the beer is brewed with barley. For individuals with severe gluten allergies or celiac disease, this might be a point of concern, and it's always recommended to consult with a healthcare provider before consuming.
