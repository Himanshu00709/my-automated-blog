What is Gluten-Free Beer?

Gluten-free beer is a type of beer that is brewed without the use of gluten-containing grains such as barley, wheat, or rye. Instead, these beers are made from alternative grains like sorghum, rice, millet, or corn. The absence of gluten makes these beers safe for individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. However, brewing a gluten-free beer that retains the flavor and texture of traditional beer is no easy task. This is where Daura Damm excels, offering a beer that not only meets gluten-free standards but also delivers a rich, satisfying taste.

The Brewing Process of Daura Damm

Daura Damm is brewed by the Spanish company Estrella Damm, which has been producing beer since 1876. The brewing process of Daura Damm is unique in that it starts with traditional barley malt, which contains gluten. However, through a proprietary process, the gluten content is reduced to less than 6 parts per million (ppm), which is well below the threshold of 20 ppm considered safe for most people with celiac disease. This process involves the use of enzymes that break down the gluten proteins, making the beer safe for consumption without compromising on flavor.

The result is a beer that has the same aroma, body, and taste as a traditional lager, but with the added benefit of being gluten-free. Daura Damm is available in several varieties, including Daura Original, Daura Marzen, and Daura IPA, each offering a unique flavor profile to suit different preferences.
