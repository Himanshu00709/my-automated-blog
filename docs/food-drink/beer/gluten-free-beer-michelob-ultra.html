What is Gluten-Free Beer?

Gluten-free beer is a type of beer that is brewed without the use of gluten-containing grains such as wheat, barley, or rye. Instead, these beers are made from alternative grains like sorghum, rice, corn, or millet. The absence of gluten makes these beers suitable for individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. However, not all gluten-free beers are created equal, and the taste can vary significantly depending on the brewing process and ingredients used.

Ingredients and Brewing Process

The gluten-free version of Michelob Ultra is made using a combination of traditional brewing ingredients and gluten-free grains. The brewing process involves fermenting these grains to produce a light, crisp beer with a clean finish. The gluten is removed during the brewing process, ensuring that the final product contains less than 20 parts per million (ppm) of gluten, which is the threshold for being considered gluten-free.
