In recent years, the demand for gluten-free products has skyrocketed, and the beer industry is no exception. What is Redbridge Beer?

Redbridge is a gluten-free beer brewed by Anheuser-Busch, one of the largest and most well-known beer producers in the world. Launched in 2006, Redbridge was one of the first gluten-free beers to hit the mainstream market. It is crafted from sorghum, a gluten-free grain, making it safe for those with celiac disease or gluten intolerance. Despite being gluten-free, Redbridge doesn't skimp on flavor, offering a rich, malty taste that appeals to both gluten-free and traditional beer drinkers alike.

The Brewing Process of Redbridge

Redbridge's brewing process is unique, primarily because it uses sorghum instead of barley or wheat, which are common in traditional beers. Sorghum is a naturally gluten-free grain that has been used for centuries in various cultures for brewing and cooking. The brewing process involves several key steps:

  • Mashing: The sorghum is mixed with water and heated to convert the starches into fermentable sugars.
  • Boiling: The mixture is boiled, and hops are added to provide bitterness and aroma.
  • Fermentation: Yeast is added to the cooled wort, converting the sugars into alcohol and carbon dioxide.
  • Conditioning: The beer is allowed to mature, developing its full flavor profile.

This process ensures that Redbridge maintains a consistent quality and taste, making it a reliable choice for gluten-free beer enthusiasts.
