The Origins of Daura Gluten-Free Beer

Daura Gluten-Free Beer is a product of the Estrella Damm brewery, a renowned Spanish brewery with a rich history dating back to 1876. The brewery is known for its commitment to quality and innovation, and Daura is a testament to that. The beer was specifically developed to cater to individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, offering them a safe and enjoyable beer-drinking experience without compromising on taste.

Daura is brewed using barley, but the gluten is removed during the brewing process to ensure it meets the strict gluten-free standards. The result is a beer that is not only safe for those with gluten intolerance but also delicious and satisfying for all beer lovers.

The Brewing Process

The brewing process of Daura Gluten-Free Beer is a meticulous one, designed to ensure the highest quality and safety standards. Here’s a step-by-step breakdown of how Daura is made:

Step Description
1. Malting Barley is soaked in water and allowed to germinate, producing enzymes that convert starches into fermentable sugars.
2. Mashing The malted barley is mixed with hot water to create a mash, which is then heated to activate the enzymes and convert the starches into sugars.
3. Lautering The liquid part of the mash, known as wort, is separated from the grain husks.
4. Boiling The wort is boiled and hops are added for bitterness, flavor, and aroma.
5. Fermentation The wort is cooled and yeast is added to begin the fermentation process, where sugars are converted into alcohol and carbon dioxide.
6. Gluten Removal After fermentation, the beer undergoes a specialized process to remove gluten, ensuring it meets gluten-free standards.
7. Packaging The beer is filtered, carbonated, and packaged for distribution.

This careful brewing process ensures that Daura Gluten-Free Beer retains the traditional flavors of a classic lager while being safe for those with gluten sensitivities.
