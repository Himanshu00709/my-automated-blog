The History of Estrella Galicia

Estrella Galicia, a renowned Spanish brewery, has been crafting high-quality beers since 1906. Founded by José María Rivera Corral in A Coruña, Galicia, the brewery has always been committed to using the finest ingredients and traditional brewing methods. Over the years, Estrella Galicia has expanded its product line to cater to diverse consumer needs, including the introduction of gluten-free beer options.

What Makes Estrella Galicia Gluten-Free?

Gluten-free beer is made by either using gluten-free grains or by removing gluten from barley or wheat during the brewing process. Estrella Galicia's gluten-free beer is crafted using the latter method, ensuring that the final product contains less than 20 parts per million (ppm) of gluten, which is the threshold considered safe for most people with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity.

The brewing process involves the use of enzymes that break down the gluten proteins into smaller fragments, making them safe for consumption by those with gluten-related disorders. This method allows Estrella Galicia to maintain the traditional flavor profile of their beers while making them accessible to a broader audience.
