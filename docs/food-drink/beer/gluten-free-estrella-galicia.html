In recent years, the demand for gluten-free products has skyrocketed, driven by the increasing awareness of celiac disease and gluten intolerance. The History of Estrella Galicia

Estrella Galicia is a Spanish beer brand that has been brewing since 1906. Founded by José María Rivera Corral in A Coruña, Galicia, the brand has grown to become one of Spain's most beloved beers. Known for its high-quality ingredients and traditional brewing methods, Estrella Galicia has earned a reputation for excellence. The introduction of their gluten-free beer is a testament to their commitment to innovation and inclusivity, ensuring that everyone can enjoy their products, regardless of dietary restrictions.

What Makes Estrella Galicia Gluten-Free?

Gluten-free beers are crafted to eliminate or reduce gluten content to levels that are safe for individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. Estrella Galicia achieves this by using alternative grains such as rice, corn, or millet, which do not contain gluten. The brewing process is meticulously controlled to prevent cross-contamination, ensuring that the final product meets the stringent standards required for gluten-free certification.

The Brewing Process

The brewing process for gluten-free Estrella Galicia is a carefully orchestrated symphony of science and tradition. The grains are milled and mashed to extract fermentable sugars, which are then boiled with hops to impart bitterness and aroma. The wort is cooled and fermented with specially selected yeast strains that do not produce gluten. The beer is then filtered to remove any remaining gluten particles, ensuring that the final product is safe for consumption by those with gluten intolerance.
