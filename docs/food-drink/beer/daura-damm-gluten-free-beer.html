What is Daura Damm Gluten-Free Beer?

Daura Damm is a gluten-free beer produced by the Spanish brewery Estrella Damm. It was specifically crafted to cater to individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. Unlike many other gluten-free beers that are made from alternative grains like sorghum or rice, Daura Damm is brewed from barley, which is traditionally a gluten-containing grain. However, through a specialized brewing process, the gluten content is reduced to less than 6 parts per million (ppm), making it safe for most people with gluten intolerance.

The Brewing Process: How Daura Damm Achieves Gluten-Free Status

The brewing process of Daura Damm is a fascinating blend of traditional brewing techniques and modern technology. Here’s a step-by-step breakdown of how this gluten-free beer is made:

Step Description
1. Malting Barley is malted to convert starches into fermentable sugars.
2. Mashing The malted barley is mashed with water to extract the sugars.
3. Fermentation Yeast is added to the wort (the liquid extracted from the mashing process) to ferment the sugars into alcohol.
4. Gluten Reduction After fermentation, the beer undergoes a specialized filtration process to remove gluten proteins, reducing the gluten content to less than 6 ppm.
5. Packaging The final product is packaged and ready for distribution.

This meticulous process ensures that Daura Damm retains the rich flavors of traditional beer while being safe for those who need to avoid gluten.
