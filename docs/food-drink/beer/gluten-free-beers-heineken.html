What Makes a Beer Gluten-Free?

Before diving into Heineken's offerings, it's essential to understand what makes a beer gluten-free. Traditional beers are brewed using barley, wheat, or rye, all of which contain gluten. Gluten-free beers, on the other hand, are made using alternative grains such as sorghum, rice, millet, or corn. These grains do not contain gluten, making the beer safe for those with gluten-related disorders.

However, brewing gluten-free beer is not without its challenges. The absence of gluten can affect the beer's texture, mouthfeel, and flavor. Brewers must carefully select ingredients and adjust their brewing techniques to ensure that the final product is both gluten-free and delicious.

Ingredients and Brewing Process

Heineken's gluten-free beer is made using a combination of gluten-free grains, including rice and corn. The brewing process involves several steps to ensure that the beer is free from gluten contamination:

  • Selection of Gluten-Free Grains: Only grains that are naturally gluten-free are used in the brewing process.
  • Dedicated Brewing Equipment: To avoid cross-contamination, Heineken uses separate brewing equipment for its gluten-free beer.
  • Rigorous Testing: The final product is tested to ensure that it meets the strict gluten-free standards set by regulatory bodies.
