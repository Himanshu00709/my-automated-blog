Gluten-Free Beer: Bud Light's Contribution to a Healthier Lifestyle

In recent years, the demand for gluten-free products has skyrocketed, driven by an increasing awareness of gluten-related health issues such as celiac disease and gluten sensitivity. What is Gluten-Free Beer?

Gluten-free beer is a type of beer that is brewed without the use of gluten-containing grains such as barley, wheat, or rye. Instead, it is made from alternative grains like sorghum, rice, corn, or millet. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, including digestive issues, fatigue, and even long-term health complications. Gluten-free beer provides a safe and enjoyable alternative for those who need to avoid gluten but still want to enjoy a cold brew.

Creating a gluten-free beer that still tastes great is no small feat. Bud Light achieves this by using a combination of alternative grains and a specialized brewing process. The primary grain used in Bud Light's gluten-free beer is sorghum, a naturally gluten-free grain that provides a mild, slightly sweet flavor. Additionally, Bud Light employs rigorous testing protocols to verify that their gluten-free beer contains less than 20 parts per million (ppm) of gluten, which is the threshold set by the FDA for a product to be labeled as gluten-free.
