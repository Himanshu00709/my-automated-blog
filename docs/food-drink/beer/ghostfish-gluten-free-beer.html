The Rise of Ghostfish Brewing Company

Ghostfish Brewing Company was founded in 2015 in Seattle, Washington, by Brian Thiel, Randy Schroeder, and Jason Yerger. The founders were driven by a shared passion for craft beer and a desire to create high-quality gluten-free options for those who couldn't enjoy traditional beers due to gluten intolerance or celiac disease. Ghostfish quickly gained recognition for its innovative approach to brewing, using alternative grains like millet, buckwheat, and brown rice to create beers that are not only gluten-free but also rich in flavor and complexity.

What sets Ghostfish apart from other gluten-free breweries is its commitment to quality and authenticity. The brewery adheres to strict gluten-free standards, ensuring that every batch of beer is safe for those with gluten sensitivities. Additionally, Ghostfish has received numerous awards and accolades, solidifying its reputation as a leader in the gluten-free beer market.

The Brewing Process: Crafting Gluten-Free Excellence

Ghostfish Brewing Company takes a unique approach to brewing gluten-free beer, using a combination of traditional brewing techniques and innovative ingredients. The brewery avoids barley, wheat, and rye—common sources of gluten in beer—and instead relies on alternative grains like millet, buckwheat, and brown rice. These grains are carefully selected for their ability to impart flavor, body, and mouthfeel to the beer, ensuring that the final product is as close to traditional beer as possible.

The brewing process begins with the milling of the grains, followed by mashing, where the grains are mixed with hot water to extract fermentable sugars. The wort (the liquid extracted from the mashing process) is then boiled, and hops are added for bitterness, flavor, and aroma. After boiling, the wort is cooled and transferred to fermentation tanks, where yeast is added to convert the sugars into alcohol. The beer is then conditioned, carbonated, and packaged, ready to be enjoyed by beer lovers everywhere.
