What is Garage Beer Gluten-Free?

Garage Beer Gluten-Free is a craft beer that caters to individuals with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease. Unlike traditional beers that are brewed with barley or wheat, Garage Beer uses alternative grains such as sorghum, rice, or millet to create a gluten-free beverage. The result is a beer that not only meets the dietary needs of gluten-intolerant individuals but also delivers on taste and quality.

The brewing process of Garage Beer Gluten-Free is meticulous and designed to ensure that the final product is completely free of gluten. Here’s a step-by-step breakdown of how this unique beer is made:

Step Description

2. Mashing The grains are mashed to extract fermentable sugars, which will later be converted into alcohol.
3. Boiling The mash is boiled, and hops are added to impart bitterness and aroma to the beer.
4. Fermentation Yeast is added to the cooled wort, initiating the fermentation process where sugars are converted into alcohol and carbon dioxide.
5. Filtration The beer is filtered to remove any remaining solids, ensuring a clear and crisp final product.
6. Packaging The beer is packaged in gluten-free facilities to prevent any cross-contamination.
