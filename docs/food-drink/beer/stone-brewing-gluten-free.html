What is Gluten-Free Beer?

Gluten-free beer is brewed without the use of gluten-containing grains such as barley, wheat, or rye. Instead, these beers are made with alternative grains like sorghum, rice, millet, or even gluten-free oats. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, gluten-free beer provides a safe and enjoyable way to indulge in the craft beer experience without the adverse health effects.

Stone Brewing, known for its adventurous spirit, has ventured into the gluten-free beer market, offering options that cater to this growing demographic. Let’s explore what they have to offer.

The Brewing Process: How Stone Brewing Achieves Gluten-Reduced Beers

Stone Brewing’s approach to creating gluten-reduced beers involves a combination of innovative brewing techniques and high-quality ingredients. Here’s a closer look at their process:

1. Ingredient Selection

Stone Brewing carefully selects grains that are naturally low in gluten or uses alternative grains to create their gluten-reduced beers. While traditional beers rely on barley and wheat, Stone Brewing incorporates grains like rice and oats to achieve a similar flavor profile without the gluten content.

2. Use of Clarex Enzyme

One of the key components in Stone Brewing’s gluten-reduced beers is the use of Clarex, an enzyme that breaks down gluten proteins during the brewing process. This enzyme reduces the gluten content to levels that are typically below 20 parts per million (ppm), which is the threshold for gluten-free labeling in many countries.

3. Rigorous Testing

To ensure the safety and quality of their gluten-reduced beers, Stone Brewing conducts rigorous testing throughout the brewing process. This includes testing the raw ingredients, the beer during fermentation, and the final product to confirm that the gluten levels meet the required standards.

FAQs About Stone Brewing Gluten-Free Beers

1. Are Stone Brewing beers completely gluten-free?

No, Stone Brewing’s beers are gluten-reduced, not completely gluten-free. They use an enzyme to break down gluten proteins, but trace amounts may still be present.

2. Can people with celiac disease drink Stone Brewing gluten-reduced beers?

Individuals with celiac disease should consult with a healthcare professional before consuming gluten-reduced beers. While the gluten levels are low, they may not be safe for everyone with celiac disease.

3. What is the difference between gluten-free and gluten-reduced beer?

Gluten-free beer is made without any gluten-containing ingredients, while gluten-reduced beer is made with gluten-containing grains but treated with an enzyme to reduce the gluten content.

4. Where can I buy Stone Brewing gluten-reduced beers?

Stone Brewing gluten-reduced beers are available at select retailers, bars, and restaurants. You can also check their website for availability in your area.
