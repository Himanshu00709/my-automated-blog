The History of Estrella Damm Daura

Estrella Damm Daura is produced by the renowned Spanish brewery, Damm. Established in 1876, Damm has a long history of brewing excellence. The company decided to venture into the gluten-free market to cater to the growing demand for high-quality gluten-free beverages. Daura was introduced in 2006 and has since become a favorite among those who need or prefer gluten-free options.

What sets Daura apart is its unique brewing process. Unlike many gluten-free beers that use alternative grains like sorghum or rice, Daura is made from barley, a traditional beer ingredient. The gluten is then removed through a specialized process, ensuring that the final product contains less than 6 parts per million (ppm) of gluten, well below the threshold for gluten-free certification.

The Brewing Process

The brewing process of Estrella Damm Daura is a fascinating blend of tradition and innovation. Here's a step-by-step breakdown:

Step Description
1. Malting Barley grains are soaked in water and allowed to germinate, producing enzymes that convert starches into fermentable sugars.
2. Mashing The malted barley is mixed with hot water to extract the sugars, creating a sweet liquid known as wort.
3. Boiling The wort is boiled and hops are added for bitterness and aroma.
4. Fermentation Yeast is added to the cooled wort, converting the sugars into alcohol and carbon dioxide.
5. Gluten Removal After fermentation, the beer undergoes a specialized process to remove gluten, ensuring it meets gluten-free standards.
6. Packaging The beer is filtered, carbonated, and packaged for distribution.
