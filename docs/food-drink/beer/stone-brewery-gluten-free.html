Understanding Gluten-Free Beer

Gluten-free beer is crafted to cater to individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. Traditional beer is made from barley, wheat, or rye, all of which contain gluten. Gluten-free beer, on the other hand, is brewed using alternative grains like sorghum, rice, millet, or even gluten-free oats. The process requires meticulous attention to avoid cross-contamination, ensuring the final product is safe for those who need to avoid gluten.

The Brewing Process: How Stone Brewery Ensures Gluten-Free Quality

Stone Brewery’s commitment to quality extends to their gluten-free brewing process. They use a combination of alternative grains and a specialized enzyme to break down gluten proteins, ensuring that the final product contains less than 20 parts per million (ppm) of gluten, which is the threshold for being considered gluten-free. Additionally, they implement rigorous testing protocols to verify that their gluten-free beers meet these standards.

Here’s a step-by-step breakdown of their gluten-free brewing process:

  1. Selection of Gluten-Free Grains: Stone Brewery carefully selects gluten-free grains such as sorghum, rice, or millet to use as the base for their gluten-free beers.
  2. Brewing: The grains are mashed and boiled, just like in traditional brewing, but with the absence of gluten-containing grains.
  3. Enzyme Treatment: A specialized enzyme is added during the brewing process to break down any residual gluten proteins.
  4. Fermentation: The wort is fermented using yeast, which converts sugars into alcohol and carbon dioxide.
  5. Testing: The final product is rigorously tested to ensure it meets the gluten-free standard of less than 20 ppm of gluten.

FAQs About Stone Brewery Gluten-Free Beers

1. Are all Stone Brewery beers gluten-free?

No, not all Stone Brewery beers are gluten-free. However, they do offer a selection of gluten-removed beers that are crafted to be safe for those with gluten sensitivity or celiac disease.

2. What is the difference between gluten-free and gluten-removed beer?

Gluten-free beer is made from grains that do not contain gluten, such as sorghum or rice. Gluten-removed beer, on the other hand, is made from traditional gluten-containing grains, but a specialized enzyme is used to break down the gluten proteins, making it safe for those with gluten sensitivity.

3. Can I drink Stone Brewery’s gluten-removed beers if I have celiac disease?

While Stone Brewery’s gluten-removed beers are crafted to contain less than 20 ppm of gluten, individuals with celiac disease should consult with their healthcare provider before consuming these beers, as sensitivity levels can vary.

4. Where can I find Stone Brewery’s gluten-free beers?

Stone Brewery’s gluten-free beers are available at select retailers, as well as at their brewery locations. You can also check their website for availability in your area.
