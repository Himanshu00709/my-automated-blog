Gluten-Free Ooey Gooey Butter Cake: A Delicious Twist on a Classic

When it comes to comfort desserts, few can rival the indulgent, melt-in-your-mouth goodness of an ooey gooey butter cake. This classic treat, with its rich, buttery base and creamy, custard-like topping, has been a favorite for generations. But what if you're following a gluten-free diet? Fear not! With a few simple substitutions, you can enjoy a gluten-free version of this beloved dessert that’s just as delicious as the original. In this blog post, we’ll explore the history of ooey gooey butter cake, share a foolproof gluten-free recipe, and answer some frequently asked questions about this delightful treat.

The History of Ooey Gooey Butter Cake

The origins of ooey gooey butter cake can be traced back to St. Louis, Missouri, in the 1930s. Legend has it that a baker accidentally reversed the proportions of flour and butter in a traditional cake recipe, resulting in a gooey, custard-like dessert that quickly became a local favorite. Over the years, the recipe has evolved, with variations including chocolate, pumpkin, and even fruit-filled versions. Today, ooey gooey butter cake is enjoyed across the United States, and its gluten-free adaptation ensures that everyone can partake in its deliciousness.

Gluten-Free Ooey Gooey Butter Cake Recipe

Creating a gluten-free ooey gooey butter cake is easier than you might think. The key is to use high-quality gluten-free flour and ensure that all other ingredients are gluten-free as well. Below is a step-by-step recipe that will guide you through the process of making this delectable dessert.

Ingredients

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free all-purpose flour 1 1/2 cups
Granulated sugar 1 1/2 cups
Unsalted butter (softened) 1/2 cup
Eggs 2 large
Cream cheese (softened) 8 oz
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Baking powder 1 tsp
Salt 1/4 tsp
Powdered sugar (for dusting) As needed

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  2. Prepare the base: In a large mixing bowl, combine the gluten-free flour, 1 cup of granulated sugar, baking powder, and salt. Add the softened butter and one egg, and mix until a dough forms. Press the dough evenly into the bottom of the prepared baking dish.
  3. Make the topping: In another bowl, beat the cream cheese until smooth. Add the remaining 1/2 cup of granulated sugar, the second egg, and the vanilla extract. Mix until well combined and creamy.
  4. Assemble the cake: Pour the cream cheese mixture over the dough base, spreading it evenly with a spatula.
  5. Bake: Bake in the preheated oven for 40-45 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown and the center is set but still slightly jiggly.
  6. Cool and serve: Allow the cake to cool completely before dusting with powdered sugar and slicing into squares. Serve at room temperature.

Tips for the Perfect Gluten-Free Ooey Gooey Butter Cake

To ensure your gluten-free ooey gooey butter cake turns out perfectly every time, keep these tips in mind:

  • Use high-quality gluten-free flour: Not all gluten-free flours are created equal. Look for a blend that’s specifically designed for baking, as it will yield the best texture and flavor.
  • Don’t overmix the dough: Overmixing can lead to a tough base. Mix just until the ingredients are combined.
  • Let the cake cool completely: The cake will continue to set as it cools, so resist the temptation to cut into it right away.
  • Experiment with flavors: While the classic version is delicious, don’t be afraid to get creative. Add lemon zest, cocoa powder, or even a layer of fruit preserves for a unique twist.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Ooey Gooey Butter Cake

Can I use a different type of flour?

Yes, you can experiment with different gluten-free flours, such as almond flour or coconut flour. However, keep in mind that the texture and flavor may vary. For best results, stick to a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend.

Can I make this cake dairy-free?

Absolutely! You can substitute the butter and cream cheese with dairy-free alternatives. Look for plant-based butter and cream cheese at your local grocery store.

How should I store the cake?

Store the cake in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days, or in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. You can also freeze the cake for up to 3 months. Just be sure to wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil before freezing.

Can I make this cake ahead of time?

Yes, you can prepare the cake a day in advance. Simply store it in the refrigerator and bring it to room temperature before serving.
