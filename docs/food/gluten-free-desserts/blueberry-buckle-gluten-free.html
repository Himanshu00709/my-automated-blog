Blueberry Buckle Gluten-Free: A Delicious and Healthy Treat

Blueberry buckle is a classic dessert that combines the sweetness of blueberries with a tender, cake-like base and a crumbly topping. For those who follow a gluten-free diet, enjoying this delightful treat might seem challenging, but with the right ingredients and techniques, you can create a gluten-free blueberry buckle that is just as delicious as the traditional version. In this blog post, we’ll explore the history of blueberry buckle, provide a detailed gluten-free recipe, and answer some frequently asked questions about this beloved dessert.

What is Blueberry Buckle?

Blueberry buckle is a type of coffee cake that originated in the United States. It is characterized by its moist cake base, which is studded with fresh or frozen blueberries, and a streusel-like topping that "buckles" as it bakes, giving the dessert its name. The combination of sweet, juicy blueberries and a buttery crumb topping makes it a favorite among dessert lovers.

Why Go Gluten-Free?

Gluten-free diets have become increasingly popular in recent years, not only among those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity but also among individuals seeking to improve their overall health. Gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, can cause digestive issues and inflammation in some people. By using gluten-free flours and ingredients, you can enjoy the same delicious flavors and textures without the negative side effects.

Gluten-Free Blueberry Buckle Recipe

Below is a detailed recipe for making a gluten-free blueberry buckle. This recipe uses almond flour and gluten-free all-purpose flour to create a light and tender cake that is perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients

Ingredient Quantity
Almond flour 1 cup
Gluten-free all-purpose flour 1 cup
Baking powder 1 tsp
Salt 1/4 tsp
Unsalted butter (softened) 1/2 cup
Granulated sugar 3/4 cup
Eggs 2 large
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Milk (or dairy-free alternative) 1/2 cup
Fresh or frozen blueberries 2 cups

For the Topping

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free all-purpose flour 1/2 cup
Brown sugar 1/2 cup
Ground cinnamon 1 tsp
Unsalted butter (cold) 1/4 cup

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9-inch square baking pan.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the almond flour, gluten-free all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate large bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, alternating with the milk, and mix until just combined. Gently fold in the blueberries.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan and spread it evenly.
  6. To make the topping, combine the gluten-free all-purpose flour, brown sugar, and cinnamon in a small bowl. Cut in the cold butter using a pastry cutter or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Sprinkle the topping evenly over the batter.
  7. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow the buckle to cool slightly before serving.

FAQs

Can I use frozen blueberries instead of fresh?

Yes, you can use frozen blueberries in this recipe. There’s no need to thaw them before adding them to the batter. However, be aware that frozen blueberries may release more moisture, which could slightly affect the texture of the buckle.

Can I make this recipe dairy-free?

Absolutely! You can substitute the butter with a dairy-free alternative, such as coconut oil or vegan butter, and use a dairy-free milk like almond or oat milk.

How should I store the blueberry buckle?

Store the blueberry buckle in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days, or in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. You can also freeze it for up to 3 months. To reheat, simply warm it in the oven or microwave.

Whether you’re serving it for breakfast, as a snack, or as a dessert, this gluten-free version is a delicious and healthy option that everyone can enjoy.
