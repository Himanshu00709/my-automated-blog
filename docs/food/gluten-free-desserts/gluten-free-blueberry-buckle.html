Gluten-Free Blueberry Buckle: A Delicious and Healthy Treat

If you're looking for a delightful dessert that's both gluten-free and bursting with flavor, look no further than the gluten-free blueberry buckle. This classic dessert combines the sweetness of blueberries with a tender, crumbly cake base, making it a perfect treat for any occasion. Whether you're gluten-intolerant or simply looking to explore healthier dessert options, this recipe is sure to satisfy your sweet tooth.

What is a Blueberry Buckle?

A blueberry buckle is a type of coffee cake that features a layer of cake topped with fresh blueberries and a streusel-like crumb topping. As the cake bakes, the blueberries sink into the batter, creating a "buckled" appearance, hence the name. The result is a moist, flavorful cake with a delightful contrast of textures—soft cake, juicy berries, and a crunchy topping.

Why Go Gluten-Free?

Gluten-free baking has gained popularity in recent years, not just among those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, but also among health-conscious individuals. Gluten-free diets can help reduce inflammation, improve digestion, and promote overall well-being. By using gluten-free flours and ingredients, you can enjoy your favorite desserts without compromising on taste or texture.

Ingredients for Gluten-Free Blueberry Buckle

To make a gluten-free blueberry buckle, you'll need the following ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free all-purpose flour 1 ½ cups
Almond flour ½ cup
Baking powder 1 ½ tsp
Salt ¼ tsp
Unsalted butter (softened) ½ cup
Granulated sugar ¾ cup
Eggs 2 large
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Milk (or dairy-free alternative) ½ cup
Fresh blueberries 2 cups
Brown sugar (for topping) ¼ cup
Cinnamon (for topping) 1 tsp

Step-by-Step Instructions

Follow these simple steps to create your gluten-free blueberry buckle:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9-inch square baking pan.
  2. Prepare the Dry Ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour, almond flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Cream the Butter and Sugar: In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients: Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, alternating with the milk, and mix until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  5. Fold in the Blueberries: Gently fold the fresh blueberries into the batter.
  6. Prepare the Topping: In a small bowl, mix together the brown sugar and cinnamon.
  7. Assemble the Buckle: Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan and sprinkle the brown sugar and cinnamon mixture evenly over the top.
  8. Bake: Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  9. Cool and Serve: Allow the buckle to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Tips for the Perfect Gluten-Free Blueberry Buckle

  • Use Fresh Blueberries: Fresh blueberries are ideal for this recipe, but frozen blueberries can also be used. If using frozen, do not thaw them before adding to the batter.
  • Don’t Overmix: Overmixing the batter can lead to a dense cake. Mix until just combined to ensure a light and tender texture.
  • Experiment with Flours: Feel free to experiment with different gluten-free flours, such as oat flour or coconut flour, to find the texture and flavor you prefer.
  • Add a Citrus Twist: For an extra burst of flavor, add the zest of a lemon or orange to the batter.

FAQs

Can I use frozen blueberries instead of fresh?

Yes, you can use frozen blueberries. However, do not thaw them before adding to the batter, as this can cause the berries to release too much moisture and affect the texture of the cake.

Can I make this recipe dairy-free?

Absolutely! You can substitute the butter with a dairy-free alternative, such as coconut oil or vegan butter, and use a dairy-free milk like almond or oat milk.

How should I store the blueberry buckle?

Store the blueberry buckle in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days, or in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. You can also freeze it for up to 3 months.

Can I use other fruits instead of blueberries?

Yes, you can substitute blueberries with other fruits like raspberries, blackberries, or even diced apples. Adjust the sugar accordingly based on the sweetness of the fruit.
