Gluten-Free Rhubarb Bars: A Delicious and Healthy Treat

Rhubarb, with its tangy flavor and vibrant color, is a versatile ingredient that can be used in a variety of desserts. When combined with a gluten-free crust, it transforms into a delightful treat that is not only delicious but also suitable for those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease. In this blog post, we will explore the world of gluten-free rhubarb bars, from their health benefits to a step-by-step recipe that you can easily make at home.

Why Choose Gluten-Free Rhubarb Bars?

Gluten-free diets have gained popularity over the years, not just among those with celiac disease but also among individuals who experience gluten sensitivity or simply want to reduce their gluten intake. Rhubarb bars made with gluten-free ingredients offer a healthier alternative to traditional desserts, without compromising on taste or texture.

Health Benefits of Rhubarb

Rhubarb is a nutrient-rich vegetable that is often used in desserts. It is low in calories and high in fiber, making it a great addition to a healthy diet. Rhubarb is also a good source of vitamins C and K, as well as calcium and potassium. Additionally, it contains antioxidants that can help protect your body from oxidative stress and inflammation.

Benefits of a Gluten-Free Diet

For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, a gluten-free diet is essential to avoid adverse health effects. Even for those without gluten-related issues, reducing gluten intake can lead to improved digestion, increased energy levels, and better overall health. Gluten-free rhubarb bars are a perfect way to enjoy a sweet treat while adhering to a gluten-free lifestyle.

Gluten-Free Rhubarb Bars Recipe

Now that we’ve covered the benefits, let’s dive into the recipe for gluten-free rhubarb bars. This recipe is simple, requires minimal ingredients, and yields a delicious dessert that everyone will love.

Ingredients

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free flour blend 1 ½ cups
Almond flour ½ cup
Coconut sugar ¾ cup
Unsalted butter (or coconut oil for a vegan option) ½ cup, melted
Rhubarb, chopped 2 cups
Egg (or flax egg for a vegan option) 1
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Baking powder 1 tsp
Salt ¼ tsp

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line an 8x8 inch baking pan with parchment paper.
  2. Prepare the crust: In a large bowl, mix the gluten-free flour blend, almond flour, and ½ cup of coconut sugar. Add the melted butter and mix until the dough comes together. Press the dough evenly into the prepared baking pan.
  3. Prepare the rhubarb filling: In another bowl, combine the chopped rhubarb, remaining ¼ cup of coconut sugar, egg (or flax egg), vanilla extract, baking powder, and salt. Mix well.
  4. Assemble the bars: Spread the rhubarb mixture evenly over the crust in the baking pan.
  5. Bake: Bake in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the filling is set.
  6. Cool and serve: Allow the bars to cool completely before cutting into squares. Serve and enjoy!

FAQs About Gluten-Free Rhubarb Bars

Can I use frozen rhubarb for this recipe?

Yes, you can use frozen rhubarb. Just make sure to thaw it completely and drain any excess liquid before using it in the recipe.

Can I substitute the almond flour with another type of flour?

Yes, you can substitute almond flour with other gluten-free flours like oat flour or coconut flour. However, keep in mind that the texture and flavor may vary slightly.

How should I store gluten-free rhubarb bars?

Store the bars in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days, or in the refrigerator for up to a week. You can also freeze them for longer storage.

Are these bars suitable for vegans?

Yes, you can make these bars vegan by using coconut oil instead of butter and a flax egg instead of a regular egg.
