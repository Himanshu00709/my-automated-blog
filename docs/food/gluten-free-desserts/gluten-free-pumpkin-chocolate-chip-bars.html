Gluten-Free Pumpkin Chocolate Chip Bars: A Perfect Fall Treat

As the leaves change color and the air turns crisp, there's nothing quite like the comforting aroma of pumpkin and chocolate wafting through your kitchen. Gluten-free pumpkin chocolate chip bars are the perfect treat to celebrate the season. These bars are not only delicious but also cater to those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease. In this blog post, we'll dive into the recipe, nutritional benefits, and some frequently asked questions about this delightful dessert.

Why Gluten-Free Pumpkin Chocolate Chip Bars?

Gluten-free baking has gained immense popularity over the years, and for good reason. Many people suffer from gluten intolerance or celiac disease, which makes it essential to find alternatives to traditional wheat-based recipes. Pumpkin is a fantastic ingredient for gluten-free baking because it adds moisture and a rich, earthy flavor. When combined with chocolate chips, it creates a dessert that is both indulgent and wholesome.

Moreover, pumpkin is packed with nutrients. It's rich in vitamins A and C, which are essential for maintaining healthy skin and boosting the immune system. Pumpkin also contains fiber, which aids in digestion. By incorporating pumpkin into your gluten-free bars, you're not just satisfying your sweet tooth but also nourishing your body.

Ingredients You'll Need

Before we get into the recipe, let's take a look at the ingredients you'll need to make these gluten-free pumpkin chocolate chip bars:

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free flour blend 1 ½ cups
Baking powder 1 tsp
Baking soda ½ tsp
Salt ¼ tsp
Pumpkin puree 1 cup
Eggs 2 large
Brown sugar ¾ cup
Granulated sugar ¼ cup
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Ground cinnamon 1 tsp
Ground nutmeg ½ tsp
Ground cloves ¼ tsp
Chocolate chips 1 cup

Step-by-Step Recipe

Now that you have all your ingredients ready, let's get started with the recipe:

Step 1: Preheat and Prepare

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease an 8x8-inch baking pan and line it with parchment paper, leaving some overhang on the sides for easy removal.

Step 2: Mix Dry Ingredients

In a medium bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour blend, baking powder, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves. Set aside.

Step 3: Combine Wet Ingredients

In a large bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, eggs, brown sugar, granulated sugar, and vanilla extract until well combined.

Step 4: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix. Fold in the chocolate chips.

Step 5: Bake

Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan and spread it evenly. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Step 6: Cool and Serve

Allow the bars to cool in the pan for about 10 minutes, then use the parchment paper to lift them out of the pan. Let them cool completely on a wire rack before cutting into squares.

FAQs

Can I use a different type of flour?

Yes, you can use almond flour or oat flour as a substitute for the gluten-free flour blend. However, the texture and taste may vary slightly.

Can I make these bars dairy-free?

Absolutely! Simply use dairy-free chocolate chips to make these bars dairy-free.

How should I store these bars?

Store the bars in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or in the refrigerator for up to a week. You can also freeze them for up to 3 months.

Can I add nuts to the recipe?

Yes, you can add chopped nuts like walnuts or pecans for an extra crunch. Just fold them in with the chocolate chips.
