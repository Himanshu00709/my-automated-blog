Gluten-Free Pumpkin Whoopie Pies: A Fall Delight

As the leaves turn golden and the air becomes crisp, there's no better way to embrace the flavors of fall than with a batch of gluten-free pumpkin whoopie pies. These delightful treats combine the warm, spiced flavors of pumpkin with a creamy filling, all while being completely gluten-free. Whether you're hosting a fall gathering or simply craving a seasonal treat, these whoopie pies are sure to impress.

What Are Pumpkin Whoopie Pies?

Pumpkin whoopie pies are a classic American dessert that originated in New England. They consist of two soft, cake-like pumpkin cookies sandwiched together with a creamy filling, often made with cream cheese or marshmallow fluff. The combination of the spiced pumpkin cookies and the sweet filling creates a perfect balance of flavors that is irresistible.

Why Go Gluten-Free?

For those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, enjoying traditional baked goods can be a challenge. Gluten-free baking has come a long way, and with the right ingredients, you can create delicious treats that everyone can enjoy. Gluten-free pumpkin whoopie pies are a great option for those who want to indulge in a fall favorite without the worry of gluten.

Ingredients You'll Need

To make gluten-free pumpkin whoopie pies, you'll need a few key ingredients. Here's a list of what you'll need:

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free flour blend 2 cups
Pumpkin puree 1 cup
Brown sugar 1 cup
Eggs 2 large
Butter (or dairy-free alternative) 1/2 cup
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Pumpkin pie spice 1 tsp
Baking powder 1 tsp
Baking soda 1/2 tsp
Salt 1/4 tsp
Cream cheese (or dairy-free alternative) 8 oz
Powdered sugar 2 cups

Step-by-Step Instructions

Follow these simple steps to create your gluten-free pumpkin whoopie pies:

1. Prepare the Pumpkin Cookies

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a large mixing bowl, combine the gluten-free flour blend, pumpkin pie spice, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. In a separate bowl, cream together the butter and brown sugar until light and fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract and pumpkin puree. Gradually mix in the dry ingredients until a smooth batter forms.

2. Bake the Cookies

Drop spoonfuls of the batter onto a parchment-lined baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the cookies are set and lightly golden around the edges. Allow them to cool completely on a wire rack.

3. Make the Filling

While the cookies are cooling, prepare the filling. In a mixing bowl, beat the cream cheese until smooth. Gradually add the powdered sugar, mixing until the filling is creamy and well combined. If desired, you can add a splash of vanilla extract for extra flavor.

4. Assemble the Whoopie Pies

Once the cookies are completely cool, spread a generous amount of the cream cheese filling onto the flat side of one cookie. Top with another cookie, pressing gently to create a sandwich. Repeat with the remaining cookies and filling.

Tips for Perfect Gluten-Free Whoopie Pies

Here are some tips to ensure your gluten-free pumpkin whoopie pies turn out perfectly:

  • Use a reliable gluten-free flour blend: Not all gluten-free flours are created equal. Look for a blend that is specifically designed for baking, as it will yield the best texture.
  • Don't overmix the batter: Overmixing can lead to dense cookies. Mix just until the ingredients are combined.
  • Let the cookies cool completely: This will prevent the filling from melting and make the whoopie pies easier to handle.
  • Store properly: Keep the whoopie pies in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. They can also be frozen for longer storage.

FAQs

Can I make these whoopie pies dairy-free?

Yes, you can easily make these whoopie pies dairy-free by using a dairy-free butter alternative and a dairy-free cream cheese substitute. Just make sure to check the labels to ensure they are also gluten-free.

Can I use fresh pumpkin instead of canned pumpkin puree?

Absolutely! If you prefer to use fresh pumpkin, simply roast or steam a small sugar pumpkin, then puree the flesh until smooth. Make sure to drain any excess liquid to achieve the right consistency.

How do I know if my gluten-free flour blend is good for baking?

Look for a gluten-free flour blend that contains a mix of flours and starches, such as rice flour, tapioca starch, and potato starch. These blends are typically designed to mimic the texture of all-purpose flour and work well in baking.
