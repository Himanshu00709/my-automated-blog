What is Gluten and Why Go Gluten-Free?

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For most people, gluten is harmless, but for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, it can cause severe health issues. Celiac disease is an autoimmune disorder where the ingestion of gluten leads to damage in the small intestine. Symptoms can range from digestive problems to fatigue, skin rashes, and even neurological issues. Gluten sensitivity, on the other hand, may cause similar symptoms but without the intestinal damage seen in celiac disease.

Going gluten-free is not just a trend; for many, it's a medical necessity. However, even for those without gluten-related health issues, a gluten-free diet can offer benefits such as improved digestion, increased energy levels, and better overall health.
