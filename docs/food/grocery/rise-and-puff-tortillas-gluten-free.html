The Gluten-Free Movement: A Brief Overview

The gluten-free movement has gained significant momentum over the past decade. Gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, can cause serious health issues for individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. For these individuals, consuming gluten can lead to symptoms such as digestive problems, fatigue, and even long-term damage to the small intestine. As a result, there has been a surge in the availability of gluten-free products, ranging from bread and pasta to snacks and desserts.

However, not all gluten-free products are created equal. Many early gluten-free alternatives were criticized for their poor texture, taste, and nutritional value. Fortunately, advancements in food technology and a better understanding of gluten-free baking have led to the development of products that are not only safe for those with gluten intolerance but also delicious and satisfying for everyone.
