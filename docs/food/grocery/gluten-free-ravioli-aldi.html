FAQs About Aldi’s Gluten-Free Ravioli

1. Is Aldi’s gluten-free ravioli suitable for vegans?

No, Aldi’s gluten-free ravioli contains cheese, which is derived from dairy. Therefore, it is not suitable for vegans. However, it is suitable for vegetarians.

2. Can I freeze Aldi’s gluten-free ravioli?

Yes, Aldi’s gluten-free ravioli can be frozen. To maintain its quality, store it in an airtight container or freezer bag. When ready to eat, cook it directly from frozen, adding a couple of extra minutes to the cooking time.

3. How long does Aldi’s gluten-free ravioli take to cook?

Aldi’s gluten-free ravioli typically takes about 4-5 minutes to cook in boiling water. Be sure to follow the instructions on the packaging for the best results.

4. Is Aldi’s gluten-free ravioli certified gluten-free?

Yes, Aldi’s gluten-free ravioli is certified gluten-free, meaning it has been tested and meets the strict standards for gluten-free products. This makes it a safe option for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity.
