RX Bars Gluten-Free: A Comprehensive Guide

In recent years, the demand for gluten-free products has skyrocketed, driven by both medical necessity and lifestyle choices. What Are RX Bars?

RX Bars are a line of protein bars that have gained a loyal following due to their simple, whole-food ingredients and transparent labeling. Each bar is made with a short list of ingredients, including egg whites for protein, dates for natural sweetness, and nuts for healthy fats. The brand prides itself on its "No B.S." philosophy, meaning no artificial additives, no added sugars, and no gluten.

Are RX Bars Gluten-Free?

Yes, RX Bars are gluten-free. The company clearly states on its packaging and website that all RX Bars are free from gluten-containing ingredients. This makes them a safe option for individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. However, it’s important to note that while RX Bars are made in a facility that processes gluten, the company follows strict protocols to prevent cross-contamination.

FAQs About RX Bars Gluten-Free

Are RX Bars certified gluten-free?

While RX Bars are not certified gluten-free, the company states that all their products are made without gluten-containing ingredients. They also follow strict protocols to minimize the risk of cross-contamination.

Can people with celiac disease eat RX Bars?

Yes, RX Bars are considered safe for people with celiac disease, as they do not contain gluten. However, individuals with severe gluten sensitivity should be aware that RX Bars are produced in a facility that also processes gluten.

Do RX Bars contain dairy?

Some RX Bars contain dairy, such as those with chocolate flavors. However, the company offers dairy-free options, so be sure to check the label if you have a dairy allergy or intolerance.

Are RX Bars suitable for vegans?

Most RX Bars are not vegan-friendly because they contain egg whites as a primary source of protein. However, the company has introduced plant-based options for those following a vegan diet.
