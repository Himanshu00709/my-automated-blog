The History of Walker's Shortbread

Walker's Shortbread has a long and storied history that dates back to 1898 when Joseph Walker opened his bakery in the small Scottish village of Aberlour. Joseph's commitment to using only the finest ingredients and traditional baking methods quickly earned him a reputation for producing some of the best shortbread in Scotland. Over the years, Walker's Shortbread has grown into a globally recognized brand, but the company has remained true to its roots, continuing to use the same simple recipe that Joseph Walker perfected over a century ago.

In recent years, Walker's has expanded its product line to include gluten-free options, recognizing the growing demand for gluten-free products. Their gluten-free shortbread cookies are made with the same care and attention to detail as their traditional shortbread, ensuring that everyone can enjoy the rich, buttery flavor that Walker's is known for.

What Makes Walker's Gluten-Free Shortbread Cookies Special?

Walker's Gluten-Free Shortbread Cookies are made with a blend of rice flour, potato starch, and maize flour, which gives them a light, crumbly texture that is remarkably similar to traditional shortbread. Unlike some gluten-free products that can be dry or crumbly, Walker's gluten-free shortbread cookies are moist and tender, making them a satisfying treat for anyone, regardless of dietary restrictions.
