Gluten-Free Stroopwafel in Amsterdam: A Delicious Guide

Amsterdam, the capital of the Netherlands, is renowned for its picturesque canals, historic architecture, and vibrant culture. But for food enthusiasts, Amsterdam is also a paradise for indulging in traditional Dutch treats. One such iconic delicacy is the stroopwafel—a thin, crispy waffle filled with a gooey caramel syrup. However, for those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease, finding a gluten-free version of this beloved treat can be a challenge. In this guide, we’ll explore the best places to find gluten-free stroopwafels in Amsterdam, along with tips, recipes, and FAQs to satisfy your cravings.

What is a Stroopwafel?

The stroopwafel, which translates to "syrup waffle," is a traditional Dutch cookie made from two thin layers of baked dough with a caramel-like syrup filling in the middle. Originating in the city of Gouda in the late 18th century, stroopwafels have become a staple in Dutch cuisine and are enjoyed worldwide. The classic stroopwafel is made with wheat flour, which contains gluten, making it unsuitable for those with gluten intolerance. Fortunately, Amsterdam has embraced the gluten-free movement, and several bakeries and cafes now offer gluten-free stroopwafels.

How to Make Gluten-Free Stroopwafels at Home

If you’re feeling adventurous, you can try making gluten-free stroopwafels at home. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups gluten-free flour blend
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup milk (or dairy-free alternative)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 cup caramel syrup (ensure it’s gluten-free)

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the gluten-free flour, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add the egg, milk, and vanilla extract, and mix well.
  3. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a dough forms.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls and press them in a preheated stroopwafel iron. Cook until golden brown.
  5. Once cooled, spread caramel syrup on one waffle and sandwich it with another. Repeat with the remaining waffles.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Stroopwafels

1. Are gluten-free stroopwafels safe for people with celiac disease?

Yes, gluten-free stroopwafels are safe for individuals with celiac disease, provided they are made in a dedicated gluten-free facility or with certified gluten-free ingredients to avoid cross-contamination.

2. Can I find gluten-free stroopwafels outside of Amsterdam?

While Amsterdam is a great place to find gluten-free stroopwafels, many specialty stores and online retailers also offer gluten-free versions that can be shipped worldwide.

3. What are the best gluten-free flours to use for stroopwafels?

Popular gluten-free flours for stroopwafels include almond flour, rice flour, and oat flour. A blend of these flours often yields the best texture and flavor.

4. How should I store gluten-free stroopwafels?

Gluten-free stroopwafels should be stored in an airtight container at room temperature. They can last for up to a week, but for longer storage, consider freezing them.
