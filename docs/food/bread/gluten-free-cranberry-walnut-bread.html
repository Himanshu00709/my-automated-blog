Gluten-Free Cranberry Walnut Bread: A Delicious and Healthy Treat

Gluten-free baking has become increasingly popular as more people discover the benefits of a gluten-free diet, whether due to celiac disease, gluten sensitivity, or simply a desire for healthier eating. Among the many gluten-free recipes available, gluten-free cranberry walnut bread stands out as a delightful combination of tart cranberries, crunchy walnuts, and a moist, tender crumb. This bread is not only a treat for the taste buds but also a nutritious option for those looking to enjoy a wholesome snack or breakfast.

Why Choose Gluten-Free Cranberry Walnut Bread?

Gluten-free cranberry walnut bread is a versatile and satisfying option for anyone looking to avoid gluten without sacrificing flavor or texture. The combination of cranberries and walnuts provides a perfect balance of sweet and tart, while the gluten-free flours used in the recipe ensure a light and airy texture. This bread is also packed with nutrients, making it a healthy choice for those with dietary restrictions or anyone looking to incorporate more whole foods into their diet.

Ingredients You'll Need

To make gluten-free cranberry walnut bread, you'll need a few key ingredients. Here's a list of what you'll need:

Ingredient Quantity Notes
Gluten-free flour blend 2 cups Ensure it contains xanthan gum or add 1 tsp separately
Baking powder 1 tsp Ensure it's gluten-free
Baking soda 1/2 tsp
Salt 1/4 tsp
Eggs 2 large Room temperature
Unsweetened applesauce 1/2 cup Acts as a binder and adds moisture
Honey or maple syrup 1/2 cup For natural sweetness
Milk (dairy or non-dairy) 1/2 cup Almond, oat, or coconut milk work well
Dried cranberries 1/2 cup Unsweetened or sweetened, depending on preference
Chopped walnuts 1/2 cup Toast them for extra flavor

Step-by-Step Instructions

Making gluten-free cranberry walnut bread is a straightforward process, but it's important to follow the steps carefully to ensure the best results. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Preheat and Prepare

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan and line it with parchment paper for easy removal.

Step 2: Mix Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour blend, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Ensure the ingredients are well combined to avoid any lumps in the batter.

Step 3: Combine Wet Ingredients

In a separate bowl, beat the eggs until light and frothy. Add the applesauce, honey (or maple syrup), and milk, and mix until well combined.

Step 4: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can lead to a dense loaf.

Step 5: Fold in Cranberries and Walnuts

Gently fold in the dried cranberries and chopped walnuts, ensuring they are evenly distributed throughout the batter.

Step 6: Bake

Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Bake in the preheated oven for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Step 7: Cool and Serve

Allow the bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely. Slice and serve as desired.

FAQs

Can I use fresh cranberries instead of dried?

Yes, you can use fresh cranberries, but keep in mind that they are more tart than dried cranberries. You may want to adjust the sweetness of the bread by adding a bit more honey or maple syrup.

Can I substitute the walnuts with another nut?

Absolutely! You can use pecans, almonds, or any other nut you prefer. Just make sure to chop them into small pieces for even distribution.

How should I store gluten-free cranberry walnut bread?

Store the bread in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and freeze for up to 3 months.

Is this bread suitable for vegans?

This recipe can be made vegan by substituting the eggs with a flax egg (1 tbsp ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tbsp water per egg) and using a plant-based milk.

Enjoy it as a snack, breakfast, or even as a dessert – the possibilities are endless!
