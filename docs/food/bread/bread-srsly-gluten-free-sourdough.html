The Health Benefits of Sourdough Fermentation

Sourdough fermentation is an ancient technique that has been used for thousands of years to make bread. The process involves using a natural starter culture, which is a mixture of flour and water that has been allowed to ferment over time. This fermentation process breaks down the carbohydrates and proteins in the flour, making the bread easier to digest and increasing the availability of nutrients.

For those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease, sourdough fermentation can be particularly beneficial. The long fermentation process helps to break down gluten proteins, making the bread easier to digest for some individuals. While Bread Srsly’s sourdough is completely gluten-free, the fermentation process still offers the same digestive benefits, making it a healthier option compared to other gluten-free breads.
