Gluten-Free Jalapeño Cheddar Bread: A Flavorful Delight

Bread is a staple in many diets, but for those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease, finding a delicious and satisfying alternative can be a challenge. Enter gluten-free jalapeño cheddar bread—a savory, spicy, and cheesy delight that will make you forget you're eating gluten-free. This bread combines the heat of jalapeños with the rich, creamy flavor of cheddar cheese, all wrapped up in a soft, gluten-free loaf. Whether you're a seasoned gluten-free baker or just starting out, this recipe is sure to become a favorite.

Why Gluten-Free Jalapeño Cheddar Bread?

Gluten-free baking has come a long way in recent years, with a variety of flours and techniques available to create delicious, gluten-free versions of your favorite baked goods. Jalapeño cheddar bread is a classic favorite, and making it gluten-free allows everyone to enjoy its bold flavors. The combination of spicy jalapeños and sharp cheddar cheese creates a bread that is perfect for sandwiches, toast, or simply enjoyed on its own.

Ingredients You'll Need

To make gluten-free jalapeño cheddar bread, you'll need a few key ingredients. Here's a list of what you'll need:

Ingredient Quantity Notes
Gluten-free flour blend 2 1/2 cups Ensure it contains xanthan gum or add 1 tsp separately
Baking powder 1 tbsp Make sure it's gluten-free
Salt 1 tsp Adjust to taste
Eggs 3 large Room temperature
Milk 1 cup Dairy or non-dairy alternatives work
Butter 1/2 cup Melted and cooled
Jalapeños 2-3 Seeded and finely chopped
Cheddar cheese 1 cup Shredded

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that you have your ingredients ready, let's dive into the step-by-step process of making this delicious bread:

Step 1: Preheat and Prepare

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5 inch loaf pan and set it aside. This will ensure that your bread doesn't stick to the pan and comes out easily after baking.

Step 2: Mix Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour blend, baking powder, and salt. Make sure the ingredients are well combined to ensure an even texture in your bread.

Step 3: Combine Wet Ingredients

In a separate bowl, beat the eggs, then add the milk and melted butter. Mix until well combined. This mixture will help bind the dry ingredients together and add moisture to the bread.

Step 4: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can lead to a dense loaf. Fold in the chopped jalapeños and shredded cheddar cheese until evenly distributed throughout the batter.

Step 5: Bake

Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Bake in the preheated oven for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. The bread should be golden brown on top.

Step 6: Cool and Serve

Allow the bread to cool in the pan for about 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely. Slice and serve as desired. This bread is perfect for sandwiches, toast, or simply enjoyed on its own.

Tips for Success

Here are a few tips to ensure your gluten-free jalapeño cheddar bread turns out perfectly every time:

  • Use a good gluten-free flour blend: Not all gluten-free flours are created equal. Look for a blend that is specifically designed for baking bread and contains xanthan gum, which helps mimic the elasticity of gluten.
  • Don't overmix the batter: Overmixing can lead to a dense loaf. Mix until just combined to ensure a light and airy texture.
  • Adjust the spice level: If you prefer a milder bread, remove the seeds from the jalapeños. For a spicier loaf, leave some or all of the seeds in.
  • Experiment with cheese: While cheddar is classic, feel free to experiment with other types of cheese, such as pepper jack or mozzarella, for a different flavor profile.

FAQs

Can I make this bread dairy-free?

Yes, you can make this bread dairy-free by using a non-dairy milk alternative, such as almond or oat milk, and a dairy-free cheese substitute. Be sure to check the labels to ensure they are gluten-free as well.

How should I store the bread?

Store the bread in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, wrap the bread tightly in plastic wrap and freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw at room temperature before serving.

Can I add other ingredients to the bread?

Absolutely! Feel free to customize the bread by adding other ingredients, such as cooked bacon, green onions, or even a sprinkle of garlic powder for extra flavor.

Is this bread suitable for people with celiac disease?

Yes, as long as all ingredients used are certified gluten-free and there is no risk of cross-contamination, this bread is suitable for people with celiac disease.
