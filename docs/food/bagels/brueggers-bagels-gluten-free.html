Understanding Gluten-Free Diets

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to more severe health complications. As a result, many people are turning to gluten-free diets to manage their health. However, finding gluten-free options at traditional bagel shops can be difficult, as bagels are typically made from wheat flour. This is where Bruegger's Bagels steps in, offering a selection of gluten-free items to meet the needs of their customers.

How Bruegger's Ensures Gluten-Free Safety

For individuals with celiac disease or severe gluten sensitivity, cross-contamination is a significant concern. Bruegger's takes several steps to ensure that their gluten-free items are safe for consumption:

  • Separate Preparation Areas: Gluten-free bagels are prepared in a dedicated area to avoid contact with gluten-containing ingredients.
  • Dedicated Equipment: Specialized equipment is used to bake gluten-free bagels, reducing the risk of cross-contamination.
  • Staff Training: Bruegger's staff are trained in gluten-free preparation protocols to ensure that all gluten-free orders are handled with care.
