Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. For people with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to long-term health complications. Therefore, it’s crucial to carefully examine the ingredients and manufacturing processes of any food product, especially during the holiday season when treats like candy canes are abundant.

Manufacturing Processes and Cross-Contamination

Even if the ingredients themselves are gluten-free, cross-contamination during manufacturing can be a concern. Many candy manufacturers produce a variety of products in the same facility, some of which may contain gluten. To ensure that Skittle Candy Canes are safe for those with gluten sensitivities, it’s essential to look for a gluten-free certification on the packaging.

Skittles, the parent brand of Skittle Candy Canes, has stated that their products are gluten-free. However, it’s always a good idea to check the packaging for any allergen warnings or certifications. If the packaging does not explicitly state that the product is gluten-free, it’s best to contact the manufacturer directly for clarification.

Gluten-Free Certification

Gluten-free certification is a reliable way to ensure that a product is safe for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. Certified gluten-free products have undergone rigorous testing to confirm that they contain less than 20 parts per million (ppm) of gluten, which is the threshold considered safe by the FDA.

While Skittle Candy Canes may not always carry a gluten-free certification, the ingredients and manufacturing processes suggest that they are likely safe for most people with gluten sensitivities. However, if you have severe celiac disease, it’s always best to err on the side of caution and choose products with a clear gluten-free label.
