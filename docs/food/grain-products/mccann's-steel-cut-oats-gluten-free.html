What Are Steel Cut Oats?

Steel-cut oats, also known as Irish oats or pinhead oats, are whole oat groats that have been chopped into two or three pieces using steel blades. Unlike rolled oats or instant oats, steel-cut oats undergo minimal processing, which helps retain their natural texture, flavor, and nutritional value. This minimal processing also means that steel-cut oats take longer to cook, but the result is a hearty, chewy, and nutty-flavored oatmeal that many people find far superior to other types of oats.

How to Cook McCann's Steel Cut Oats

Cooking McCann's Steel Cut Oats is simple, though it does require a bit more time than instant oats. Here’s a basic recipe to get you started:

  1. Ingredients: 1 cup of McCann's Steel Cut Oats, 4 cups of water or milk, and a pinch of salt.
  2. Instructions: Bring the water or milk to a boil in a medium-sized pot. Add the oats and salt, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cook for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the oats reach your desired consistency.
  3. Serving Suggestions: Top your oatmeal with fresh fruits, nuts, seeds, or a drizzle of honey for added flavor and nutrition.
