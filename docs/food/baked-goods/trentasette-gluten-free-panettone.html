The History of Panettone

Panettone has a rich history that dates back to the Roman Empire. The modern version of this dessert originated in Milan, Italy, during the 15th century. Legend has it that a young nobleman named Ughetto fell in love with the daughter of a baker. To win her heart, he disguised himself as a baker and created a rich, sweet bread filled with raisins and candied fruit. The bread became so popular that it was named "panettone," which translates to "big bread." Today, panettone is a symbol of Italian holiday traditions and is enjoyed worldwide.
