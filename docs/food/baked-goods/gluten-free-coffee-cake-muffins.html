Gluten-Free Coffee Cake Muffins: A Delicious and Healthy Treat

For those who love the comforting taste of coffee cake but need to avoid gluten, gluten-free coffee cake muffins are the perfect solution. These muffins combine the rich, buttery flavor of traditional coffee cake with the convenience of a portable muffin. Whether you're gluten-intolerant, celiac, or simply looking to reduce gluten in your diet, these muffins are a delightful treat that won't compromise on taste or texture.

Why Choose Gluten-Free Coffee Cake Muffins?

Gluten-free baking has come a long way in recent years, and with the right ingredients, you can create baked goods that are just as delicious as their gluten-containing counterparts. Gluten-free coffee cake muffins are not only a great option for those with dietary restrictions, but they also offer a healthier alternative to traditional muffins. By using gluten-free flours and natural sweeteners, you can enjoy a guilt-free treat that's packed with flavor.

Ingredients You'll Need

To make gluten-free coffee cake muffins, you'll need a few key ingredients. Here's a list of what you'll need:

Ingredient Quantity Notes
Gluten-free flour blend 2 cups Look for a blend that includes xanthan gum for better texture.
Almond flour 1/2 cup Adds moisture and a nutty flavor.
Baking powder 2 tsp Helps the muffins rise.
Baking soda 1/2 tsp Works with the baking powder to create a light texture.
Salt 1/4 tsp Enhances the flavor.
Unsalted butter 1/2 cup Softened for easier mixing.
Granulated sugar 3/4 cup You can also use coconut sugar for a healthier option.
Eggs 2 large Room temperature for better incorporation.
Vanilla extract 1 tsp Adds a warm, sweet flavor.
Plain yogurt 1/2 cup Keeps the muffins moist.
Milk 1/4 cup Any type of milk will work, including almond or oat milk.
Cinnamon streusel topping 1/2 cup Made with gluten-free flour, sugar, cinnamon, and butter.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Making gluten-free coffee cake muffins is easier than you might think. Follow these simple steps to create a batch of delicious muffins:

1. Preheat and Prepare

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners or grease it lightly with butter or cooking spray.

2. Mix Dry Ingredients

In a medium bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour blend, almond flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.

3. Cream Butter and Sugar

In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. This should take about 2-3 minutes with an electric mixer.

4. Add Eggs and Vanilla

Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Then, mix in the vanilla extract.

5. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Alternately add the dry ingredients and the yogurt to the butter mixture, beginning and ending with the dry ingredients. Mix until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in dense muffins.

6. Add Milk

Stir in the milk until the batter is smooth and well combined. The batter should be thick but pourable.

7. Prepare Streusel Topping

In a small bowl, mix together the gluten-free flour, sugar, cinnamon, and softened butter until crumbly. This will be your streusel topping.

8. Fill Muffin Tin

Spoon the batter into the prepared muffin tin, filling each cup about two-thirds full. Sprinkle the streusel topping evenly over the batter.

9. Bake

Bake in the preheated oven for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean.

10. Cool and Serve

Allow the muffins to cool in the tin for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. Serve warm or at room temperature.

FAQs

Can I use a different type of flour?

Yes, you can experiment with different gluten-free flours, such as oat flour or rice flour. However, keep in mind that the texture and flavor may vary slightly.

Can I make these muffins dairy-free?

Absolutely! You can substitute the butter with a dairy-free alternative like coconut oil or vegan butter, and use a non-dairy yogurt and milk.

How should I store these muffins?

Store the muffins in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or in the refrigerator for up to a week. You can also freeze them for up to 3 months.

Can I add nuts or fruit to the batter?

Yes, you can add chopped nuts, such as walnuts or pecans, or even fresh or dried fruit like blueberries or raisins for added texture and flavor.

Conclusion

With their moist, tender crumb and sweet, cinnamon-streusel topping, these muffins are perfect for breakfast, brunch, or a midday snack. By following the simple steps and tips outlined in this post, you can easily create a batch of these delicious muffins at home.
