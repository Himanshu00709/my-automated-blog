Gluten-Free Ube Cookies: A Delicious and Healthy Treat

In recent years, gluten-free diets have gained immense popularity, not just among those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, but also among health-conscious individuals looking to reduce inflammation and improve digestion. One of the most exciting trends in gluten-free baking is the use of unique ingredients like ube, a vibrant purple yam native to the Philippines. Ube has a naturally sweet, nutty flavor and a striking color that makes it a standout ingredient in desserts. In this blog post, we’ll explore the world of gluten-free ube cookies, their health benefits, and how you can make them at home.

What is Ube?

Ube, also known as purple yam, is a starchy root vegetable that has been a staple in Filipino cuisine for centuries. It’s known for its vibrant purple color, which comes from anthocyanins, a type of antioxidant that has been linked to numerous health benefits. Ube is often used in desserts like cakes, ice cream, and pastries, and its unique flavor profile makes it a versatile ingredient in both traditional and modern recipes.

Why Choose Gluten-Free Ube Cookies?

Gluten-free ube cookies are not only a treat for the taste buds but also a healthier alternative to traditional cookies. Here are some reasons why you should consider making the switch:

  • Gluten-Free: Perfect for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity.
  • Rich in Antioxidants: Ube is packed with anthocyanins, which help fight inflammation and oxidative stress.
  • Naturally Sweet: Ube has a natural sweetness, reducing the need for added sugars.
  • Unique Flavor: The nutty, slightly earthy flavor of ube adds a unique twist to traditional cookies.

How to Make Gluten-Free Ube Cookies

Making gluten-free ube cookies is easier than you might think. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free flour blend 2 cups
Ube powder or cooked ube (mashed) 1/2 cup
Unsalted butter (softened) 1/2 cup
Granulated sugar 3/4 cup
Egg 1 large
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Baking powder 1 tsp
Salt 1/4 tsp

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, cream together the softened butter and sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Add the egg and vanilla extract, mixing until well combined.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour, baking powder, and salt.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until a dough forms.
  6. Fold in the ube powder or mashed ube until the dough is evenly colored.
  7. Scoop tablespoon-sized portions of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
  8. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden.
  9. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Ube Cookies

1. Can I use fresh ube instead of ube powder?

Yes, you can use fresh ube. Simply boil or steam the ube until tender, then mash it before adding it to the cookie dough. Keep in mind that fresh ube may add more moisture to the dough, so you may need to adjust the amount of flour.

2. Are gluten-free ube cookies suitable for vegans?

This recipe includes butter and eggs, so it’s not vegan. However, you can easily make it vegan by substituting the butter with coconut oil and using a flax egg or another egg substitute.

3. How should I store gluten-free ube cookies?

Store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days. For longer storage, you can freeze the cookies for up to 3 months.

4. Can I add other flavors to the cookies?

Absolutely! Ube pairs well with flavors like coconut, vanilla, and even chocolate. Feel free to experiment with different add-ins like shredded coconut or chocolate chips.
