Gluten-Free Viennese Biscuits: A Delightful Treat for Everyone

Viennese biscuits are a classic treat, known for their delicate, buttery texture and elegant piped shapes. Traditionally made with wheat flour, these biscuits can be easily adapted to a gluten-free diet without compromising on taste or texture. Whether you're gluten intolerant, celiac, or simply looking to reduce gluten in your diet, gluten-free Viennese biscuits are a perfect choice. In this article, we'll explore the history of Viennese biscuits, how to make them gluten-free, and answer some common questions about this delightful treat.

The History of Viennese Biscuits

Viennese biscuits, also known as Viennese whirls or Viennese fingers, originated in Austria and became popular in the UK during the 20th century. These biscuits are characterized by their light, crumbly texture and are often piped into intricate shapes using a star-shaped nozzle. Traditionally, they are made with a combination of butter, sugar, flour, and sometimes ground almonds or cornflour to enhance their texture. The biscuits are often sandwiched together with jam or buttercream, making them a favorite for afternoon tea or special occasions.

Why Go Gluten-Free?

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, and it can cause digestive issues for people with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. Even for those without these conditions, reducing gluten intake can sometimes lead to improved digestion and overall health. Gluten-free baking has come a long way in recent years, with many alternative flours and ingredients now available that can replicate the texture and taste of traditional baked goods. Gluten-free Viennese biscuits are a perfect example of how you can enjoy a classic treat without the gluten.

How to Make Gluten-Free Viennese Biscuits

Making gluten-free Viennese biscuits is surprisingly simple, and the results are just as delicious as the traditional version. Here’s a step-by-step guide to creating these delightful treats:

Ingredients

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free flour blend 200g
Unsalted butter (softened) 150g
Caster sugar 50g
Cornflour 50g
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Salt A pinch

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (160°C fan) and line two baking trays with parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and caster sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Add the vanilla extract and a pinch of salt, and mix until well combined.
  4. Sift the gluten-free flour blend and cornflour into the bowl, and gently fold them into the butter mixture until a smooth dough forms.
  5. Transfer the dough into a piping bag fitted with a star-shaped nozzle. Pipe the dough into swirls or fingers onto the prepared baking trays, leaving space between each biscuit.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until the biscuits are lightly golden around the edges.
  7. Allow the biscuits to cool on the trays for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.
  8. Once cooled, you can sandwich the biscuits together with your favorite jam or buttercream, or enjoy them as they are.

Tips for Perfect Gluten-Free Viennese Biscuits

  • Use a good quality gluten-free flour blend: Not all gluten-free flours are created equal. Look for a blend that is specifically designed for baking, as it will give you the best texture and flavor.
  • Don’t overmix the dough: Overmixing can lead to tough biscuits. Mix the dough just until the ingredients are combined.
  • Chill the dough if necessary: If the dough becomes too soft to pipe, you can chill it in the refrigerator for 15-20 minutes before piping.
  • Experiment with flavors: You can add a variety of flavors to your Viennese biscuits, such as lemon zest, cocoa powder, or even a touch of cinnamon.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Viennese Biscuits

Can I use almond flour instead of a gluten-free flour blend?

While almond flour can be used in gluten-free baking, it has a different texture and moisture content compared to a gluten-free flour blend. For Viennese biscuits, it’s best to use a blend that includes rice flour, tapioca starch, and potato starch for the best results.

Can I freeze gluten-free Viennese biscuits?

Yes, you can freeze gluten-free Viennese biscuits. Once baked and cooled, place them in an airtight container or freezer bag and store them in the freezer for up to 3 months. Thaw at room temperature before serving.

Are gluten-free Viennese biscuits suitable for vegans?

Traditional Viennese biscuits contain butter, which is not vegan. However, you can make a vegan version by using plant-based butter and ensuring that all other ingredients are vegan-friendly.

Can I make these biscuits without a piping bag?

If you don’t have a piping bag, you can use a plastic bag with the corner snipped off, or simply shape the dough into small rounds with your hands. However, piping gives the biscuits their classic look.
