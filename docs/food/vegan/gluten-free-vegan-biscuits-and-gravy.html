Gluten-Free Vegan Biscuits and Gravy: A Delicious and Healthy Twist

Biscuits and gravy is a classic comfort food that has been enjoyed for generations. However, for those following a gluten-free or vegan diet, this dish can seem out of reach. Fortunately, with a few clever substitutions and a bit of creativity, you can enjoy a delicious, gluten-free, and vegan version of this beloved dish. In this article, we’ll explore how to make gluten-free vegan biscuits and gravy, discuss the health benefits of the ingredients used, and provide answers to some frequently asked questions.

Why Choose Gluten-Free Vegan Biscuits and Gravy?

Choosing a gluten-free and vegan version of biscuits and gravy offers several benefits. For those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, avoiding gluten is essential to prevent adverse health effects. Additionally, a vegan diet can help reduce inflammation, improve heart health, and support overall well-being. By using plant-based ingredients, you can enjoy a hearty and satisfying meal without compromising your dietary needs or ethical beliefs.

Ingredients for Gluten-Free Vegan Biscuits and Gravy

To make gluten-free vegan biscuits and gravy, you’ll need a combination of gluten-free flours, plant-based milk, and vegan butter. Here’s a breakdown of the key ingredients:

Ingredient Purpose
Gluten-Free Flour Blend Provides structure and texture to the biscuits.
Almond Milk or Soy Milk Adds moisture and richness to the biscuits and gravy.
Vegan Butter Creates a flaky texture in the biscuits and adds flavor to the gravy.
Nutritional Yeast Adds a cheesy, savory flavor to the gravy.
Vegetable Broth Forms the base of the gravy, providing depth of flavor.
Spices (e.g., garlic powder, onion powder, paprika) Enhances the flavor of the gravy.

How to Make Gluten-Free Vegan Biscuits

Making gluten-free vegan biscuits is easier than you might think. Start by preheating your oven to 425°F (220°C). In a large mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of gluten-free flour blend, 1 tablespoon of baking powder, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, and 1/4 teaspoon of baking soda. Cut in 1/2 cup of cold vegan butter until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add 3/4 cup of almond milk or soy milk, stirring until the dough comes together. Roll out the dough on a floured surface and cut out biscuits using a round cutter. Place the biscuits on a baking sheet and bake for 12-15 minutes, or until golden brown.

How to Make Vegan Gravy

While the biscuits are baking, you can prepare the vegan gravy. In a saucepan, melt 1/4 cup of vegan butter over medium heat. Whisk in 1/4 cup of gluten-free flour blend and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly. Gradually add 2 cups of vegetable broth, whisking continuously to prevent lumps. Stir in 1/4 cup of nutritional yeast, 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder, 1/2 teaspoon of onion powder, and 1/4 teaspoon of paprika. Cook the gravy until it thickens, then season with salt and pepper to taste.

Serving Suggestions

Once the biscuits are done, split them in half and spoon the warm gravy over the top. You can also add a side of sautéed greens or a fresh salad to round out the meal. For an extra touch of indulgence, consider adding a dollop of vegan sour cream or a sprinkle of chopped chives.

Health Benefits of Gluten-Free Vegan Biscuits and Gravy

This gluten-free vegan version of biscuits and gravy is not only delicious but also packed with health benefits. The gluten-free flour blend provides a good source of fiber, which can aid in digestion and promote gut health. Nutritional yeast is rich in B vitamins, which are essential for energy production and brain function. Additionally, using plant-based milk and butter reduces the intake of saturated fats, making this dish heart-healthy.

FAQs

Can I use a different type of gluten-free flour?

Yes, you can use any gluten-free flour blend that you prefer. However, keep in mind that different flours may affect the texture of the biscuits. A blend of rice flour, almond flour, and tapioca starch works well for a light and fluffy texture.

Can I make the gravy ahead of time?

Absolutely! The gravy can be made ahead of time and stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat it on the stove or in the microwave before serving.

What can I use instead of nutritional yeast?

If you don’t have nutritional yeast, you can use a small amount of miso paste or a sprinkle of vegan cheese for a similar savory flavor.

Are these biscuits freezer-friendly?

Yes, you can freeze the baked biscuits for up to 3 months. To reheat, simply place them in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for 10-15 minutes, or until warmed through.

Conclusion

Gluten-free vegan biscuits and gravy is a delightful and healthy twist on a classic comfort food. With the right ingredients and a little bit of effort, you can create a dish that is both satisfying and nourishing. Whether you’re following a gluten-free or vegan diet, or simply looking to try something new, this recipe is sure to become a favorite in your household. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to enjoy a plate of delicious, guilt-free biscuits and gravy!
