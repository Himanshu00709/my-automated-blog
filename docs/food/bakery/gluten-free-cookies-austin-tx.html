DIY Gluten-Free Cookie Recipes

If you prefer baking at home, here are a couple of simple gluten-free cookie recipes to try:

Classic Gluten-Free Chocolate Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup gluten-free flour blend
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup gluten-free chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour blend, almond flour, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, cream together the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in the vanilla extract and egg until well combined.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  6. Fold in the chocolate chips.
  7. Drop tablespoon-sized portions of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
  8. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown.
  9. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Cookies in Austin

Are gluten-free cookies healthier than regular cookies?

Not necessarily. While gluten-free cookies are a great option for those with gluten intolerance or celiac disease, they can still be high in sugar and calories. It's important to enjoy them in moderation as part of a balanced diet.

Can I find gluten-free cookies at regular bakeries in Austin?

Yes, many regular bakeries in Austin offer gluten-free options. However, it's always a good idea to check with the bakery to ensure that their gluten-free products are prepared in a dedicated gluten-free environment to avoid cross-contamination.

What are some popular gluten-free cookie flavors in Austin?

Popular gluten-free cookie flavors in Austin include classic chocolate chip, oatmeal raisin, peanut butter, and snickerdoodle. Many bakeries also offer seasonal flavors, such as pumpkin spice or peppermint, during the holidays.
