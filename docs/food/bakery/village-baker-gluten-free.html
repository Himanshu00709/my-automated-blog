The Rise of Gluten-Free Living

Gluten-free diets were once considered a niche market, primarily for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. However, over the past decade, the gluten-free movement has gained significant traction, with more people adopting this lifestyle for various health reasons. Gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, can cause adverse reactions in individuals with celiac disease, leading to symptoms such as digestive issues, fatigue, and even long-term health complications. For others, reducing gluten intake can lead to improved digestion, increased energy levels, and overall well-being.

Village Baker recognized this growing trend early on and has since dedicated itself to producing high-quality gluten-free products that do not compromise on taste or texture. Their offerings range from bread and rolls to muffins and cookies, all crafted with the finest gluten-free ingredients.
