How to Make Your Own Gluten-Free Apple Cider Donuts

If you’re feeling adventurous, why not try making your own gluten-free apple cider donuts at home? Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups gluten-free flour blend
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup apple cider
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup milk (dairy or non-dairy)
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tbsp melted butter or oil

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a donut pan.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  3. In another bowl, mix the apple cider, sugar, milk, egg, and melted butter.
  4. Combine the wet and dry ingredients, stirring until just combined.
  5. Pour the batter into the donut pan, filling each cavity about 2/3 full.
  6. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into a donut comes out clean.
  7. Allow the donuts to cool before serving. Enjoy!

FAQs About Gluten-Free Apple Cider Donuts

Are gluten-free apple cider donuts healthier?

Not necessarily. While gluten-free donuts are a great option for those with gluten sensitivities, they can still be high in sugar and calories. It’s always best to enjoy them in moderation.

Can I freeze gluten-free apple cider donuts?

Yes! Gluten-free apple cider donuts freeze well. Simply place them in an airtight container or freezer bag, and they’ll keep for up to 3 months. Thaw at room temperature or warm them up in the oven before serving.

What’s the best gluten-free flour for apple cider donuts?

A gluten-free flour blend that includes a mix of rice flour, tapioca starch, and potato starch works well for apple cider donuts. These blends mimic the texture of wheat flour and provide a light, fluffy result.
