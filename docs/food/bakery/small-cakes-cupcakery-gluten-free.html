The Rise of Gluten-Free Baking

Gluten-free baking has come a long way from its humble beginnings. Initially, gluten-free products were often dry, crumbly, and lacked the flavor that gluten-containing baked goods offered. However, with advancements in gluten-free flour blends and baking techniques, gluten-free baking has become an art form. Small Cakes Cupcakery has embraced this evolution, offering a wide range of gluten-free cupcakes that are moist, flavorful, and visually stunning.

Gluten-free baking requires a deep understanding of alternative flours and binders. Small Cakes Cupcakery uses a combination of rice flour, almond flour, and tapioca starch to create a texture that mimics traditional cupcakes. They also incorporate xanthan gum, a common gluten substitute, to provide the elasticity and structure that gluten typically offers. The result is a cupcake that is indistinguishable from its gluten-containing counterpart.

1. Are all cupcakes at Small Cakes Cupcakery gluten-free?

Yes, Small Cakes Cupcakery takes precautions to prevent cross-contamination by using a dedicated gluten-free facility. However, it's always best to inform the staff of any severe allergies when placing your order.

4. How should I store my gluten-free cupcakes?

Gluten-free cupcakes should be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two days. For longer storage, you can refrigerate them for up to a week or freeze them for up to three months.
