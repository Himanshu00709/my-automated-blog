Destin, Florida, is known for its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant food scene. Why Gluten-Free Pizza?

Gluten-free pizza has become increasingly popular as more people are diagnosed with celiac disease or choose to adopt a gluten-free lifestyle for health reasons. Gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, can cause severe digestive issues and other health problems for those with gluten intolerance. Fortunately, many pizzerias now offer gluten-free crusts made from alternative flours like rice, almond, or cauliflower, allowing everyone to enjoy this beloved dish.

What to Look for in a Gluten-Free Pizza

When ordering gluten-free pizza, it’s essential to ensure that the restaurant takes proper precautions to avoid cross-contamination. Here are some key factors to consider:

  • Dedicated Kitchen Space: Some restaurants have a separate area for preparing gluten-free meals to prevent cross-contamination.
  • Certified Gluten-Free Ingredients: Look for restaurants that use certified gluten-free ingredients to ensure safety.
  • Staff Training: Ensure that the staff is knowledgeable about gluten-free diets and can answer your questions confidently.

Making Gluten-Free Pizza at Home

If you prefer to make your own gluten-free pizza, there are plenty of recipes and pre-made crusts available. Here’s a simple recipe to get you started:

  1. Choose Your Crust: You can use a store-bought gluten-free crust or make your own using almond flour, rice flour, or cauliflower.
  2. Add Your Sauce: Use a gluten-free marinara or pesto sauce. Be sure to check the label for any hidden gluten.
  3. Top It Off: Add your favorite toppings, such as mozzarella cheese, pepperoni, mushrooms, or bell peppers.
  4. Bake: Preheat your oven to 450°F and bake your pizza for 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.
