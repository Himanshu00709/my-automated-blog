Gluten-Free Pizza at LaRosa's: A Delicious and Inclusive Option

For those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease, finding a safe and delicious pizza option can be a challenge. LaRosa's, a beloved pizza chain with deep roots in Cincinnati, has stepped up to the plate by offering a gluten-free pizza that doesn’t compromise on taste or quality. In this article, we’ll explore everything you need to know about LaRosa's gluten-free pizza, from its ingredients to its preparation, and why it’s a game-changer for gluten-free diners.

What Makes LaRosa's Gluten-Free Pizza Special?

LaRosa's gluten-free pizza is crafted with care to ensure that it meets the needs of those who must avoid gluten. The crust is made from a blend of rice flour, potato starch, and tapioca flour, which creates a light and crispy texture that rivals traditional pizza crusts. The pizza is topped with LaRosa's signature sauce, which is made from vine-ripened tomatoes and a blend of herbs and spices, and your choice of fresh toppings.

One of the standout features of LaRosa's gluten-free pizza is its preparation. The pizza is prepared in a dedicated gluten-free area to minimize the risk of cross-contamination. This attention to detail ensures that even those with severe gluten sensitivities can enjoy their pizza with peace of mind.

FAQs About LaRosa's Gluten-Free Pizza

Is LaRosa's gluten-free pizza safe for people with celiac disease?

Yes, LaRosa's gluten-free pizza is prepared in a dedicated gluten-free area to minimize the risk of cross-contamination. However, it’s always a good idea to inform the staff about your dietary restrictions to ensure extra precautions are taken.

What size is the gluten-free pizza?

LaRosa's gluten-free pizza is available in a 10-inch size, which is perfect for individual servings or sharing as an appetizer.

Can I order LaRosa's gluten-free pizza online?

Yes, LaRosa's offers online ordering for their gluten-free pizza, making it easy to enjoy a safe and delicious meal from the comfort of your home.

Are there any additional charges for gluten-free pizza?

There may be a small additional charge for the gluten-free crust, but the price is well worth it for the quality and care that goes into preparing the pizza.
