Hashbrown Casserole Cracker Barrel Gluten-Free Guide

Hashbrown casserole is a beloved comfort food, and Cracker Barrel’s version is particularly famous for its creamy, cheesy goodness. However, for those following a gluten-free diet, enjoying this dish can be a challenge. In this article, we’ll explore how to make a gluten-free version of Cracker Barrel’s hashbrown casserole, discuss its nutritional aspects, and answer some frequently asked questions.

What Makes Cracker Barrel’s Hashbrown Casserole So Special?

Cracker Barrel’s hashbrown casserole is a crowd-pleaser, known for its rich, creamy texture and savory flavor. The dish is made with shredded hashbrowns, a creamy cheese sauce, and a buttery topping that creates a perfect balance of flavors. However, the traditional recipe contains ingredients that are not gluten-free, such as cream of chicken soup, which often contains wheat-based thickeners.

How to Make Gluten-Free Hashbrown Casserole

Creating a gluten-free version of this classic dish is easier than you might think. By substituting a few key ingredients, you can enjoy the same delicious flavors without the gluten. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

Ingredients

Ingredient Quantity Notes
Frozen shredded hashbrowns 1 (30 oz) package Ensure they are gluten-free
Gluten-free cream of chicken soup 1 can (10.5 oz) Or make your own
Sour cream 1 cup Full-fat for best results
Shredded cheddar cheese 2 cups Sharp cheddar recommended
Butter 1/2 cup (1 stick) Melted
Onion powder 1 tsp Optional for added flavor
Salt and pepper To taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the hashbrowns, gluten-free cream of chicken soup, sour cream, shredded cheddar cheese, melted butter, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all ingredients are evenly distributed.
  3. Transfer the mixture to the prepared baking dish and spread it out evenly.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the casserole is bubbly.
  5. Let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy your gluten-free hashbrown casserole!

FAQs

Is Cracker Barrel’s hashbrown casserole gluten-free?

No, the traditional Cracker Barrel hashbrown casserole is not gluten-free due to the use of cream of chicken soup, which typically contains gluten. However, you can make a gluten-free version at home using gluten-free substitutes.

Can I use fresh potatoes instead of frozen hashbrowns?

Yes, you can use fresh potatoes. Simply shred them using a box grater or food processor, then rinse and pat them dry to remove excess starch before using them in the recipe.

What can I use instead of cream of chicken soup?

You can use a gluten-free cream of chicken soup available in stores, or make your own by combining gluten-free chicken broth, gluten-free flour, and cream. There are also many recipes online for homemade gluten-free cream soups.

Can I make this dish ahead of time?

Yes, you can prepare the casserole up to a day in advance. Simply cover it tightly with plastic wrap or aluminum foil and refrigerate. When you’re ready to bake, let it sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes before placing it in the oven.

Conclusion

Hashbrown casserole is a comforting and delicious dish that can easily be made gluten-free with a few simple substitutions. Whether you’re following a gluten-free diet or just looking for a new twist on a classic recipe, this gluten-free hashbrown casserole is sure to satisfy. With its creamy texture, cheesy flavor, and golden crust, it’s a dish that will become a favorite at your table. Enjoy!
