Gluten-Free Lefse with Instant Potatoes: A Delicious Tradition Reimagined

Lefse, a traditional Norwegian flatbread, has been a staple in Scandinavian households for generations. Made primarily from potatoes, flour, and butter, lefse is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed sweet or savory. However, for those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease, traditional lefse can be off-limits due to its wheat flour content. Enter gluten-free lefse made with instant potatoes—a modern twist on a classic recipe that allows everyone to enjoy this beloved treat.

What is Lefse?

Lefse is a soft, thin flatbread that resembles a tortilla or crepe. It is traditionally made with riced potatoes, flour, butter, and cream. The dough is rolled out thinly and cooked on a griddle until it develops light brown spots. Lefse is often served with butter, sugar, cinnamon, or even savory fillings like smoked salmon or cheese. It is a cherished part of Norwegian holiday celebrations, particularly during Christmas.

Why Go Gluten-Free?

Gluten-free diets have become increasingly popular, not only for those with celiac disease but also for individuals with gluten sensitivities or those seeking a healthier lifestyle. Traditional lefse recipes call for wheat flour, which contains gluten. By substituting gluten-free flour and using instant potatoes, you can create a version of lefse that is safe for gluten-free diets without sacrificing flavor or texture.

Ingredients for Gluten-Free Lefse with Instant Potatoes

Here’s what you’ll need to make gluten-free lefse:

Ingredient Quantity
Instant mashed potatoes 2 cups
Gluten-free flour blend 1 ½ cups
Butter (unsalted, melted) 4 tablespoons
Heavy cream ¼ cup
Salt ½ teaspoon
Water (as needed) 2-4 tablespoons

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Instant Potatoes

Start by preparing the instant mashed potatoes according to the package instructions. Allow them to cool completely before using them in the dough. This step is crucial to ensure the dough has the right consistency.

2. Mix the Dough

In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooled instant potatoes, gluten-free flour blend, melted butter, heavy cream, and salt. Mix until the ingredients come together to form a dough. If the dough feels too dry, add water one tablespoon at a time until it reaches a pliable consistency.

3. Roll Out the Dough

Divide the dough into small balls, about the size of a golf ball. On a lightly floured surface (use gluten-free flour), roll out each ball into a thin circle, approximately 8-10 inches in diameter. The thinner the dough, the more authentic your lefse will be.

4. Cook the Lefse

Heat a griddle or large skillet over medium heat. Carefully transfer the rolled-out dough to the griddle and cook for about 1-2 minutes on each side, or until light brown spots appear. Avoid overcooking, as this can make the lefse brittle.

5. Serve and Enjoy

Once cooked, stack the lefse on a plate and cover with a clean kitchen towel to keep them soft. Serve warm with your favorite toppings, such as butter, sugar, or jam.

Tips for Perfect Gluten-Free Lefse

  • Use a Lefse Stick: A lefse stick is a handy tool for transferring the delicate dough to the griddle without tearing it.
  • Keep the Dough Moist: Gluten-free dough can dry out quickly, so keep it covered with a damp cloth while rolling out the lefse.
  • Experiment with Toppings: While traditional toppings are delicious, don’t be afraid to get creative with fillings like Nutella, fresh berries, or even savory options like ham and cheese.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Lefse

Can I use fresh potatoes instead of instant potatoes?

Yes, you can use fresh potatoes. Simply boil, mash, and cool them before incorporating them into the dough. However, instant potatoes offer convenience and consistency, making them a great option for beginners.

What gluten-free flour works best for lefse?

A gluten-free flour blend that includes xanthan gum works best, as it helps mimic the elasticity of gluten. Popular brands include Bob’s Red Mill and King Arthur Flour.

How do I store leftover lefse?

Store leftover lefse in an airtight container or plastic bag at room temperature for up to 2 days. For longer storage, freeze the lefse between layers of parchment paper and reheat on a griddle when ready to serve.

Can I make lefse ahead of time?

Absolutely! Lefse can be made a day in advance and reheated before serving. Just be sure to keep it covered to prevent it from drying out.

Conclusion

Gluten-free lefse with instant potatoes is a wonderful way to enjoy a traditional Norwegian dish without compromising on taste or texture. Whether you’re new to lefse or a seasoned pro, this recipe is easy to follow and yields delicious results. By using gluten-free flour and instant potatoes, you can create a dish that is inclusive and accessible to everyone. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to enjoy a taste of Norway—gluten-free style!
