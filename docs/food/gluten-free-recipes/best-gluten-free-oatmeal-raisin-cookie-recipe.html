Best Gluten-Free Oatmeal Raisin Cookie Recipe

If you're on a gluten-free diet but still crave the comforting taste of homemade cookies, you're in for a treat. Oatmeal raisin cookies are a classic favorite, and with this gluten-free recipe, you can enjoy them without any worries. This recipe is not only delicious but also easy to make, ensuring that you can whip up a batch whenever the craving strikes. Let's dive into the details of how to make the best gluten-free oatmeal raisin cookies.

Why Choose Gluten-Free Oatmeal Raisin Cookies?

Gluten-free diets are essential for individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. However, even if you don't have these conditions, opting for gluten-free options can sometimes be a healthier choice. Oats are naturally gluten-free, but they can be contaminated with gluten during processing. Therefore, it's crucial to use certified gluten-free oats in this recipe. Oatmeal raisin cookies are a great source of fiber, and when made with wholesome ingredients, they can be a nutritious snack or dessert.

Ingredients You'll Need

To make the best gluten-free oatmeal raisin cookies, you'll need the following ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free rolled oats 2 cups
Gluten-free flour blend 1 cup
Baking soda 1 tsp
Salt 1/2 tsp
Unsalted butter (softened) 1/2 cup
Brown sugar 3/4 cup
Granulated sugar 1/4 cup
Egg 1 large
Vanilla extract 1 tsp
Raisins 1 cup

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Preheat Your Oven

Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). This ensures that your cookies bake evenly and achieve the perfect texture.

2. Prepare the Dry Ingredients

In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the gluten-free rolled oats, gluten-free flour blend, baking soda, and salt. Set this mixture aside.

3. Cream the Butter and Sugars

In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened unsalted butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until the mixture is light and fluffy. This usually takes about 2-3 minutes with an electric mixer.

4. Add the Egg and Vanilla

Beat in the egg and vanilla extract until well combined. The mixture should be smooth and slightly creamy.

5. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in tough cookies.

6. Fold in the Raisins

Gently fold in the raisins until they are evenly distributed throughout the dough.

7. Scoop and Bake

Using a cookie scoop or a tablespoon, drop rounded balls of dough onto a parchment-lined baking sheet. Space them about 2 inches apart to allow for spreading. Bake in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown.

8. Cool and Enjoy

Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for 5 minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely. Enjoy your gluten-free oatmeal raisin cookies with a glass of milk or your favorite beverage!

Tips for Perfect Gluten-Free Oatmeal Raisin Cookies

  • Use Certified Gluten-Free Oats: Ensure that the oats you use are certified gluten-free to avoid any cross-contamination.
  • Don't Overmix the Dough: Overmixing can lead to dense cookies. Mix until just combined for the best texture.
  • Chill the Dough: If you have time, chilling the dough for 30 minutes before baking can help the cookies hold their shape better.
  • Experiment with Add-Ins: Feel free to add nuts, chocolate chips, or other dried fruits to customize your cookies.

FAQs

Can I use quick oats instead of rolled oats?

Yes, you can use quick oats, but the texture of the cookies will be slightly different. Rolled oats provide a chewier texture, while quick oats will make the cookies softer.

Can I make these cookies vegan?

Yes, you can make these cookies vegan by substituting the butter with a plant-based alternative and using a flax egg (1 tablespoon ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tablespoons water) instead of a regular egg.

How should I store these cookies?

Store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days. You can also freeze the dough or baked cookies for up to 3 months.

Can I use a different type of flour?

Yes, you can experiment with different gluten-free flour blends, but the texture and flavor may vary slightly. Almond flour or coconut flour can also be used, but you may need to adjust the quantities.
