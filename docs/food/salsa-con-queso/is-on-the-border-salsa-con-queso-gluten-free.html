Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to more severe health complications. Therefore, it's essential to carefully examine the ingredients of any food product, especially processed ones like dips and sauces.

Ingredients in On The Border Salsa Con Queso

To determine whether On The Border Salsa Con Queso is gluten-free, we need to take a closer look at its ingredients. The primary ingredients typically include:

  • Cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack)
  • Tomatoes
  • Green chilies
  • Onions
  • Spices
  • Stabilizers and emulsifiers

At first glance, these ingredients do not appear to contain gluten. However, it's important to consider potential cross-contamination during manufacturing or the presence of hidden gluten in additives.

Is On The Border Salsa Con Queso Certified Gluten-Free?

As of the latest information available, On The Border Salsa Con Queso is not certified gluten-free. While the ingredients themselves do not contain gluten, the product is not labeled as gluten-free, which means there could be a risk of cross-contamination during production. For individuals with severe gluten intolerance or celiac disease, this is an important consideration.

Cross-Contamination Risks

Cross-contamination can occur when gluten-free products are processed in facilities that also handle gluten-containing ingredients. Even trace amounts of gluten can cause reactions in sensitive individuals. Since On The Border Salsa Con Queso is not certified gluten-free, it's possible that it may have come into contact with gluten during manufacturing.
