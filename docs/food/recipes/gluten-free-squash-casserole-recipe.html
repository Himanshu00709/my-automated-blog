Gluten-Free Squash Casserole Recipe

When it comes to comfort food, few dishes can rival the creamy, savory goodness of a squash casserole. But for those who follow a gluten-free diet, finding a recipe that fits their dietary needs can be a challenge. That's where this gluten-free squash casserole recipe comes in. Packed with flavor, easy to make, and completely free of gluten, this dish is sure to become a family favorite. Whether you're hosting a holiday dinner or just looking for a cozy weeknight meal, this casserole is the perfect choice.

Why Choose a Gluten-Free Squash Casserole?

Gluten-free diets have become increasingly popular, not just for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, but also for people looking to improve their overall health. Gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, can cause digestive issues and inflammation in some individuals. By opting for a gluten-free squash casserole, you can enjoy all the flavors of a traditional casserole without the discomfort or health risks associated with gluten.

Moreover, squash is a nutrient-dense vegetable that is rich in vitamins A and C, fiber, and antioxidants. When combined with other wholesome ingredients, this casserole becomes a powerhouse of nutrition, making it a great addition to any meal plan.

Ingredients You'll Need

Before you start cooking, make sure you have all the necessary ingredients on hand. Here's what you'll need:

Ingredient Quantity
Yellow squash 4 cups, sliced
Zucchini 2 cups, sliced
Onion 1 medium, chopped
Gluten-free breadcrumbs 1 cup
Shredded cheddar cheese 1 cup
Eggs 2 large
Milk (or dairy-free alternative) 1/2 cup
Butter (or dairy-free alternative) 2 tablespoons
Salt 1 teaspoon
Black pepper 1/2 teaspoon
Garlic powder 1/2 teaspoon

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preheat and Prepare

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). While the oven is heating, grease a 9x13 inch baking dish with butter or a non-stick cooking spray.

Step 2: Cook the Vegetables

In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it becomes translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the sliced yellow squash and zucchini to the skillet, and cook for another 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender. Season with salt, pepper, and garlic powder.

Step 3: Mix the Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked vegetables, gluten-free breadcrumbs, shredded cheddar cheese, eggs, and milk. Stir until all the ingredients are well combined.

Step 4: Assemble the Casserole

Transfer the mixture to the prepared baking dish, spreading it out evenly. If desired, sprinkle a little extra cheese on top for added flavor.

Step 5: Bake

Place the baking dish in the preheated oven and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the casserole is set. You can test for doneness by inserting a knife into the center; it should come out clean.

Step 6: Serve and Enjoy

Remove the casserole from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. This dish pairs wonderfully with a fresh salad or a side of roasted vegetables.

FAQs

Can I make this casserole ahead of time?

Yes, you can prepare the casserole up to a day in advance. Simply assemble the dish, cover it with plastic wrap, and store it in the refrigerator. When you're ready to bake, remove the plastic wrap and place the dish in the oven. You may need to add a few extra minutes to the baking time if the casserole is cold from the fridge.

Can I freeze this casserole?

Absolutely! This casserole freezes well. After baking, let it cool completely, then wrap it tightly in aluminum foil or plastic wrap. It can be stored in the freezer for up to 3 months. To reheat, thaw the casserole in the refrigerator overnight and then bake it in a 350°F oven until heated through.

What can I use instead of gluten-free breadcrumbs?

If you don't have gluten-free breadcrumbs on hand, you can use crushed gluten-free crackers, almond flour, or even rolled oats as a substitute. Each option will give the casserole a slightly different texture, but all will work well in this recipe.

Is this recipe dairy-free?

This recipe can easily be made dairy-free by using dairy-free cheese, milk, and butter alternatives. Just make sure to choose products that are labeled as gluten-free if you're strictly avoiding gluten.
