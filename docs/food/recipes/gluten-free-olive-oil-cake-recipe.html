Gluten-Free Olive Oil Cake Recipe

Gluten-free baking has become increasingly popular, not only among those with celiac disease or gluten intolerance but also among health-conscious individuals looking to reduce their gluten intake. One of the most delightful and versatile gluten-free desserts you can make is an olive oil cake. This cake is moist, flavorful, and incredibly easy to prepare. In this blog post, we’ll walk you through a detailed recipe for a gluten-free olive oil cake, along with tips, variations, and answers to frequently asked questions.

Why Choose Olive Oil for Baking?

Olive oil is a fantastic ingredient for baking, especially in gluten-free recipes. It adds moisture to the cake, which is crucial when working with gluten-free flours that can sometimes result in dry baked goods. Additionally, olive oil imparts a rich, fruity flavor that complements the other ingredients beautifully. Extra virgin olive oil is the best choice for this recipe, as it has a more pronounced flavor and higher nutritional value compared to refined olive oils.

Ingredients

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s take a look at the ingredients you’ll need:

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free flour blend 1 ½ cups
Almond flour ½ cup
Baking powder 1 ½ teaspoons
Salt ½ teaspoon
Granulated sugar 1 cup
Extra virgin olive oil ¾ cup
Eggs 3 large
Vanilla extract 1 teaspoon
Lemon zest 1 tablespoon
Lemon juice 2 tablespoons
Milk (or dairy-free alternative) ½ cup

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9-inch round cake pan with olive oil and line the bottom with parchment paper to ensure easy removal of the cake after baking.

Step 2: Mix the Dry Ingredients

In a medium-sized bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour blend, almond flour, baking powder, and salt. Set this mixture aside.

Step 3: Combine the Wet Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the sugar and olive oil until well combined. Add the eggs one at a time, whisking after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract, lemon zest, and lemon juice.

Step 4: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a dense cake. Finally, stir in the milk until the batter is smooth and well incorporated.

Step 5: Bake the Cake

Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Bake in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out clean.

Step 6: Cool and Serve

Allow the cake to cool in the pan for about 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely. Once cooled, you can dust the cake with powdered sugar or serve it with a dollop of whipped cream or fresh berries.

Tips for Success

  • Choose the Right Flour: Not all gluten-free flours are created equal. A blend that includes rice flour, tapioca flour, and potato starch works well for this recipe. Avoid using single-grain flours like almond or coconut flour alone, as they can result in a dense cake.
  • Room Temperature Ingredients: Make sure your eggs and milk are at room temperature before you start. This helps the ingredients blend more easily and results in a smoother batter.
  • Don’t Overmix: Overmixing the batter can lead to a tough cake. Mix until the ingredients are just combined.
  • Experiment with Flavors: While this recipe uses lemon zest and juice, you can experiment with other flavors like orange zest, almond extract, or even a splash of liqueur.

FAQs

Can I use a different type of oil?

Yes, you can use other oils like coconut oil or vegetable oil, but keep in mind that the flavor will be different. Olive oil adds a unique richness to the cake that other oils may not provide.

Can I make this cake dairy-free?

Absolutely! Simply replace the milk with a dairy-free alternative like almond milk, oat milk, or coconut milk.

How should I store the cake?

Store the cake in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days, or in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. You can also freeze the cake for up to 3 months.

Can I add toppings to the cake?

Yes, you can top the cake with fresh fruit, a glaze, or even a dusting of powdered sugar. Get creative with your toppings to make the cake your own!

Conclusion

This gluten-free olive oil cake is a perfect example of how simple ingredients can come together to create something truly special. Whether you’re serving it as a dessert, a snack, or even breakfast, this cake is sure to impress. The combination of olive oil, lemon, and almond flour creates a moist, flavorful cake that’s both healthy and delicious. Give this recipe a try, and don’t be afraid to experiment with different flavors and toppings to make it your own. Happy baking!
