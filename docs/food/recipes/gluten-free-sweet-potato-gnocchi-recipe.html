Gluten-Free Sweet Potato Gnocchi Recipe

Gnocchi, the soft and pillowy Italian dumplings, are a beloved comfort food. Traditionally made with wheat flour, they can be a challenge for those following a gluten-free diet. However, with a few simple substitutions, you can enjoy this delicious dish without compromising on taste or texture. This recipe for gluten-free sweet potato gnocchi is not only easy to make but also packed with nutrients, making it a perfect addition to your gluten-free repertoire.

Why Sweet Potato Gnocchi?

Sweet potatoes are a fantastic base for gluten-free gnocchi. They are naturally gluten-free, rich in vitamins A and C, and provide a subtle sweetness that pairs beautifully with savory sauces. The natural moisture in sweet potatoes also helps bind the dough, reducing the need for excessive flour. This results in a lighter, more delicate gnocchi that is both satisfying and nutritious.

Ingredients

Here’s what you’ll need to make gluten-free sweet potato gnocchi:

Ingredient Quantity
Sweet potatoes 2 medium-sized
Gluten-free flour (e.g., rice flour or almond flour) 1 1/2 cups
Egg 1 large
Salt 1/2 teaspoon
Nutmeg (optional) 1/4 teaspoon
Olive oil 1 tablespoon

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Sweet Potatoes

Start by preheating your oven to 400°F (200°C). Wash the sweet potatoes thoroughly and pierce them several times with a fork. Place them on a baking sheet and bake for about 45-60 minutes, or until they are tender and can be easily pierced with a fork. Once baked, let them cool slightly, then peel off the skin and mash the flesh in a large bowl until smooth.

2. Make the Dough

In the bowl with the mashed sweet potatoes, add the gluten-free flour, egg, salt, and nutmeg (if using). Mix everything together until a dough forms. Be careful not to overmix, as this can make the gnocchi tough. If the dough is too sticky, add a bit more flour, but do so sparingly to keep the gnocchi light.

3. Shape the Gnocchi

Divide the dough into four equal parts. On a lightly floured surface, roll each portion into a long rope about 1/2 inch thick. Cut the ropes into 1-inch pieces. If desired, you can use a fork to create ridges on each piece, which helps the gnocchi hold onto sauce better.

4. Cook the Gnocchi

Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the gnocchi in batches, being careful not to overcrowd the pot. Cook for about 2-3 minutes, or until the gnocchi float to the surface. Remove them with a slotted spoon and place them on a plate. Drizzle with a bit of olive oil to prevent sticking.

5. Serve and Enjoy

Your gluten-free sweet potato gnocchi is now ready to be served! You can toss them with your favorite sauce, such as a simple brown butter and sage sauce, a creamy Alfredo, or a light tomato basil sauce. Garnish with fresh herbs and grated Parmesan cheese for an extra touch of flavor.

FAQs

Can I make the gnocchi ahead of time?

Yes, you can prepare the gnocchi ahead of time. After shaping, place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper and freeze until solid. Once frozen, transfer them to a zip-top bag and store in the freezer for up to 2 months. Cook directly from frozen, adding an extra minute or two to the cooking time.

What gluten-free flour works best for this recipe?

Rice flour and almond flour are both excellent choices for this recipe. Rice flour provides a neutral flavor and light texture, while almond flour adds a slightly nutty taste and extra moisture. You can also use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend if you prefer.

Can I use regular potatoes instead of sweet potatoes?

Absolutely! Regular potatoes can be used in place of sweet potatoes if you prefer a more traditional gnocchi flavor. Just be sure to use starchy potatoes like Russets, and adjust the amount of flour as needed to achieve the right dough consistency.

How do I prevent the gnocchi from becoming too dense?

The key to light and fluffy gnocchi is to avoid overmixing the dough and to use just enough flour to bring the dough together. Overworking the dough or adding too much flour can result in dense, heavy gnocchi. Handle the dough gently and add flour sparingly.

Conclusion

Gluten-free sweet potato gnocchi is a delightful and nutritious alternative to traditional gnocchi. With its vibrant color, subtle sweetness, and tender texture, it’s sure to become a favorite in your household. Whether you’re cooking for someone with gluten intolerance or simply looking to try something new, this recipe is a must-try. Enjoy the process of making these little dumplings from scratch, and savor the delicious results!
