Gluten-Free Cranberry Orange Bread Recipe

Gluten-free baking has become increasingly popular, and for good reason. Whether you have celiac disease, a gluten intolerance, or simply want to explore healthier baking options, gluten-free recipes can be both delicious and nutritious. Today, we’re diving into a delightful gluten-free cranberry orange bread recipe that’s perfect for breakfast, a snack, or even dessert. This bread is moist, flavorful, and packed with the tangy sweetness of cranberries and the bright citrus notes of orange. Let’s get started!

Why Choose Gluten-Free Cranberry Orange Bread?

Gluten-free baking doesn’t have to be bland or dry. This cranberry orange bread is a testament to how flavorful and satisfying gluten-free recipes can be. The combination of tart cranberries and zesty orange creates a perfect balance of flavors, while the gluten-free flour blend ensures a soft, tender crumb. Plus, this recipe is easy to make and requires no special equipment. Whether you’re new to gluten-free baking or a seasoned pro, this recipe is sure to become a favorite.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before we dive into the recipe, let’s take a look at the ingredients you’ll need. Most of these are pantry staples, and the rest can be easily found at your local grocery store.

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free flour blend 2 cups
Baking powder 1 1/2 tsp
Baking soda 1/2 tsp
Salt 1/4 tsp
Unsalted butter (softened) 1/2 cup
Granulated sugar 3/4 cup
Eggs 2 large
Orange zest 1 tbsp
Fresh orange juice 1/2 cup
Plain Greek yogurt 1/2 cup
Fresh or frozen cranberries 1 cup

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that you have all your ingredients ready, let’s walk through the steps to make this delicious gluten-free cranberry orange bread.

Step 1: Preheat and Prepare

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan and line it with parchment paper, leaving some overhang on the sides for easy removal.

Step 2: Mix Dry Ingredients

In a medium bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour blend, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.

Step 3: Cream Butter and Sugar

In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy. This should take about 2-3 minutes with an electric mixer.

Step 4: Add Wet Ingredients

Beat in the eggs one at a time, ensuring each egg is fully incorporated before adding the next. Then, mix in the orange zest, orange juice, and Greek yogurt. The mixture should be smooth and well combined.

Step 5: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing on low speed until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a dense loaf.

Step 6: Fold in Cranberries

Gently fold in the cranberries using a spatula. If you’re using frozen cranberries, there’s no need to thaw them beforehand.

Step 7: Bake

Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Bake in the preheated oven for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Step 8: Cool and Serve

Allow the bread to cool in the pan for about 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely. Slice and serve!

FAQs

Can I use a different type of flour?

Yes, you can use a different gluten-free flour blend, but make sure it’s a 1:1 substitute for all-purpose flour. Almond flour or coconut flour won’t work as a direct substitute in this recipe.

Can I use dried cranberries instead of fresh?

Yes, you can use dried cranberries, but keep in mind that they are sweeter and less tart than fresh cranberries. You may want to reduce the sugar slightly if using dried cranberries.

How should I store the bread?

Store the bread in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or in the refrigerator for up to a week. You can also freeze it for up to 3 months.

Can I make this recipe vegan?

Yes, you can make this recipe vegan by substituting the butter with coconut oil, the eggs with flax eggs, and the Greek yogurt with a plant-based yogurt.
