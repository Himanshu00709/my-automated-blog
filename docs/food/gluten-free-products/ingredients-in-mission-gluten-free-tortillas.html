Ingredients in Mission Gluten-Free Tortillas: A Comprehensive Guide

Gluten-free diets have become increasingly popular in recent years, whether due to celiac disease, gluten sensitivity, or personal preference. Mission Foods, a well-known brand in the tortilla industry, has responded to this demand by offering gluten-free tortillas. But what exactly goes into these tortillas? In this article, we’ll take a deep dive into the ingredients used in Mission Gluten-Free Tortillas, their nutritional benefits, and how they compare to traditional tortillas.

What Are Mission Gluten-Free Tortillas Made Of?

Mission Gluten-Free Tortillas are crafted with a blend of alternative flours and ingredients to ensure they are safe for those avoiding gluten. The primary ingredients include:

Ingredient Purpose
Brown Rice Flour Provides a gluten-free base and adds a mild, nutty flavor.
Tapioca Starch Enhances texture and elasticity, mimicking the chewiness of traditional tortillas.
Potato Starch Adds softness and helps retain moisture.
Water Acts as a binding agent for the dough.
Vegetable Shortening Improves flexibility and prevents the tortillas from becoming brittle.
Xanthan Gum Acts as a stabilizer and thickener, replacing the role of gluten.
Salt Enhances flavor and acts as a preservative.

These ingredients work together to create a tortilla that is not only gluten-free but also soft, pliable, and versatile for various culinary uses.

How Do Mission Gluten-Free Tortillas Compare to Traditional Tortillas?

Traditional tortillas are typically made from wheat flour, which contains gluten. This gives them a distinct texture and elasticity that can be challenging to replicate in gluten-free versions. However, Mission Gluten-Free Tortillas come remarkably close. Here’s a comparison:

Aspect Mission Gluten-Free Tortillas Traditional Wheat Tortillas
Texture Soft and pliable, with a slight chewiness Soft and elastic, with a more pronounced chew
Flavor Mild, slightly nutty Neutral, with a wheaty undertone
Calories 90 per tortilla 100-120 per tortilla
Protein Content 1g per tortilla 3-4g per tortilla
Gluten Content Gluten-free Contains gluten

While there are some differences, Mission Gluten-Free Tortillas are an excellent alternative for those who need or prefer to avoid gluten without sacrificing taste or texture.

FAQs About Mission Gluten-Free Tortillas

Are Mission Gluten-Free Tortillas suitable for vegans?

Yes, Mission Gluten-Free Tortillas are vegan-friendly. They do not contain any animal-derived ingredients.

Can I use Mission Gluten-Free Tortillas for baking?

Absolutely! These tortillas are versatile and can be used for wraps, quesadillas, and even as a base for gluten-free pizzas.

Do Mission Gluten-Free Tortillas contain any allergens?

While they are gluten-free, they are produced in facilities that also process wheat, so cross-contamination is possible. Always check the packaging for the most current allergen information.

How should I store Mission Gluten-Free Tortillas?

Store them in a cool, dry place. Once opened, keep them in an airtight container or resealable bag to maintain freshness.

Conclusion

Mission Gluten-Free Tortillas are a fantastic option for anyone looking to enjoy tortillas without the gluten. Made with a thoughtful blend of alternative flours and ingredients, they offer a texture and flavor that closely mimic traditional tortillas. Whether you’re following a gluten-free diet or simply exploring new options, these tortillas are a versatile and nutritious choice for your meals. With their low calorie count and vegan-friendly ingredients, they’re a great addition to any kitchen pantry.
