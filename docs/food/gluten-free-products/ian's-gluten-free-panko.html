What is Panko, and Why Go Gluten-Free?

Panko is a type of breadcrumb that originated in Japan. Unlike traditional breadcrumbs, panko is made from bread without crusts, resulting in a lighter, flakier texture that is perfect for coating and frying. Panko is widely used in dishes like tonkatsu (Japanese pork cutlets), tempura, and as a topping for casseroles and baked dishes. However, traditional panko is made from wheat bread, making it unsuitable for those who need to avoid gluten.

For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, including digestive issues, fatigue, and even long-term health complications. This is where Ian's Gluten-Free Panko comes in. Made from gluten-free ingredients, this product allows everyone to enjoy the crispy, crunchy texture of panko without the worry of gluten-related health issues.
