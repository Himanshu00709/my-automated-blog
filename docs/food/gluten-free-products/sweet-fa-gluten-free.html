The Importance of Gluten-Free Diets

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. While it is harmless for most people, it can cause serious health issues for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. Consuming gluten can lead to symptoms such as bloating, diarrhea, fatigue, and even long-term damage to the small intestine. For these individuals, a gluten-free diet is not just a lifestyle choice but a medical necessity.

Who Should Consider a Gluten-Free Diet?

  • Celiac Disease Patients: A strict gluten-free diet is essential for managing this autoimmune disorder.
  • Gluten Sensitivity: Individuals who experience discomfort after consuming gluten may benefit from eliminating it from their diet.
  • Wheat Allergy: Those allergic to wheat should avoid gluten-containing products.
  • General Health: Some people choose a gluten-free diet for perceived health benefits, though it's important to consult a healthcare provider before making significant dietary changes.
