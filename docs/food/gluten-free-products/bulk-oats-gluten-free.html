In recent years, the demand for gluten-free products has skyrocketed, driven by the increasing awareness of celiac disease and gluten sensitivity. What Are Gluten-Free Oats?

Gluten-free oats are oats that have been processed and handled in a way that prevents cross-contamination with gluten-containing grains like wheat, barley, and rye. While oats themselves are naturally gluten-free, they are often processed in facilities that also handle gluten-containing grains, which can lead to contamination. Gluten-free oats are grown, harvested, and processed in dedicated gluten-free facilities to ensure their purity.

FAQs About Bulk Oats Gluten Free

Are all oats gluten-free?

No, not all oats are gluten-free. While oats are naturally gluten-free, they are often contaminated with gluten during processing. It's important to look for oats that are specifically labeled as gluten-free.

Can people with celiac disease eat gluten-free oats?

Yes, people with celiac disease can safely consume gluten-free oats, provided they are certified gluten-free and have not been cross-contaminated with gluten-containing grains.

How should I store bulk oats gluten free?

Bulk oats gluten free should be stored in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. Proper storage will help maintain their freshness and prevent them from becoming rancid.

Can I use gluten-free oats in place of regular oats in recipes?

Yes, gluten-free oats can be used in place of regular oats in any recipe. They have the same texture and flavor, making them a suitable substitute.
