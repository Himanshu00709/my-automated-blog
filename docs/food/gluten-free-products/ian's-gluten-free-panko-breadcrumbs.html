What Are Panko Breadcrumbs?

Panko breadcrumbs are a type of Japanese breadcrumb known for their light, airy texture and ability to create a crispy coating when used in cooking. Unlike traditional breadcrumbs, which are made from finely ground bread, panko is made from crustless bread that is processed into large, flaky crumbs. This unique texture allows panko to absorb less oil, resulting in a lighter, crispier finish.

Traditional panko breadcrumbs are made from wheat bread, which means they contain gluten. However, Ian's Gluten-Free Panko Breadcrumbs are made from a blend of rice flour, corn flour, and other gluten-free ingredients, making them a safe and delicious alternative for those avoiding gluten.
