Understanding Gluten and Its Impact on Health

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. For individuals with celiac disease, consuming gluten can lead to severe health complications, including damage to the small intestine, nutrient malabsorption, and a host of other symptoms like fatigue, bloating, and joint pain. Even for those without celiac disease, gluten sensitivity can cause discomfort and digestive issues. As a result, many people are opting for gluten-free diets to improve their overall health and well-being.

Black Tap recognizes the importance of catering to this growing demographic. Their gluten-free menu is designed to provide delicious options without compromising on taste or quality. Whether you're a celiac sufferer or simply looking to reduce your gluten intake, Black Tap has something for everyone.

Tips for Dining Out Gluten-Free at Black Tap

Dining out gluten-free can be challenging, but with a few tips, you can enjoy your meal at Black Tap without worry:

  • Communicate Your Needs: Always inform your server about your gluten intolerance or celiac disease. Black Tap staff are trained to accommodate dietary restrictions and will guide you through the menu.
  • Ask About Preparation: Inquire about how your food is prepared to avoid cross-contamination. Black Tap takes precautions, but it’s always best to double-check.
  • Stick to Safe Options: Opt for items that are naturally gluten-free, like salads or grilled meats, and avoid anything fried unless confirmed to be gluten-free.
  • Enjoy the Shakes: Black Tap’s milkshakes are a gluten-free indulgence, so don’t hesitate to treat yourself!
