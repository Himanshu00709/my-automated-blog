What Does Gluten-Free Mean?

Before we delve into the specifics of Great Value Gummy Worms, it's essential to understand what "gluten-free" means. Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, including digestive issues, fatigue, and even long-term health complications. A gluten-free diet is the only effective treatment for these conditions.

Foods labeled as "gluten-free" must meet strict regulatory standards. In the United States, the FDA requires that foods labeled as gluten-free contain less than 20 parts per million (ppm) of gluten. This threshold is considered safe for most people with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity.

Are Great Value Gummy Worms Certified Gluten-Free?

While the ingredients in Great Value Gummy Worms do not contain gluten, the product is not certified gluten-free by a third-party organization. Certification by organizations like the Gluten-Free Certification Organization (GFCO) ensures that a product has been rigorously tested and meets strict gluten-free standards. Without this certification, there is a possibility of cross-contamination during the manufacturing process.

If you have celiac disease or a severe gluten sensitivity, it's always a good idea to look for products that are certified gluten-free. However, for those with mild gluten sensitivities, Great Value Gummy Worms may still be a safe option, as the ingredients themselves are gluten-free.
