What is Daura Damm?

Daura Damm is a gluten-free beer produced by the Spanish brewery, Damm. It is crafted using traditional brewing methods but with a twist—it is made from barley that has been specially treated to remove gluten. The result is a beer that is safe for those with gluten intolerance while still retaining the rich, full-bodied flavor that beer enthusiasts love.

The Brewing Process

The brewing process of Daura Damm is a fascinating blend of tradition and innovation. Here’s a step-by-step breakdown:

Step Description
1. Malting Barley is soaked in water and allowed to germinate, producing enzymes that convert starches into fermentable sugars.
2. Mashing The malted barley is mixed with hot water to create a mash, which is then heated to extract sugars.
3. Lautering The liquid (wort) is separated from the grain husks.
4. Boiling The wort is boiled and hops are added for flavor and aroma.
5. Fermentation Yeast is added to the cooled wort, converting sugars into alcohol and carbon dioxide.
6. Gluten Removal The beer undergoes a special process to remove gluten, ensuring it meets gluten-free standards.
7. Packaging The beer is filtered, carbonated, and packaged for distribution.
