Is Russell Stover Gluten-Free? A Comprehensive Guide

For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, finding safe and delicious treats can be a challenge. Russell Stover, a well-known brand in the confectionery world, offers a wide range of chocolates and candies. But is Russell Stover gluten-free? Understanding Gluten and Its Impact

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For people with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to more severe health complications. Therefore, it’s crucial for these individuals to avoid gluten in their diet. This includes being vigilant about the ingredients in the foods they consume, especially when it comes to treats like chocolates and candies.

Russell Stover’s Gluten-Free Commitment

Russell Stover has made efforts to cater to the needs of gluten-sensitive consumers. The company has a dedicated section on their website that lists gluten-free products. However, it’s important to note that while some Russell Stover products are gluten-free, not all of them are. The company clearly labels their gluten-free products, making it easier for consumers to identify safe options.

Ingredients to Watch Out For

While many Russell Stover products are gluten-free, there are certain ingredients that could potentially contain gluten. These include:

Ingredient Potential Gluten Source
Malt Often derived from barley, which contains gluten
Modified Food Starch Can be derived from wheat
Natural Flavors May contain gluten-containing ingredients

If you’re unsure about any ingredient, it’s best to contact Russell Stover directly or consult their gluten-free product list.

Cross-Contamination Risks

Even if a product is labeled gluten-free, there’s always a risk of cross-contamination during manufacturing. Russell Stover states that they take precautions to prevent cross-contamination, but they also acknowledge that their facilities handle gluten-containing ingredients. Therefore, individuals with severe gluten sensitivity or celiac disease should exercise caution and consider their personal tolerance levels.
