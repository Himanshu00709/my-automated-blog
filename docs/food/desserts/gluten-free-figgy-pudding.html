Gluten-Free Figgy Pudding: A Delicious Holiday Tradition

When the holiday season rolls around, few desserts evoke the warmth and nostalgia of figgy pudding. This traditional British dessert, often associated with Christmas, has been enjoyed for centuries. However, for those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease, enjoying this classic treat can be a challenge. Enter gluten-free figgy pudding—a delightful adaptation that allows everyone to partake in this festive tradition. In this article, we’ll explore the history of figgy pudding, how to make a gluten-free version, and why it’s a must-try for your holiday table.

The History of Figgy Pudding

Figgy pudding, also known as plum pudding, has its roots in medieval England. Despite its name, the dessert traditionally contains no plums. Instead, it’s a rich, dense cake filled with dried fruits, spices, and sometimes alcohol. The term "figgy" refers to the inclusion of figs, which were a common ingredient in early recipes. Over time, figgy pudding became synonymous with Christmas, thanks in part to its mention in the popular carol "We Wish You a Merry Christmas," which famously demands, "Now bring us some figgy pudding!"

Traditionally, figgy pudding is made with wheat flour, which poses a problem for those avoiding gluten. However, with the rise of gluten-free diets, many bakers have adapted the recipe to accommodate these dietary needs without sacrificing flavor or texture.

How to Make Gluten-Free Figgy Pudding

Making gluten-free figgy pudding is easier than you might think. The key is to use a high-quality gluten-free flour blend that mimics the texture of wheat flour. Here’s a step-by-step guide to creating this holiday classic:

Ingredients

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free flour blend 1 ½ cups
Dried figs (chopped) 1 cup
Raisins ½ cup
Currants ½ cup
Brown sugar ½ cup
Unsalted butter (softened) ½ cup
Eggs 2 large
Milk (or dairy-free alternative) ½ cup
Ground cinnamon 1 tsp
Ground nutmeg ½ tsp
Ground cloves ¼ tsp
Brandy or rum (optional) 2 tbsp

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a pudding basin or heatproof bowl.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the butter and brown sugar until light and fluffy.
  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time, ensuring each is fully incorporated before adding the next.
  4. Gradually add the gluten-free flour blend, spices, and milk, mixing until a smooth batter forms.
  5. Fold in the dried figs, raisins, currants, and brandy or rum (if using).
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared pudding basin and cover with a lid or aluminum foil.
  7. Place the basin in a large pot and add enough boiling water to reach halfway up the sides of the basin.
  8. Steam the pudding for 2-2.5 hours, checking occasionally to ensure the water hasn’t evaporated.
  9. Once cooked, remove the pudding from the pot and let it cool slightly before turning it out onto a serving plate.
  10. Serve warm with a dollop of whipped cream or a drizzle of brandy sauce.

Why Gluten-Free Figgy Pudding is a Holiday Must-Try

Gluten-free figgy pudding is more than just a dessert—it’s a celebration of tradition and inclusivity. By adapting this classic recipe, you can ensure that everyone at your holiday table can enjoy a slice of history. The rich, spiced flavors of the pudding, combined with the sweetness of the dried fruits, make it a perfect ending to any festive meal. Plus, the gluten-free version is just as moist and flavorful as the original, proving that dietary restrictions don’t have to mean sacrificing taste.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Figgy Pudding

Can I make figgy pudding ahead of time?

Yes! Figgy pudding actually tastes better when made a day or two in advance. Simply store it in an airtight container and reheat before serving.

What can I use instead of brandy or rum?

If you prefer to avoid alcohol, you can substitute the brandy or rum with apple juice or orange juice for a similar depth of flavor.

Is gluten-free figgy pudding suitable for vegans?

This recipe can be made vegan by replacing the butter with a plant-based alternative, using flax eggs instead of regular eggs, and opting for a dairy-free milk.

Conclusion

Gluten-free figgy pudding is a wonderful way to bring a touch of tradition to your holiday celebrations while accommodating dietary needs. With its rich, spiced flavors and moist texture, it’s a dessert that’s sure to impress. Whether you’re new to figgy pudding or a longtime fan, this gluten-free version is a must-try for your festive table. So gather your ingredients, put on some holiday music, and get ready to create a dessert that’s as memorable as it is delicious.
