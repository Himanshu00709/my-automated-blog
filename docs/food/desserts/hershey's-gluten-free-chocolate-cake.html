Hershey's Gluten-Free Chocolate Cake: A Delicious Treat for Everyone

Chocolate cake is a timeless dessert that brings joy to any occasion. For those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease, finding a delicious and safe chocolate cake can be a challenge. Enter Hershey's Gluten-Free Chocolate Cake—a rich, moist, and decadent dessert that everyone can enjoy. Whether you're hosting a party, celebrating a birthday, or simply craving something sweet, this cake is sure to satisfy your chocolate cravings without compromising on taste or texture.

Why Choose Hershey's Gluten-Free Chocolate Cake?

Hershey's has long been synonymous with high-quality chocolate, and their gluten-free chocolate cake is no exception. Made with Hershey's cocoa powder, this cake delivers the same rich chocolate flavor that you know and love, but without the gluten. The recipe is simple, using readily available gluten-free ingredients, making it accessible for home bakers of all skill levels. Plus, it's versatile—you can customize it with your favorite frostings, toppings, or fillings.

Ingredients You'll Need

To make Hershey's Gluten-Free Chocolate Cake, you'll need the following ingredients:

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free all-purpose flour 1 ¾ cups
Hershey's cocoa powder ¾ cup
Granulated sugar 2 cups
Baking powder 1 ½ tsp
Baking soda 1 ½ tsp
Salt 1 tsp
Eggs 2 large
Milk (or dairy-free alternative) 1 cup
Vegetable oil ½ cup
Vanilla extract 2 tsp
Boiling water 1 cup

Step-by-Step Instructions

Follow these simple steps to create your Hershey's Gluten-Free Chocolate Cake:

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round baking pans or one 9x13-inch rectangular pan.
  2. Mix dry ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the gluten-free flour, cocoa powder, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Whisk until well combined.
  3. Add wet ingredients: Add the eggs, milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract to the dry ingredients. Mix on medium speed until the batter is smooth.
  4. Incorporate boiling water: Carefully stir in the boiling water. The batter will be thin, but this is normal and ensures a moist cake.
  5. Bake: Pour the batter into the prepared pans and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. Cool and frost: Allow the cake to cool completely before frosting with your favorite gluten-free frosting.

Tips for the Perfect Gluten-Free Chocolate Cake

Here are some expert tips to ensure your Hershey's Gluten-Free Chocolate Cake turns out perfectly every time:

  • Use a high-quality gluten-free flour blend: Not all gluten-free flours are created equal. Look for a blend that includes xanthan gum, which helps mimic the texture of gluten.
  • Don't overmix the batter: Overmixing can lead to a dense cake. Mix just until the ingredients are combined.
  • Let the cake cool completely: Frosting a warm cake can cause it to melt and slide off. Patience is key!
  • Experiment with toppings: Try adding fresh berries, whipped cream, or a drizzle of chocolate ganache for an extra special touch.

FAQs About Hershey's Gluten-Free Chocolate Cake

Is Hershey's cocoa powder gluten-free?

Yes, Hershey's cocoa powder is naturally gluten-free. However, always check the label to ensure there are no cross-contamination risks.

Can I make this cake dairy-free?

Absolutely! Simply substitute the milk with a dairy-free alternative like almond milk, oat milk, or soy milk.

How should I store the cake?

Store the cake in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 days, or in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. You can also freeze it for up to 3 months.

Can I use this recipe for cupcakes?

Yes, this recipe works well for cupcakes. Simply adjust the baking time to 18-22 minutes.
