What is Gluten and Why Go Gluten-Free?

Gluten is a group of proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale. For most people, gluten is harmless, but for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to serious health issues. Celiac disease is an autoimmune disorder where the ingestion of gluten leads to damage in the small intestine. Gluten sensitivity, on the other hand, can cause symptoms like bloating, diarrhea, and fatigue without the intestinal damage seen in celiac disease.

Going gluten-free involves eliminating all foods that contain gluten. This can be challenging, especially when dining out, as gluten is a common ingredient in many dishes. However, restaurants like Lenwich have made it easier by offering gluten-free options that are both delicious and safe for those with gluten-related disorders.

How Lenwich Ensures Gluten-Free Safety

For individuals with celiac disease or severe gluten intolerance, cross-contamination is a significant concern. Lenwich takes this issue seriously and has implemented several measures to ensure the safety of their gluten-free offerings. These measures include:

  • Using separate preparation areas for gluten-free items.
  • Training staff on the importance of avoiding cross-contamination.
  • Clearly labeling gluten-free options on the menu.
  • Regularly auditing their kitchen practices to maintain high standards.

These precautions help ensure that gluten-free diners can enjoy their meals with peace of mind, knowing that their dietary needs are being respected and prioritized.
