Gluten-Free Peach Bread: A Delicious and Healthy Treat

Gluten-free baking has become increasingly popular, and for good reason. Whether you're managing celiac disease, gluten sensitivity, or simply looking to reduce gluten in your diet, gluten-free recipes can be both delicious and nutritious. One such recipe that stands out is gluten-free peach bread. This moist, flavorful bread is perfect for breakfast, a snack, or even dessert. In this article, we'll explore the benefits of gluten-free baking, the nutritional value of peaches, and provide a step-by-step guide to making your own gluten-free peach bread.

Why Choose Gluten-Free?

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. For individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, consuming gluten can lead to a range of symptoms, from digestive issues to more severe health problems. Even for those without gluten-related conditions, reducing gluten intake can sometimes lead to improved digestion and overall well-being. Gluten-free baking allows you to enjoy your favorite treats without the negative effects of gluten.

The Nutritional Benefits of Peaches

Peaches are not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. They are a great source of vitamins A and C, which are essential for maintaining healthy skin and a strong immune system. Peaches also contain antioxidants, which help protect your body from damage caused by free radicals. Additionally, they are low in calories and high in fiber, making them a great addition to any diet.

How to Make Gluten-Free Peach Bread

Making gluten-free peach bread is easier than you might think. The key is to use the right combination of gluten-free flours to achieve the perfect texture. Here's a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups gluten-free all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • ½ cup coconut oil, melted
  • ¾ cup coconut sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup diced peaches (fresh or canned)
  • ½ cup unsweetened almond milk

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x5 inch loaf pan.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the gluten-free flour, almond flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the melted coconut oil, coconut sugar, eggs, and vanilla extract until well combined.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined.
  5. Fold in the diced peaches and almond milk, being careful not to overmix.
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula.
  7. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow the bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

FAQs About Gluten-Free Peach Bread

Can I use frozen peaches instead of fresh?

Yes, you can use frozen peaches. Just make sure to thaw and drain them well before adding them to the batter to avoid excess moisture.

Can I substitute almond flour with another type of flour?

Yes, you can substitute almond flour with other gluten-free flours like oat flour or coconut flour. However, keep in mind that this may alter the texture and flavor of the bread.

How should I store gluten-free peach bread?

Store the bread in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days, or in the refrigerator for up to a week. You can also freeze it for up to 3 months.

Is this recipe suitable for vegans?

This recipe contains eggs, so it is not vegan. However, you can try substituting the eggs with flax eggs or another vegan egg replacement.

With its moist texture, rich flavor, and nutritional benefits, gluten-free peach bread is perfect for any occasion. So why not give it a try? Your taste buds—and your body—will thank you!
