The Science Behind Gluten-Free Baking

Creating a gluten-free donut that rivals its gluten-containing counterpart is no easy feat. Gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye, is responsible for the elasticity and chewiness in traditional baked goods. When removed, bakers must find alternative ingredients to replicate these qualities. Stan's Gluten-Free Donuts achieve this by using a blend of gluten-free flours, such as almond flour, rice flour, and tapioca starch, along with xanthan gum to mimic the texture of gluten.

The process of making gluten-free donuts also requires careful attention to detail. Cross-contamination is a significant concern for those with celiac disease or severe gluten sensitivity. Stan's ensures that their donuts are made in a dedicated gluten-free facility, eliminating the risk of cross-contamination and providing peace of mind for their customers.
