How Does Amy's Gluten-Free Mac and Cheese Compare to Traditional Mac and Cheese?

When it comes to taste and texture, Amy's Gluten-Free Mac and Cheese holds its own against traditional versions. The rice pasta used in this product has a slightly different texture compared to wheat-based pasta, but it is still tender and satisfying. One of the key differences between Amy's Gluten-Free Mac and Cheese and traditional versions is the absence of gluten. For those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity, this is a significant advantage. Traditional mac and cheese often contains wheat flour as a thickening agent in the cheese sauce, which can cause digestive issues for those with gluten intolerance. Amy's version uses alternative thickeners, ensuring that the product is safe for those who need to avoid gluten.
