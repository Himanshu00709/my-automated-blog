What Makes Stouffer's Gluten-Free Lasagna Special?

Stouffer's Gluten-Free Lasagna is crafted with the same care and attention to detail as their traditional lasagna. The key difference lies in the ingredients. Instead of using wheat-based pasta, Stouffer's uses gluten-free pasta made from rice and corn. This ensures that the dish is safe for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. The lasagna is layered with a rich tomato sauce, creamy ricotta cheese, and a blend of mozzarella and Parmesan cheeses, creating a harmonious blend of flavors that will satisfy even the most discerning palate.

One of the standout features of Stouffer's Gluten-Free Lasagna is its convenience. It comes frozen and ready to bake, making it an excellent option for busy weeknights or when you need a quick meal without compromising on taste. The lasagna is also free from artificial flavors and preservatives, ensuring that you're getting a wholesome meal that's as close to homemade as possible.

Is Stouffer's Gluten-Free Lasagna safe for people with celiac disease?

Yes, Stouffer's Gluten-Free Lasagna is made with gluten-free ingredients and is safe for individuals with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. However, it's always a good idea to check the packaging for any potential cross-contamination warnings.

How do I prepare Stouffer's Gluten-Free Lasagna?

Stouffer's Gluten-Free Lasagna is easy to prepare. Simply remove the lasagna from its packaging, place it in a preheated oven, and bake according to the instructions on the box. It typically takes about 60-70 minutes to cook thoroughly.

Can I freeze Stouffer's Gluten-Free Lasagna?

Stouffer's Gluten-Free Lasagna comes frozen, so it's already ready for your freezer. If you have leftovers after baking, you can freeze them in an airtight container for up to three months.

Does Stouffer's Gluten-Free Lasagna contain dairy?

Yes, Stouffer's Gluten-Free Lasagna contains dairy in the form of ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan cheeses. If you're lactose intolerant or avoiding dairy, this may not be the best option for you.
