The History of Pizzelles

Pizzelles are believed to have originated in the Abruzzo region of Italy, where they were traditionally made to celebrate the "Festival of the Snakes" in the village of Cocullo. Over time, these cookies became a symbol of celebration and were often made for weddings, holidays, and other special occasions. The name "pizzelle" comes from the Italian word "pizze," meaning round and flat, which perfectly describes their shape.

Traditionally, pizzelles are made using a pizzelle iron, which imprints intricate designs onto the cookies. The batter is typically made from flour, sugar, eggs, butter, and flavorings such as anise or vanilla. However, the key ingredient—wheat flour—poses a problem for those who need to avoid gluten.
