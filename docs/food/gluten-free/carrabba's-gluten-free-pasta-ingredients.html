For those with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease, finding delicious and safe dining options can be a challenge. Carrabba's Italian Grill has stepped up to the plate by offering a gluten-free pasta option that doesn’t compromise on taste or quality. In this article, we’ll take a deep dive into the ingredients used in Carrabba's gluten-free pasta, ensuring you know exactly what you’re eating and why it’s a great choice for a gluten-free diet.

What Makes Carrabba's Gluten-Free Pasta Special?

Carrabba's gluten-free pasta is crafted with care, ensuring that it meets the dietary needs of those avoiding gluten while still delivering the authentic Italian flavors the restaurant is known for. The pasta is made from a blend of gluten-free grains and starches, which provide a texture and taste that closely resembles traditional wheat-based pasta. Let’s break down the key ingredients:

Primary Ingredients

Ingredient Purpose
Corn Flour Provides a smooth texture and mild flavor, serving as the base for the pasta.
Rice Flour Adds lightness and helps mimic the chewiness of traditional pasta.
Potato Starch Enhances the pasta's elasticity, making it less likely to break apart during cooking.
Tapioca Starch Contributes to the pasta's softness and helps bind the ingredients together.
Xanthan Gum Acts as a stabilizer and thickener, ensuring the pasta holds its shape and texture.

How Carrabba's Ensures Gluten-Free Safety

Carrabba's takes gluten-free dining seriously. The restaurant has implemented strict protocols to prevent cross-contamination, ensuring that their gluten-free pasta is safe for those with celiac disease or severe gluten sensitivities. Here’s how they do it:

  • Dedicated Cooking Equipment: Gluten-free pasta is cooked in separate pots and utensils to avoid contact with gluten-containing foods.
  • Separate Preparation Areas: Ingredients for gluten-free dishes are stored and prepared in designated areas to minimize the risk of cross-contamination.
  • Staff Training: Employees are trained on the importance of gluten-free protocols and how to handle gluten-free orders safely.
