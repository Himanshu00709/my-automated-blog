Gluten-Free Orange Cranberry Bread: A Delicious and Healthy Treat

Gluten-free baking has become increasingly popular, not only among those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity but also among health-conscious individuals looking to reduce their gluten intake. One of the most delightful gluten-free recipes to try is the Gluten-Free Orange Cranberry Bread. This bread combines the tangy sweetness of oranges with the tartness of cranberries, creating a perfect balance of flavors. Whether you're new to gluten-free baking or a seasoned pro, this recipe is sure to impress.

Why Choose Gluten-Free Orange Cranberry Bread?

Gluten-free Orange Cranberry Bread is not only delicious but also packed with nutrients. Oranges are rich in vitamin C, which boosts the immune system, while cranberries are known for their antioxidant properties. Additionally, using gluten-free flour ensures that this bread is suitable for those with gluten intolerance or celiac disease. The combination of these ingredients makes for a wholesome and satisfying treat that can be enjoyed any time of the day.

Ingredients You'll Need

Before diving into the recipe, let's take a look at the ingredients required to make this delightful bread. Most of these ingredients are easily available in your local grocery store or health food shop.

Ingredient Quantity
Gluten-free all-purpose flour 2 cups
Almond flour 1/2 cup
Baking powder 1 1/2 tsp
Baking soda 1/2 tsp
Salt 1/4 tsp
Eggs 2 large
Orange zest 1 tbsp
Fresh orange juice 1/2 cup
Honey or maple syrup 1/2 cup
Unsweetened applesauce 1/2 cup
Dried cranberries 1/2 cup
Chopped walnuts (optional) 1/4 cup

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that you have all your ingredients ready, let's move on to the step-by-step instructions to make this delicious Gluten-Free Orange Cranberry Bread.

Step 1: Preheat and Prepare

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x5 inch loaf pan with a little oil or line it with parchment paper to prevent the bread from sticking.

Step 2: Mix Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the gluten-free all-purpose flour, almond flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Ensure that all the dry ingredients are well combined.

Step 3: Combine Wet Ingredients

In a separate bowl, beat the eggs lightly. Add the orange zest, fresh orange juice, honey (or maple syrup), and unsweetened applesauce. Mix until all the wet ingredients are well incorporated.

Step 4: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring gently until just combined. Be careful not to overmix, as this can result in a dense bread.

Step 5: Add Cranberries and Nuts

Fold in the dried cranberries and chopped walnuts (if using) into the batter. This will add a delightful texture and flavor to your bread.

Step 6: Bake

Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Bake in the preheated oven for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Step 7: Cool and Serve

Allow the bread to cool in the pan for about 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely. Once cooled, slice and serve. Enjoy your Gluten-Free Orange Cranberry Bread with a cup of tea or coffee!

FAQs

Can I use fresh cranberries instead of dried?

Yes, you can use fresh cranberries, but keep in mind that they are more tart than dried cranberries. You may want to adjust the sweetness of the bread by adding a bit more honey or maple syrup if using fresh cranberries.

Can I substitute almond flour with another type of flour?

Almond flour adds moisture and a nutty flavor to the bread, but if you have a nut allergy, you can substitute it with another gluten-free flour like coconut flour or oat flour. Keep in mind that the texture and flavor may vary slightly.

How should I store the bread?

Store the bread in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days. For longer storage, you can wrap it tightly in plastic wrap and freeze it for up to 2 months. Thaw at room temperature before serving.

Can I make this bread vegan?

Yes, you can make this bread vegan by substituting the eggs with flax eggs (1 tbsp ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tbsp water per egg) and using maple syrup instead of honey. The texture may be slightly different, but it will still be delicious.
